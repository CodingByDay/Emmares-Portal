Turning the last page of the yearly calendar usually brings a sense of "out with the old, in with the new."

This year, though, there's a lot of old coming in with the new. Donald Trump still faces an impeachment trial. The U.S. and China continue inching toward a trade-war truce. Kim Jong Un issues another threat to unleash a "new strategic weapon." And Hong Kong's streets are still besieged by violent protests and vandalism. If you've been busy trying to replace your old with a batch of new, here are some stories you may have missed.

The Great Escape

In a performance worthy of Harry Houdini, former Nissan chairman Carlos Ghosn disappeared from Japan while awaiting trial and surfaced in Lebanon. While the machinations remain a mystery, Ghosn likely will use his freedom to tell tales and settle scores concerning his 2018 arrest.

Rich Get Richer

If you were a billionaire one year ago, you've probably got even more money now. The world's 500 richest people added $1.2 trillion in wealth last year, with Jeff Bezos maintaining his No. 1 spot in Bloomberg's ranking. A social media star and auto-junkyard king also had good years.

The Street Climbs the Wall

China opens its $45 trillion financial industry to foreign-owned firms this year, and the biggest names in insurance, asset management and investment banking are going in. The firms see billions of dollars in potential profits, and the country sees a cushion for its slowing economy.

9,000,000% Spike

If Old MacDonald had a server farm, he wouldn't have needed all those animals. The best-performing asset of the past decade is Bitcoin, which has posted gains of more than 9,000,000% and may experience even more volatility as a May deadline approaches for miners.

"Fire and Ice"

That's one tough bull. Markets squashed by the financial crisis rebounded to near-record highs, even after political upheavals, the euro-area crisis and the oil-price crash. The "fire and ice" decade provides clues to what lies ahead. And financial institutions are making their own prognostications.

China's Tech Troubles

China's tech titans once seemed on a path of unstoppable growth, but now they're being sidetracked by troubles foreign and domestic. That may trigger a reboot of the global supply chain and prompt Chinese companies to become more self-reliant.

Pack Your Bags

Tired of the same old vacation spots? Here are 24 suggested destinations around the world for the coming year, with activities ranging from cliff diving to elephant watching to camping to horseback riding. Don't forget to send a postcard.

Endangered Species

The global banking industry is on track for the biggest round of job cuts in four years, with Deutsche Bank topping the list so far. The outlook is just as gloomy for hedge fund managers, with the market likely seeing more closures than launches for a fifth straight year.

And the Oscar Goes To...

A company built for TV now produces more movies than any other studio, and Netflix's ambitions include putting a gold statuette on its mantel. The streaming service released about 60 English-language films last year, and "The Irishman" and "Marriage Story" are viewed as Oscar contenders.
