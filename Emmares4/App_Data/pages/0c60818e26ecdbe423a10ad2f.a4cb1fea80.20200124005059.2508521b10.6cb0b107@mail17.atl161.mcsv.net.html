Got my finger on the pulse

 

A new Apple patent application was published today for a “computer system with finger devices,” which is an interesting variation on wearables and potentially important for AR/VR.
 

The patent contemplates Band Aid and fingernail-sized devices. Sensors in the device would detect electrical and optical signals, allowing for the manipulation of objects in virtual or mixed reality.

The fingers would then receive “associated haptic output.” In other words, this tech would bring a potentially realistic sense of touch to virtual interfaces and experiences: VR volleyball anyone?

One More Thing
Source: Marc Jacobs / Stray Rats

Saturday marks the beginning of the Lunar New Year and the dawn of the Year of the Rat. 

 

Luxury brands looking to haul in some of the $150B in Chinese consumer holiday spending have begun to sell annual zodiac-themed collections, which raises the question: can rats be luxury?

 

Fashion houses are making valiant attempts to make the rodent more glamorous in their campaigns. For one, Burberry created “Ratberry,” a cartoon rat who dons a pearl necklace and is featured in a special online game as well as WeChat stickers, while Marc Jacobs took a more true-to-life approach by inviting live rats to the photo shoots.
