Last month, Benjamin Moore revealed its 2020 Color of the Year—one that sparked a bit of surprise among the design world. First Light is, essentially, that hue that has come to be known as "Millennial Pink," a soft, rosy blush. But the truth is, far before the hubbub around First Light, and even before the term Millennial Pink was coined, there was another color that designers had quietly been touting for years for its ability to cast the perfect rosy glow: Tissue Pink.

 

So, when I moved into my own apartment, you can bet the first thing I did was... paint 👏🏼 it 👏🏼 pink 👏🏼

WHAT I DID

STEP 1: Tape your edges 

Since my wall has molding at the top and bottom, I decided to just paint the space between, so I taped off the edges (you'll also want to remove switch-plates if they fall within the taped off area). 

STEP 2: Hang a dropcloth 
For extra security, spread a tarp or dropcloth under the wall you're painting to catch any drips. Then, you're ready to paint!
