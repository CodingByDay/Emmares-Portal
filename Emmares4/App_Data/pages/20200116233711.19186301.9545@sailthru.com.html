Donald Trump details 11 tactics of his negotiating style in the 1987 book "Trump: The Art of the Deal."

"Think Big" comes first, and "Deliver the Goods" a bit later. Trump argues he achieved both with the "big, beautiful monster" phase-one trade deal with China. Expert assessments aren't unanimous, and China cushioned the impact of tariffs by trading more with everyone else. But the U.S. agriculture and energy industries could benefit.

Trump also says in the book that he likes to "Fight Back." That was evident as the U.S. House of Representatives delivered articles of impeachment to the U.S. Senate, where a trial will be held. Trump tweeted that the process was "another Con Job by the Do Nothing Democrats."

If you were busy negotiating your way through the intricacies of the 86-page trade deal or the much shorter U.S. House resolution, here are some stories you may have missed.

Rise of the Machines

The list of things made obsolete by automation has another entry: the bonus culture on Wall Street. Technology's takeover of finance is affecting compensation as traders increasingly see themselves being replaced by software, robots and programmers.

Pricey Places

If you want to live in one of Asia's financial hubs, it's going to cost you. A new ranking of the world's most expensive cities puts Hong Kong at the top, even after violent protests pushed the economy into recession. Shanghai and Tokyo are close behind.

Twitter's Enforcer

Jack Dorsey may be the public face of Twitter, but the person with power to bounce you off the platform is its top lawyer, Vijaya Gadde. Her teams write and enforce rules for the social media giant, and they're frequently tested by the prolific @realDonaldTrump.

Inside the Escape From Tokyo

Carlos Ghosn's escape from 24-hour surveillance in Japan took months of planning by a team of security operatives, led by a veteran of the U.S. Army's Special Forces. But while he may have dodged the lawman in Tokyo, he's still under investigation in France. This is how an elite extraction team smuggled him out.

The Burning Bush

The scorching of Australia will have ramifications beyond losses of life and property. The agriculture industry is especially vulnerable, with analysts saying dairy, meat, wool and honey production will likely suffer the most.

Not-So-Golden Years

Government efforts to improve conditions for Japan's working women aren't doing enough to keep them out of poverty in retirement. Here's why half the nation's elderly single women may spend their golden years in financial ruin.

Election Observers

It's going to be a long year for election campaigning, and we're not just talking about the U.S. Emerging markets such as Russia, Brazil and South Korea are holding votes, and investors are keeping close watch for any turbulence in the aftermath of Taiwan's contest.

Bachelor on the Moon

Elton John warned, "It's lonely out in space," and Yusaku Maezawa is heeding those words. The Japanese billionaire is paying SpaceX to fly him to the moon, and now he's seeking a female companion for what could be the most expensive first date ever.
