One of the best-known American songs of the Great Depression was "Brother, Can You Spare a Dime?" The narrator asks why people who built railroads and skyscrapers, and fought in wars, suddenly were unemployed and seeking food handouts.

We're not at that stage yet, but governments and central banks are trying to spare us from a coronavirus-induced depression. They're unleashing a fire hose of stimulus measures that eventually could surpass $2 trillion — an amount exceeding the gross domestic products of Brazil, Russia or South Korea.

You'll notice there's one glaring holdout: China. The country that began as the epicenter of the pandemic is taking a more gradual approach even as projections for economic growth go lower.

If you've been busy pondering how to benefit from a stimulus package while you're still locked indoors, here are some stories you may have missed.

What Virus?

It's either a medical miracle, self-denial or disinformation. North Korea says it has no coronavirus cases even though it's sandwiched between infection-laden China and South Korea. International observers believe otherwise.

To the Jitney!

Wealthy New Yorkers are heading to the Hamptons to ride out the national emergency, and it seems they can't live without their beans and cash. The population in parts of the vacation retreat is reaching summertime levels, and that's straining grocery stores and banks.

Looked Good on Paper

The current pandemic isn't surprising to 50 national security experts who met in Washington last year to role-play the scenario. What is surprising is the U.S. response: Few predicted the Trump administration would play down the threat initially.

A.I. for Sale

Tech industry whales keep swallowing artificially intelligent guppies, and that's triggering alarms in Washington about a lack of competition. The number of deals to acquire A.I. startups grew by five times over a five-year period, with Apple buying the most.

B-School Brands

A top-shelf MBA may not always be the best choice. A Bloomberg Businessweek survey of recruiters shows lesser-known schools are moving toward the head of the class. We also explore the value of ESG experience and the case for one-year programs.

Keeping It Hush-Hush

Did you hear about the private equity firm that paid a ransom after cybercriminals hacked into one of their companies? Didn't think so. That's because PE firms are less likely to report those attacks — they don't want to take the reputation hit.

The Street Moves Home

The new normal in finance soon may be "Wall Street From Home." Some of the emergency measures rolled out by the industry to keep employees safe during the pandemic could become lasting practices. The NYSE is going all-electronic starting Monday.

White Lightning

We're all under some form of lockdown for the near future, so now's as good a time as any to transport yourself to backwoods America with a glass of moonshine. The notorious corn liquor has gone legit, and we recommend brands from Brooklyn to California.
