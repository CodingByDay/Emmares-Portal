The forgotten vaccine: Sanofi (SNY) and GlaxoSmithKline (GSK) announced a successful Phase 2 clinical trial of their joint COVID-19 vaccine. The vaccine will begin a final Phase 3 trial in a few weeks that will be international in scope. While success will come way too late to affect vaccinations in the U.S., it could be helpful in vaccination programs in Africa, Asia and Latin America, where many countries are still in their early stages.

Time Warner, Discovery to merge into a content titan. AT&T (T) today announced a deal that's certain to transform the media landscape. The telecom company plans to unwind its Time Warner division, including HBO, CNN, TNT and Warner Bros. properties, which would merge with Discovery (DISCA), whose channels include its namesake Discovery Channel, TLC and Animal Planet. AT&T says it expects to receive $43 billion in the transaction, which would create an entity that the Financial Times says could be valued as high as $150 billion and is projected to generate $52 billion in 2023 revenues. Early reactions from the analyst community show little shock given years of rumors that AT&T was looking to unload its Time Warner assets. "With intensifying pressures in the traditional pay-TV ecosystem through the pandemic, the potential deal is only mildly surprising as both parties scale up their nascent streaming offerings' head-to-head competition with Netflix, Hulu, Amazon Prime Video, Disney+, and others," says CFRA analyst Tuna Amobi. "The deal should squeeze out very meaningful synergies but could face some challenging regulatory odds." The deal, if approved by regulators, would mark the latest in what has been a string of transformative mergers-and-acquisitions activity over the past few months.
