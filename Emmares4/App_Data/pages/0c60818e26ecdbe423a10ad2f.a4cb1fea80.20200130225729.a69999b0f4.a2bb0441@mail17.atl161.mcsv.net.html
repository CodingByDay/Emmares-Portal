THIS WEEK IN DATA

  • 450 unicorns: Healthcare company Alto Pharmacy, point-of-sale startup Pine Labs, and smart security camera company Verkada raised funding at $1B+ valuations to become the latest unicorns. There are now 450 unicorns in the world. Alto Pharmacy was included in CB Insights’ 2019 Future Unicorn list made in collaboration with the New York Times. See all the other predictions here.
  • $140M: Warren Buffett is selling the newspapers owned by Berkshire Hathaway for $140M. The renowned investor is reported to have been surprised by the ongoing decline in demand for the publications. From not investing in bargains to the risks of debt, we highlight 24 lessons from Warren Buffett’s annual letters to shareholders here. 
     
  • 64 emojis: Unicode announced 64 new emojis to be released this year. New entrants include a dodo, an array of ninjas, and an anatomical heart. 
     
  • 341 GB: Google announced a new AI-powered chatbot, which it says has been trained on 341 GB of data to help it respond to questions in more natural and engaging ways. Chatbots are going through a resurgence after interest in the tech waned. Just this week, Samsung led a $20M investment into chatbot startup Directly. We dig into lessons from the failed chatbot revolution, and why the tech is back on the rise, in this report.
  • $186,667 per second: The average cost of a 30-second ad for this weekend’s Super Bowl was reported to be $5.6M, or $186,667 per second of airtime. 100M viewers in the US are expected to tune in. 
     
  • $1B+: Sweden-based fintech unicorn Klarna raised $200M, bringing its total disclosed funding to over $1B. Take a look at all the emerging fintech funding trends in our latest quarterly fintech report. 
     

  • 35%: The UK announced that it would allow China-based tech co Huawei to help build its 5G network — a tech that promises to vastly improve internet connectivity. The move comes despite warnings from the US government that Huawei poses a security risk. The UK has tried to mitigate such concerns by setting restrictions on the company’s participation, including limiting the use of its equipment to 35% of the country’s network. Learn why 5G is attracting attention in our explainer. 

  • 78.7 years: Life expectancy in the US rose to 78.7 years, according to the Centers for Disease Control and Prevention (CDC) — the first rise in 4 years. Emerging tech may help push up life expectancy even further. See how in our future of aging report.  
     

  • 4,309 feet: A 4,309-foot long tunnel (the equivalent of 16 Manhattan blocks) linking Mexico to the US has been discovered. 
     

  • 18.5 miles: A new image of the sun has been released. It is the highest resolution photo taken of the star to date. Produced by the Daniel K. Inouye Solar Telescope in Hawaii, the image shows features as small as 18.5 miles across — revealing a complex surface, reminiscent of a close up view of skin, made of Texas-sized cell-like structures.
