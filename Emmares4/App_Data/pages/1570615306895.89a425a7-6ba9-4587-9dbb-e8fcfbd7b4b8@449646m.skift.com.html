New Skift Research — UK Traveler Profile and Key Statistics: The Brexit Effect

The UK is not only one of the largest outbound source markets in the world, the country is also at a pivotal point in its history due to the 2016 Brexit vote, which will undoubtedly have an impact on future travel behavior.

In the first part of this report, Skift Research provides a detailed profile of the UK traveler, with both volume and value data breakdowns for age groups, trip purpose, and travel destinations. 

The second part focuses on Brexit, which is changing the UK's place in the world and its residents’ willingness to spend on international vacations. After a brief explanation of the ever-changing situation, we analyze the impact on the travel industry by highlighting three scenarios, all still possible at present: 1) the UK remains in a single market, 2) the UK leaves the EU with Theresa May’s deal or a similar deal, or 3) the UK leaves the EU without a deal and starts trading under WTO terms. Skift Research proprietary data provides an insight into the possible impact on UK inbound, domestic, and outbound travel for each scenario. 

What you'll learn from this report:

  • The number of UK residents who travel and how much the average household spends on travel.
  • Historical time series data on travel volume and value from 2006-2018
  • Insight into trips and spending broken down by age, trip purpose, and destinations.
  • Key facts about the Brexit process, and how it is forecast to impact the travel industry
  • 2019-2024 forecast data based on three Brexit scenarios: the UK Remains in a single market, the UK leaves the EU with a deal, or the UK leaves the EU without a deal.
