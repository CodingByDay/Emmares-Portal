This week brought some welcome news about the job market: Initial unemployment claims have dropped 13% so far in April versus March, to reach their lowest level since the beginning of the pandemic, indicating that the economy is picking up. Claims are still nearly double what they were pre-pandemic, and they had stopped falling last autumn as an infection surge that lasted through January forced consumers and businesses to cut back on normal activities. Now that the surge has dissipated, claims have resumed their downward march, indicating that the economy continues to heal from the wounds it suffered in 2020.

Investors still love tried and true funds. In a year that has so far been dominated by big, flashy headlines such as the massive short squeezes in GameStop (GME) and other “meme stocks,” as well as the continued rise of cryptocurrencies, you might be relieved to know that “plain ol’ reliable” is doing just fine, too. Eric Balchunas, senior ETF analyst for Bloomberg Intelligence, notes in a Friday tweet that “Vanguard took in $121 [billion] in Q1, which is $2b/day pace … [and] puts them on track to beat their [annual] record set in 2017. Also shows cheap beta still king despite all the focus and appetite for things that shine.” He also notes that exchange-traded funds and collective investment trusts (CITs) are Vanguard’s biggest areas of growth, with the former taking in $1.5 billion every day. At its current pace of gaining market share, Vanguard is primed to become the biggest ETF issuer in four years, Balchunas says. Among some of Vanguard’s most popular ETFs?
