Methane on Mars

An unknown source may be producing methane close to NASA’s Curiosity rover, with potential implications for life on Mars. Curiosity’s instruments have recorded methane spikes where methane levels have risen to 10 parts per billion, compared to a background level of about 0.41 parts per billion. By modelling the results and taking into account factors such as the local wind speed and direction at the time a spike was detected, researchers have located a possible source a few dozen kilometres away from Curiosity inside the 150-kilometre-wide Gale crater. If correct, this would be the most accurate localisation of a methane source ever on Mars. It is possible the source is geological, connected perhaps to asteroids or comets hitting Mars, but the prospect of a biological origin remains a possibility. Read more

3D-printed bridge

The first ever 3D-printed steel bridge has opened in Amsterdam, the Netherlands. It was created by robotic arms using welding torches to deposit the structure of the bridge layer by layer, and is made of 4500 kilograms of stainless steel. The structure was transported to its location over the Oudezijds Achterburgwal canal in central Amsterdam last week and is now open to pedestrians and cyclists. More than a dozen sensors attached to the bridge after the printing was completed will monitor strain, movement, vibration and temperature across the structure as people pass over it and the weather changes. This data will be fed into a digital model of the bridge and used to determine when maintenance or modification is necessary.  Read more

Long read: The lowdown on stretching

Harriet Noble/Studio Pi
Flexibility, according to the prevailing wisdom, is something we should aim for - something that is both impressive and essential for a healthy body. But does science back this up? Should you stretch to become more flexible? And does it matter if you can’t touch your toes, let alone do the splits? In recent years answers have started to emerge. The surprising outcome is that, while stretching may well be good for us, it is for reasons that have nothing to do with being able to get your leg behind your head. Read more
