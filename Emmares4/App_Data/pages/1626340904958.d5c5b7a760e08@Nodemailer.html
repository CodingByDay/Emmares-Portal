MORE EVENTS ON THIS DAY
2006: Cofounders Jack Dorsey, Evan Williams, and Biz Stone publicly launched their online microblogging service Twitter, and within the following decade it had more than 300 million users. [Test your knowledge of tech companies.]

1997: Italian fashion designer Gianni Versace was murdered by serial killer Andrew Cunanan. [Read our list of infamous serial killers.]

1988: The thriller Die Hard was first released in U.S. theatres, launching a hugely popular series and helping establish Bruce Willis as an action star. [Take our quiz about movies.]

1922: Leon Max Lederman, who was a corecipient of the 1988 Nobel Prize for Physics for research on neutrinos, was born in New York City. [Take our quiz about the Nobel Prize.]

1978: American singer-songwriter Bob Dylan performed in England before 200,000 people. [Test your knowledge of songwriters.]

1912: American Jim Thorpe won a gold medal for the decathlon at the Olympics in Stockholm; he also captured a gold medal for the pentathlon. [Test your knowledge of the Olympics.]
