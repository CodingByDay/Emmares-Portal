  Mending mind and body: Emerging links between mental health, diabetes and heart disease
 
 

Date:  Tuesday, May 18, 2021

Time: 1 PM PDT | 4 PM EDT

 
This event has been produced on behalf of the sponsor who retains sole responsibility for content.
About this content.
 
 
 
 

A diagnosis of chronic disease is stressful, and anxiety and depression can heighten the dangers of chronic disease. This vicious cycle is especially pronounced for diabetes, a disease that demands knowledge and diligence for effective self-care. In this sponsored virtual salon, three leading experts in diabetes care will discuss the latest research linking mental health, diabetes and heart disease. They’ll also point the way toward effective interventions in the clinic and community that can replace the vicious cycle with a virtuous one that improves health and quality of life.
 

Please join us for a virtual discussion and Q&A with luminaries in the field. Speakers include:

  • Kenneth E. Freedland, PhD, Professor of Psychiatry and Psychology, Washington University School of Medicine in St. Louis
  • Lawrence Fisher, PhD, Professor of Family & Community Medicine, University of California, San Francisco
  • Paula M. Trief, PhD, Distinguished Service Professor of Psychiatry & Behavioral Sciences and Medicine, State University of New York Upstate Medical University
