Apple fulfilled the wish of countless Mac fans this week by announcing that it has redesigned the famously poor keyboard in the MacBook Pro laptop. But while the reengineered keyboard fixes a number of problems with the Pro's janky design—keys would fail, get stuck, or register two keystrokes when pressed once—the new hardware is available only on a single model of laptop: the new 16-inch MacBook Pro.

The smaller, often cheaper models of the MacBok Pro are still for sale, and they still come with the infamous butterfly-switch keyboard atop the chassis. Anyone who wants a baseline MacBook Pro with the new keyboard is going to have to wait for the updated design to trickle down into their model of choice. That could happen to most of the MacBooks next year, but it seems like the butterflies will stick around a while.

The new version of the MacBook Pro—new keyboard and all—goes on sale this week, starting at $2,399. WIRED's Julian Chokkattu has the details in his hands-on report.

Also this week, Motorola's Razr flip phone is back, now with a folding screen; the streaming website Quinn wants to be the Spotify for audio porn; and Best Buy turns out the lights.
