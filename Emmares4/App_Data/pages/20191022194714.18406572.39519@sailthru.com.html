Japan’s 126th emperor takes throne

Japan's Emperor Naruhito ascended to the Chrysanthemum Throne and officially proclaimed himself the country’s 126th emperor on Tuesday at the Imperial Palace in Tokyo. In front of dignitaries from 180 countries, Naruhito pledged to “always wish the happiness of the people and the peace of the world” and was presented with the nearly 2,700-year-old sacred sword and jewel that have been passed through generations of monarchs. The ancient ceremony completed his months-long coronation after he assumed the throne in May when his father, Akihito, abdicated, citing poor health.
