U.S., China talk tariff rollback

The U.S. and China agreed to roll back some tariffs on each others' goods in stages as any part of a “phase one” trade deal, Beijing officials announced Thursday. The development follows two weeks of “constructive discussions” among negotiators, who set a key condition that both countries must scrap an equal amount of levies simultaneously. President Trump and Chinese President Xi Jinping may not be able to sign a partial trade deal until December.

More: 

  • Senior White House Adviser Kellyanne Conway said Trump is “anxious” to sign the accord.

Highly quotable

“Brain death.” French President Emmanuel Macron said Trump’s neglect of U.S. allies is killing NATO, creating a “considerable risk that we will disappear geopolitically.”

“Biggest foreign policy blunder.” Ex-House of Commons speaker John Bercow said Brexit would be the biggest mistake since World War II and called for another referendum.

This is not normal

Haven no more. It's one of the most remote places on Earth, but tons of ocean plastic still washes up on Hawaii's Midway Atoll every year. Despite being an official wildlife refuge, the tiny, uninhabited island is littered with bird skeletons full of "brightly colored plastic protruding from their bellies."

The future is now

Next-level empathy. This car can detect how you're feeling via internal cameras and EKG sensors that read your emotions and make adjustments to temperature, lighting, sound, and seat vibrations for ultimate customization. 

What's good

Closing the gap. Australia’s top women footballers will earn the same as male players after the country’s governing soccer body and the players' union struck a deal to give members of both teams an equal share of revenue.
