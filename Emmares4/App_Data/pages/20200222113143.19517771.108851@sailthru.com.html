The Big Story

Every so often a company in Silicon Valley raises cash and pisses everyone off. This week it was a canned water company that raised $9 million. Few startups seem to encapsulate so many trends at once.

  • There is without a doubt a reckoning coming for direct-to-consumer brands. I doubt Liquid Deathis the clearest sign of that reckoning coming, but the writing is on the wall in the wake of Casper’s IPO. There are plenty of successes but it’s clear that those wins are causing some headstrong investors to get a bit cocky.
  • The more salient trend is just how big the list of startups trying to be clean-tech companies are. The feel-good tag makes dumb companies like this feel better while investors that know they should be embarrassed to stick this in their firm’s column are happy to bail themselves out. Liquid Death is hardly the most egregious example of misplaced self-classifications, and as you’ll read in the story, there is at least some level-headed thinking behind sustainability claims, but they’re still pushing it.

Our premium subscription business had another great week of content. My colleague Alex Wilhelm dove a bit deeper into the world of commission-free trading and how value is determined for the startups throwing their weight there.

What the $13B E-Trade deal says about Robinhood’s valuation

“…In 2019, E-Trade reported record revenue of $2.9 billion, net income of $955 million and 304,000 net new accounts. However, dialing into Q4 2019, we see a bit of a surprise: E-Trade’s revenue fell from both the sequentially-preceding quarter (Q3 2019) and the year-ago period.

Why? The company’s interest revenue dipped some (E-Trade makes money off an interest rate differential between what it collects and what it pays to the holders of certain interest-bearing assets). But more notable was the difference between the company’s Q4 2019 commissions revenue and what it collected previously. Down from $123 million in Q4 2018 and $122 million in Q3 2019, E-Trade brought in just $56 million in commissions revenue in its most recent quarter.
