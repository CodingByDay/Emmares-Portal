Sagas remain relevant 800 years after Icelanders first recorded their oral histories. Some modern versions closed significant chapters this week, with protagonists readying for new ones. Donald Trump was impeached by the U.S. House of Representatives, and now he awaits a trial by the Senate. Boris Johnson won a resounding election victory in the U.K., and now he awaits approval of his Brexit deal by Parliament. And both have trade deals requiring attention. If you've been busy honing your Icelandic to read the original Njals saga, here are some stories you may have missed this week. — Michael Tighe

Getting Double Vision

The outlook for Masayoshi Son's Vision Fund is getting fuzzier. Japanese bankers are less enthusiastic about SoftBank's debt-fueled ventures after WeWork's meltdown, and current and former employees describe a culture of harassment, compliance issues and sycophancy.

 

Targeting Tycoons

Chinese media is playing the blame game over Hong Kong's protests, and it's putting property tycoons in the winner's circle. Communist Party outlets call Hong Kong's housing crisis the genesis of the demonstrations, and they've suggested the government seize developers' idle lands.

 

Max Salaries Disappear

Expatriate pilots used to command $300,000-plus salaries from Chinese airlines because of their expertise with Boeing's 737 Max. That lucrative stream is drying up now that the company has grounded the plane.

 

Vanillanomics

It's used in ice cream, coffee, vodka and an airline's name, and its price has skyrocketed during the past five years. Yet the volatile marketplace for vanilla is feeding boom-and-bust cycles that keep one of the biggest producers of the beans stuck in poverty.

 

Rat Race

The Rat may wind up being even slower than the Pig, according to some of the world's most prominent China watchers. The economy suffered its worst slowdown in decades in 2019 — the Year of the Pig —and experts foresee an even worse performance in 2020 —the Year of the Rat.
 

A 300% Return

If you're looking for outsize returns on your stash of cash, don't bother with New York or London. Singapore's hedge funds are trouncing rivals in those financial capitals, and here are some reasons why.

 

Sacre Bleu!

The path from British rainmaker to winemaker is well-trod, but the fields now bear some award-winning grapes. England's sparkling wines are gaining international recognition, even beating the French at this year's "Oscars of Alcohol."

 

Waking From a Dream

Who wouldn't wish for a dream house in the country, with every detail painstakingly designed by a famous architect? Well, if you ever want to sell that house, be careful what you wish for.
