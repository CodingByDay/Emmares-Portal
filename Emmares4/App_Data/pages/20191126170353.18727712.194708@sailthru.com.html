On Monday, Bloomberg reported how global warming has made feeding humanity harder. On Tuesday, the United Nations rang a starker alarm, one more grim than a decade of grim updates on the consequences of placing short-term financial growth over species survival. Major nations led by most of the G20 have effectively ignored the warnings, the UN said, and instead accelerated fossil fuel use and exploration. Now, the world must cut pollution levels in half by 2030 in order to have a chance at arresting climate change. And the restructuring of economies needed to achieve that end would be only the start: By 2050, humanity must spend $42 trillion (that’s $42,000,000,000,000) to truly slow the quick-march toward catastrophe. —David E. Rovella
