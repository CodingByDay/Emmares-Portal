The Gunshot That Unleashed a Day of Mayhem

Police in Hong Kong are under scrutiny again after an officer grabbed one protester in a headlock and shot another in the abdomen at close range. Chaos erupted after video of the incident was widely circulated on social media, leading protesters to take over the city’s central business district during lunch hours. Police fired a round of tear gas into streets, sending workers sprinting to safety. Also on Monday, a man was doused in fluid and set on fire after what appears to be a confrontation during the protests. He is in critical condition in the hospital, according to police.

More: 

  • Hospital authorities confirmed the patient shot is in critical condition and undergoing an operation. 

  • Activists are calling on people to disrupt traffic during Tuesday’s morning commute.

  • The University of Hong Kong canceled all classes on Tuesday. 

$ignificant figures

13 years. That’s how long Evo Morales was the President of Bolivia before resigning, making him South America's longest-serving leader. Morales quit after election irregularities triggered weeks of violent protests and intervention from the armed forces. He has been granted asylum in Mexico.

700,000. About the number of people protected from deportation by DACA. The Supreme Court will hear arguments on Trump’s order to end the program on Tuesday.

$38.3 billion. An estimated half-billion shoppers spent that record amount during Alibaba’s Singles’ Day, the world’s largest shopping event.

What’s good

Age is just a number. Japanese multimedia artist Yayoi Kusama isn't slowing down at age 90. Her latest exhibition could have New Yorkers waiting up to 2 hours to spend a single minute in one of her famous infinity mirror rooms. Her work is displayed this month at the David Zwirner Gallery, in a show entitled “Every Day I Pray for Love.”
