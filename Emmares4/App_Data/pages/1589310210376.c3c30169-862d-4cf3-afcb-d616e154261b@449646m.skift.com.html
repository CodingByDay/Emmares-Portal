New Skift Research — Travel Winners and Losers During the 2008 Financial Crisis: Lessons for COVID-19

History doesn’t repeat but it often rhymes. COVID-19 has different root causes than the financial crisis of 2008, but there are still lessons from that last crisis that apply today. 

Our latest report takes a big picture look at changes in consumer behavior during the great recession and how sectors like hotels, airlines, and travel agents responded. We also draw on two case studies to highlight successful and unsuccessful crisis responses.

We distill 13 in-depth lessons for the travel industry that are relevant today. These include insights on a potential near-term shift towards domestic travel and a look at how the timing of the recovery could vary across segments like leisure versus business, and budget compared to luxury.

What You’ll Learn From This Report:

  • 13 in-depth lessons from the travel industry learned during the 2008 financial crisis that still apply to companies dealing with COVID-19 today
  • How U.S. consumers shifted behaviors during the great recession of 2008
  • How different parts of the travel industry performed during the crisis including the hotel, airline, and travel agent sectors
  • Winner Case Study: What Marriott did well to emerge from the last travel recession a stronger company
  • Loser Case Study: Why the crisis forced Extended Stay America to declare bankruptcy and restructure its business
