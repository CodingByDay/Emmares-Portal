It is not clear yet whether or when scientists will recommend lifting the suspension of the Johnson & Johnson (JNJ) vaccine in the U.S. Right now, they are hunting for data and trying to understand the reason for the rare blood clotting problem that a small number of recipients experience. Six women in the U.S., between the ages of 18 and 48, developed dangerous blood clots six to 13 days after getting the vaccine, out of 7 million recipients in the U.S. so far. The theory at the moment is that in a few people, the vaccine produces antibodies to platelet factor 4, which leads to the clots. Symptoms are severe headache, abdominal pain, leg pain, or shortness of breath.

The reason researchers were on the alert for this issue is reports of a similar rare problem with the AstraZeneca (AZN) vaccine in Europe. Both vaccines use a similar technology. There have been no reports of such problems with the Pfizer (PFE) and Moderna (MRNA) vaccines in the U.S., which work differently.

Vaccination rates have dipped only modestly in the U.S., so far. But if the suspension is not lifted quickly, it will focus attention on the age-old tussle between risks and benefits. Most drugs have rare side effects. Should the authorities allow people to weigh the risks and choose for themselves? While blood clots are a risk, so is dying of COVID-19. In Europe, the authorities are trying to walk the tightrope, recommending the AstraZeneca vaccine for older people with higher virus risk, and discouraging it for those under 30.

Wait for a chip dip if you’re thinking about investing in semiconductor makers. Taiwan Semiconductor (TSM) yesterday kept up a string of good news for semiconductor stocks with a Wall Street-beating earnings report in which it indicated the global chip shortage could continue into next year. However, BCA Research warns that, while semiconductor stocks “are a high-beta play on the global recovery” and poised to perform well in 2021, patience might pay off. BCA notes that the “SOX index” of chip stocks tends to closely track a number of gauges, such as the prices of gold and bitcoin, and that weakening momentum there could be problematic: “A relapse would herald a decline in semiconductor stocks,” BCA’s analysts say. They remain bullish in the longer term on the chip space, but advise investors to “stay on the sidelines in anticipation of a better entry point.” If you’re looking to take advantage of a dip in chips, consider these top semiconductor names.
