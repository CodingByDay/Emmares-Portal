"The Telephone Game" usually livens up parties, as someone whispers a message to their neighbor and hears how much it's changed when the last person in the chain repeats it. The "Impeachment Version" underway in Washington certainly is livening up the Democratic and Republican parties.

Donald Trump says his July 25 call with Ukraine's president was "perfect," but other U.S. officials on the line say they heard Trump solicit interference in next year's election. If you've been busy trying to decide which message is more accurate, here are some stories you might have missed. —Michael Tighe

Divided by Rumors

Fake news and rumors are heightening the tensions ripping Hong Kong apart. With protests in their sixth month and becoming increasingly violent, people are inundated with fictitious stories, online rumors and propaganda from both sides of the political divide.

 

Right in BMW's Backyard

Elon Musk is gigabusy. Soon after the first Teslas rolled out of his new factory in Shanghai, the billionaire said he would build another plant near Berlin. That decision was made after much wooing by several regions in Germany.

 

You Make How Much?

Apple and Microsoft did it, but Oracle won't. More Silicon Valley companies are publishing pay data to address concerns about the gap between men and women, but Larry Ellison says no. Here's why outside investors keep pressuring the software titan. 

 

In Flames

Tracts of rural Australia are on fire, and Prime Minister Scott Morrison's government refuses to discuss whether climate change is contributing. That puts him at odds with most Australians, including one who's suing his pension fund to find out whether its investments are green enough.

 

The Next Top Economy Is...

Replacing the world's factory floor may not be possible, but building a few satellite locations should be. Bloomberg Economics says no single nation can replicate China's success in transforming its economy, though a few countries can become "mini Chinas" if the trade war lingers.

 

Things Could Get Weird

Want to blow out 99 candles on your birthday cake? A Chinese genetics company says everyone could live that long with regular testing and specialized treatments. Its nimble founder says getting your entire genome sequenced will eventually be as common as getting vaccinated.

 

Surviving the Apocalypse

If you're heading to the North Pole next month, you may notice Svalbard, a Norwegian archipelago where the world's seed vault is. It also houses an archive of the world's open source software code. So in the event of an apocalypse, we can regrow corn and rebuild YouTube.

 

Inside a YouTube Empire

Maybe that Academy Awards musical chestnut should be remixed as "Hooray for Bollywood." It certainly strikes a chord at T-Series, the biggest record label in India and creator of the most-watched YouTube channel in the world.
