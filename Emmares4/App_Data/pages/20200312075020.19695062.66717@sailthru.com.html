Here's the latest news:

  • Confirmed cases have hit 125,326 globally, with 4,625 dead
  • Stock futures plummeted in the U.S., triggering circuit breakers. 
  • India suspended visas; Scandinavia is in a lockdown.

Our take on the latest developments

President Trump's announcement Wednesday night that the U.S. would ban travel from most of Europe for 30 days will cut the U.S. off from coronavirus outbreaks across the Atlantic. Unfortunately, it doesn't do anything to stop the outbreak inside America's own borders. 

Covid-19 is already in the U.S., with clusters in Seattle, New York, Boston and elsewhere helping add up to more than 1,200 cases (a figure that's likely far too low because of major testing bottlenecks). In fact, there are more confirmed cases in Washington state than there are in some of the countries facing Trump's flight bans. 

Never mind that such restrictions don't appear to be effective at containing infectious diseases once they've become a pandemic. What they do appear good at is fanning panic among investors, as evidenced by the worsening rout in global stock markets overnight. 

Disease experts in the U.S. government have announced they're moving toward a strategy of "mitigation." Because there's not enough coronavirus testing to track down cases, isolate them, and contain them, the U.S. is focusing on limiting the impact.

Like travel bans, those are blunt tools. Containment means identifying patients, finding their contacts, and cutting off a disease in a small part of the population before it has can grow. It's a scalpel. Mitigation is baseball bat. Instead of isolating the sick, you effectively isolate everyone. 

Those blunt tools come with enormous economic consequences -- as China has demonstrated and the U.S. is about to learn.--Drew Armstrong
