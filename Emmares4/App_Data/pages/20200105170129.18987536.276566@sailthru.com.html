Pre-Dawn Long Runs Helped This Pediatric Neurologist OTQ in Debut Marathon
 
When Duriel Hardy arrived at the start line of the California International Marathon (CIM) on December 8, he realized he had forgotten to practice something key: grabbing water bottles.

Before the race began, the organizers told the elite corral that they could have up to seven bottles on the course, staggered every 5K. While Hardy, a pediatric neurologist from Philadelphia who was debuting at the distance, had brought the right number of bottles, he was only able to grab one during the race.

"I kept missing them, and I was freaking out because I thought I wouldn't have enough fuel," Hardy, 31, told Runner's World. "It was my first marathon, and I didn't know what I was doing. Luckily, the other guys at CIM were super supportive and shared their bottles during the race."

In most marathons, participants are racing to beat one another. But at CIM, dozens of runners—Hardy included—were aiming to get an Olympic Trials-qualifying time (sub-2:19 for men) to secure a bib for the 2020 Olympic Marathon Trials happening this February in Atlanta. With this shared goal in mind, the men worked together to keep one another on pace and properly fueled all the way to the finish line.
