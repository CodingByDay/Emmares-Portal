Good afternoon and welcome to Fox News Today: The Coronavirus Crisis. 

Trump administration moves tax filing day to July 15, Mnuchin says

The Trump administration has decided to extend the deadline for filing taxes from April 15 to July 15 as the nation deals with the coronavirus outbreak, Treasury Secretary Steven Mnuchin said in a tweet on Friday.

The move comes as Trump announced new travel restrictions between the U.S. and Mexico and more restrictions involving travel to Canada as part of the ongoing effort to stem the coronavirus pandemic. The president was revealing details during Friday’s press briefing with the White House Coronavirus Task Force. 

California woke up Friday morning to the nation’s strictest coronavirus measures under Gov. Gavin Newsom’s statewide “stay-at-home” order. Here’s a look at what those restrictions are. 

Sen. Kelly Loeffler, R-Ga., one of four senators accused of selling off stocks worth millions of dollars in the days before coronavirus wreaked havoc on the market, responded to the allegations in an interview on "America's Newsroom." And NASA's plan to return to the moon in 2024 could be in jeopardy.

And there were several other developments:

New York state now accounts for 40 percent of coronavirus cases in the U.S, with New York City itself responsible for approximately 30 percent.

A longtime employee at NBC News died after Thursday after testing positive for the coronavirus, the company announced.

More men are dying from coronavirus than women, early data suggests.

China posthumously exonerated a doctor from Wuhan who had been reprimanded for giving early warnings about the coronavirus in late December — and died from it weeks later.

As theaters across the country close their doors due to government mandates prohibiting large gatherings, the North American movie box office has reached new, never-before-seen lows.
