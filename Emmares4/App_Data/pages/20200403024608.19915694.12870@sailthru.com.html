The more we crunch the numbers, the more we see our world getting crunched by the coronavirus pandemic.

The total number of known infections surpassed 1 million globally. The number of deaths surpassed 50,000.

Then there's the economic toll as people abide by stay-at-home orders and avoid restaurants, stores and movie theaters, triggering massive job cuts. The pandemic's cost could reach $4.1 trillion — equal to about 5% of global GDP — and cause the global economy to shrink almost 2% in the first half.

If you've been busy pounding on your calculator, moving beads on your abacus or updating your spreadsheets, here are some stories you may have missed.

Nursing Shortage

There are many qualified nurses ready to help U.S. hospitals deal with the coronavirus pandemic, but tighter immigration rules under the Trump administration are keeping most of them away. Agencies recruiting from the Philippines say they can't get visas processed.

Infection or Starvation?

India's 21-day lockdown to curb coronavirus infections is forcing hundreds of thousands of migrant workers to flee cities since there's no work. Many of the poor say the exodus is necessary to find food, but authorities worry the trips could wind up spreading the disease.

iWork From Home

Working from home isn't so easy when you work for Apple. Not only are the Apple Park offices way nicer than most employees' homes, but the company also is ruled by a culture of secrecy that's hard to replicate when employees are in close quarters with their families.

The Five Ps

Before we hit the panic button on the global economy, bulls and bears suggest studying five other "Ps" for clues about whether risk assets have hit a bottom. Here are five charts to help you make up your own mind.

Google Is Thirsty

All that Google searching and YouTube watching we're doing at home is sucking up billions of gallons of water. Google is building more data centers across the U.S. to handle demand, but critics say the complexes' need for cooling is parching surrounding areas.

Late Payments

Stay-at-home orders are costing millions of people their jobs, and that's causing many to default on personal loans. A wave of overdue loans is starting to form in China, and analysts say it's just a matter of time before it swamps the rest of the world.

The Screens Win

One lesson parents are learning while sharing tight quarters with their kids: You can't beat the screens. Time limits are going by the wayside now that children are schooling remotely and socially distancing from their friends, all while parents are trying to get some work done.

Coal-Powered Manatees

They're also called sea cows, and they love to graze on seagrass. The problem is that these endangered mammals search for food near utility-plant pipes in Florida, and that dependency may end up killing them — but for a surprising reason.
