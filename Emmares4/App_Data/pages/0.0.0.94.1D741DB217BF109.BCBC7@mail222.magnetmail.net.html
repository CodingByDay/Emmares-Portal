The number of COVID-19 cases globally appears to have peaked, helped by an apparent peak in India’s infection rate. Though still just below 400,000 daily confirmed cases, any decline would help India’s beleaguered health care system. Infections began surging in March, going from 15,000 a day to 400,000 a day at the end of April. Estimates show that only 2% of the population has been vaccinated so far, meaning that further surges are possible.

Lowering the infection rate in India will help the whole world. First, a large number of infections in India makes it easier for new viral variants to develop, which travelers could then spread to other countries. Second, India’s huge pharmaceutical industry accounts for 20% of world exports of generic drugs, and a large share of its vaccines. Because China suspended cargo flights to India amid the latest infection surge, pharmaceutical manufacturers in India, which rely on Chinese suppliers for raw materials, have had to cut back production of various drugs. They were also producing AstraZeneca’s (AZN) COVID-19 vaccine, many doses of which were supposed to go to African countries until the Indian government stopped all COVID vaccine exports. Finally, being a large country, India is an important destination for the world’s exports, including 2.3% of all U.S. exports.

Looking for a near-term focus on what stocks to buy? Most analyst insights have a longer-term tilt, with price targets at least a year out, and often far past that. But William Blair is among a few outfits that keeps shorter-term, tactical investors in mind, regularly updating its Consumer Near-Term Focus List: A list that’s "bottom-up in nature with no thematic top-down approach, with each analyst including his or her highest conviction ideas for stock appreciation over the next 60 days.” Currently on Blair’s list are Burlington Stores (BURL), National Vision Holdings (EYE), Six Flags (SIX), J&J Snack Foods (JJSF) and Dave & Buster’s (PLAY). Despite the supposed lack of a thematic approach, an investing theme (the economy reopening) is nonetheless apparent. Burlington “should be an important beneficiary of a reopening economy,” Six Flags is enjoying improving attendance trends, and J&J Snack Foods generates two-thirds of its sales “in away-from-home venues.” So, despite the run-up in a number of recovery plays, economic recovery themes remain expected drivers of additional short-term performance.
