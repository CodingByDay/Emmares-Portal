THE LATEST
Michael Beach Nichols Goes Viral With Wrinkles The Clown
Director Michael Beach Nichols (the acclaimed Welcome To Leith) digs deep into a bizarre internet sensation with his chilling documentary Wrinkles The Clown, which raises questions about truth, perception and the madness of the online world.

Sarah Gaul: Hot Mess

Comedienne, actress, writer and singer Sarah Gaul is a delightful flurry of winning humour and raw honesty in the Aussie comedy drama Hot Mess. 
Roschdy Zem: Gallic Authority
Roschdy Zem takes on roles as two authority figures in projects set to screen as part of the Alliance Francais French Film Festival.

Benedict Andrews: Seberg

Australian director Benedict Andrews (Una) continues his international ascent with Seberg, the biopic on rebel femme actress, Jean Seberg.
Partho Sen-Gupta: Hits With Slam
With the fascinating world of slam poetry as his background, director Partho Sen-Gupta crafts a compelling drama about Australia today.

Aussie Flick Relic Rocks Sundance

The Aussie horror flick Relic - about three generations of women haunted by an evil presence - has been the unlikely breakout title at Sundance.
Great Screen Couples, Bad Films
Just because two actors strike gold as an on-screen couple once, it doesn't necesarily mean that they can do it again, as our examples amply prove. 

Four Good Days At Sundance

A powerful drama about a mother (Glenn Close) navigating the drug addiction of her daughter (Mila Kunis), Four Good Days has hit at Sundance.
James Grace: Ashburn Waters
New Zealand-born, Gold Coast-based actor James Grace brings the malevolence in David Pether's low budget Aussie horror film Ashburn Waters.
