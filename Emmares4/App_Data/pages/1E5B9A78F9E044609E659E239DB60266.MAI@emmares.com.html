Heading from crosses and Dealing with high balls
 
This week's session focuses on heading the ball from crosses into the opposition box. A high number of goals are scored from players getting their heads on the end of crosses into the box, with crosses often being an affective way of catching defences off-guard. This session will teach your players the best and safest way to meet crosses with their heads and score handfuls of goals.

Meanwhile, this week's advanced session focuses on your goalkeepers dealing with high balls. High balls often represent nervy moments for the defence during a game, with their unpredictability ensuring they pose significant threat to the goal. This session will help your goalkeepers with their footwork and positioning, boosting their confidence and ability to deal with high balls in their area.
 
Today's Sessions
 
 
Heading from crosses
 
A high percentage of goals are scored from crosses into the penalty area, which is why it is crucial to practise attacking headers. Some footballers, especially young players, don’t like to head the ball. Use this session to teach your players the correct technique when running to meet a cross and heading towards goal.
 
 
Dealing with high balls
 
This session works on improving a ‘keeper’s ability to deal with a high ball over their head. This can happen when caught off their line or if a cross catches on the wind. It will also test general footwork and handling. Keepers must be “on toes” at starting position and throughout drill.
