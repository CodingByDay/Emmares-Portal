There's typically a trade-off involved in knowing too much information—just ask Sting—and lately there's been much information about trade. The U.S. and China keep working toward a deal as Donald Trump threatens more duties. Trump also restored metal tariffs on Brazil and Argentina, and put France on notice. All this has Dennis Gartman telling investors to raise cash but Citigroup's private bank urging clients to stay invested. While you consider the trade-offs, here are some stories you might have missed. — Michael Tighe

 

The Bloomberg 50

Hawaii has its "Five-0," and Bloomberg has its 5-0. This is our annual report on the 50 people in business, finance, entertainment and technology who shaped 2019. Plus a preview of who may be on next year's list.

 

More Debt, Please

Everyone has debts to pay, and an increasingly popular way to pay them is by borrowing more money. Global debt skyrocketed to record levels in an era of low rates, and that sky may not be the limit as policy makers consider going deeper in the hole to energize a slowing economy.

 

Super Swine

Chinese scientists are in the lab trying to put more pork on the nation's plates. They're busy altering genes in an effort to create a super pig that's resistant to disease, including the African swine fever that's ravaging herds and causing a global ham shortage.

 

Making a Quantum Leap

Billionaire hedge-fund manager Michael Hintze wants to move beyond quantitative investing without really getting started. His CQS firm is studying the potential of quantum computing, and it's working with a startup to develop a quantum chip that would help execute strategies.

 

Billionaires' Electric Bets

Electric cars have become hot wheels in China, and some of its richest people are steering billions of dollars into startups trying to become the next Tesla. We follow the money to explain what the tycoons have to show for these EV investments.

 

Too Much TikTok?

Take a wildly popular social-video app and put it in the hands of 200 million Bollywood fans, and what do you get? According to some Indian officials, a national security risk. The addiction to TikTok is so bad that at least one mosque felt compelled to ban its use inside.

 

Mexico's Mayhem

Drug cartels are moving into some of Mexico's prime tourist destinations, and that's triggering spasms of violence once considered unthinkable. The bloodshed also is discouraging investment just as the new government tries to lift the economy out of recession.

 

Stretching Exercises

"You're gonna need a bigger boat," Chief Brody tells shark hunter Quint in the movie "Jaws." Some cruise lines are thinking the same thing, and instead of waiting for new vessels from the shipyard, they're using a cheaper, faster way to add capacity.
