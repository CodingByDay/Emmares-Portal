Investors have inflation on their minds. The stock market might be rallying in 2021, but it’s anything but an even ascent, with investors seemingly favoring “inflation plays” all year long. Through May 7, the S&P 500 has set 26 new all-time highs, but Sam Stovall, chief investment strategist at CFRA, notes that three S&P 500 sectors -- industrials (with 29 new all-time highs YTD through May 7), financials (with 26), and materials (22) -- have “exceeded or approximated that count.” The imbalance continued last week, with gains for those sectors and energy stocks, while growth stocks slid. “Add to this Treasury Secretary Yellen’s earlier ill-timed comment on when the Fed may need to raise rates, and one gets a clear sense that investors are realigning their portfolios in preparation for an increase in both inflation readings and interest rates,” Stovall says. “Indeed, CFRA thinks that the market remains skeptical of policymakers’ views that the inflation pick-up is ‘transitory.’”

AstraZeneca (AZN) and Novavax (NVAX), two major vaccine makers, face more delays in getting FDA approval for use here in the U.S. However, that may not matter as supplies of other COVID-19 vaccines appear to be ample. Novavax’s emergency use authorization application will likely be delayed until June. AstraZeneca has decided to forego the EUA approval that other vaccine makers have received, and seek a normal license from the FDA. This will delay approval for its vaccine because the process is longer. These delays will likely not affect the pace of vaccinations in the U.S., however, as current vaccine supplies are sufficient, and the demand for shots is easing.
The Biden Administration’s support for waiving patent protection of COVID-19 vaccines will not have much impact. Obtaining the ingredients and expertise to produce these vaccines is not easy, especially for the Pfizer (PFE) and Moderna (MRNA) mRNA versions, so the waiver is not likely to boost production.
