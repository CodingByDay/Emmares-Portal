Californians go dark as fires grow

Hundreds of residents in Northern California were forced to evacuate their homes Thursday as a fast-moving wildfire fueled by strong winds and dry conditions prompted utility providers to cut power to head off more risk. The state's three major utilities cut service to hundreds of thousands of customers, with officials warning up to 1.5 million could be left in the dark. At a news conference, Gov. Gavin Newsom chided the companies for the scale of the outages and their preparedness: "It's about dog-eat-dog capitalism meeting climate change. It's about corporate greed meeting climate change." 

More: 

  • This is California's second major intentional blackout in October to prevent potential wildfires.
