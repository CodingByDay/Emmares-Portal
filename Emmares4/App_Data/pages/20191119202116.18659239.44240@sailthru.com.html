Impeachment hearings begin again

Kicking off the second week of public impeachment hearings into President Trump's dealings with Ukraine, the House Intelligence Committee heard from four witnesses Tuesday.

These were the high points:  

  • Alexander Vindman, director for European Affairs on the National Security Council, said Trump's July 25th call with Ukrainian President Zelenskiy was "improper" and saw Trump’s request for investigations as a demand. He said it was his "duty" to report his concerns to "the proper authorities" or risk "significant national security implications."
  • Jennifer Williams, special adviser for Europe and Russia in Vice President Mike Pence‘s office, said she found the call “unusual because, in contrast to other presidential calls I observed, it involved discussion of what appeared to be a domestic political matter.”
  • Kurt Volker, former special envoy to Ukraine, said he wasn’t aware of any effort to urge Ukraine to investigate Biden and felt it was "inappropriate" to discuss one in a July meeting between U.S. and Ukrainian officials. He added, Biden “respects his duties of higher office” and it’s “not credible” that he’d act in any other way.
  • Timothy Morrison, NSC senior director for Europe and Russia, said he was disappointed by Trump’s handling of the July 25th call and worried that disclosing its contents would have a negative effect in Washington, adding, “My fears have been realized.” 

$ignificant figures

400 people. Carrie Lam said hundreds of protesters were “immediately arrested” after a standoff with police at Hong Kong Polytechnic University. 

9 years. Sweden dropped its probe into an alleged rape by WikiLeaks founder Julian Assange because too much time has passed since the Aug. 2010 accusation.

106 people. How many have been killed in Iran's protests over rising fuel prices, Amnesty International said, citing "credible reports," adding, "the real death toll may be much higher."

Highly quotable

“Dither and delay.” Boris Johnson blamed opposition leader Jeremy Corbyn for Brexit deadlock in an election debate, saying a Conservative government would “end this national misery.”

"Very hard." Pence said it'd be difficult for the U.S. to sign a trade deal with China if Hong Kong protesters are met with violence. Later, the U.S. Senate unanimously passed a bill in support of the protesters. 

"Appeared to have been asleep." Two guards at an NYC jail were charged with falsifying records to conceal they were sleeping at their desks when Jeffrey Epstein killed himself.

This is not normal

Unfightable fires. Fierce and unruly fire tornadoes that make their own storm systems may get more common in Australia, with researchers predicting bushfire-fueling conditions will occur earlier and more often.

The future is now

Efficiency boost. Drones that do the work of 500 farmers in less than half the time are modernizing Asia's massive palm oil plantations by spotting fires, collecting data on crops' nutrient levels, and finding irrigation leaks.

What's good

A greener Google? German search engine Ecosia is donating up to 80% of its ad revenue to planting trees around the world. It says searches on the site rose 82% over last year aided in part by Greta Thunberg.
