Had enough of MasterChef and news programmes about COVID-19? Well, you can change it up with our reviews of the tasty flicks hitting digital platforms this week, as well as reams of perfect movie reading: Dane DeHaan scores with the new drug trafficking drama ZeroZeroZero; we take an in-depth look at the making of the Aussie classic Chopper; we get the low-down on three exciting female filmmakers (Shannon Murphy, Shelagh McLeod, Criss Gidas); and check in with Aussie cinematographer Toby Oliver, who is smashing it in the US. Stay safe, stay sane, and see you at the movies (hopefully physically, in "stage two")!  
THE LATEST
Dane DeHaan Deals In On ZeroZeroZero
A true rising star with a host of fine credits to his name - Chronicle, Lawless, Life, The Place Beyond The Pines -  Dane DeHaan now takes the lead role in the Amazon Prime series ZeroZeroZero, a gritty tale of drug trafficking and family fractures. 

The Making Of Chopper

We take an in-depth look back at the troubled production of Andrew Dominik's 2000 masterpiece Chopper, starring the mighty Eric Bana.
Filmmakers In Lockdown
The gang at Melbourne film tech company Blackmagic Design get the lowdown on life in lockdown for three Australian filmmakers.

Shannon Murphy: Killing Eve

Her acclaimed debut feature Babyteeth is very close to release, but in the meantime, Shannon Murphy has helmed two episodes of Killing Eve.
Shelagh McLeod: Astronaut
With the deeply moving and quietly inspirational drama Astronaut, Canadian/British director Shelagh McLeod has crafted something truly special.

Criss Gidas: Wireless

With her latest short film Wireless nearing release, young Gold Coast filmmaker Criss Gidas takes us through her journey in getting there.
Toby Oliver: Dead Like Me
Renowned Aussie cinematographer Toby Oliver (Looking For Alibrandi, Wolf Creek 2) has decamped to the US for a slew of top-flight projects.

Capricorn Film Fest Goes To Class

The Capricorn Film Festival will present the upcoming four-day online Screen Acting Essentials workshop with four of Australia’s best acting tutors.
