Trump tests the Supreme Court

President Trump is taking the drawn-out fight over publicizing his tax returns to the Supreme Court. He asked justices to block a subpoena from a New York prosecutor seeking his tax returns back to 2011, marking the first time the high court has been drawn into the president's personal and business dealings. The case, if the Supreme Court decides to take it, will test the body's willingness to intervene on Trump's behalf as well as his claims to absolute immunity from investigations.

More: 

  • Trump's lawyers asked SCOTUS to fast-track the case so it could be heard this term, which ends in late June.
  • A lower court has already ruled the subpoena for the returns was proper, and a separate case concluded Congress should have access to the records.
