SUPPORT PLAY
 
Supporting your teammates in possession of the ball

It is vital to the success of your team that when your players pass the ball they're aware that their job is not yet over. By ensuring your players remain switched on and focused in supporting their teammates, you'll see a growth in positive possession and passing efficiency all over the pitch.

The best support play will require your team to make dynamic runs, create space and make themselves a viable passing option. In doing so, you can ensure that your opposition find it that much harder to regain possession and thus will reduce the attacking chances they have. By drilling your squad on their support play it will bring new dimensions to each training session you run, encouraging creative individual and team play that stands to take your play to the next level.

Today we have four sessions that will help to show your players the value of supporting one another.

TONY PULIS
 
SUPPORTING THE STRIKER
 
This session is all about encouraging players to make forward runs to support the striker. To make attacks lightning fast, it is also focused on getting the ball to the striker as quickly as possible. We would run this session when we see a team that has defenders and wingers that aren’t very quick and aren’t good at seeing danger and getting into good positions.

PETER TAYLOR
 
DEVELOPING AND SUPPORTING A MOVE
 
This session progresses a move from the back into midfield and the strikers, with a strong emphasis on supporting runs and getting extra players into advanced areas. Movement is all about creating space which is vital in giving players the time and freedom to express themselves.

RITEESH MISHRA
 
PLAY FORWARD AND SUPPORT
 
This training session is about creating opportunities to play forward passes into the feet of forward players, or into attacking space. It is also aimed at encouraging quick support play from players behind the pass in order to either offer secure support behind the ball or to join in the attack with runs past the ball.

DOUGIE FREEDMAN
 
SUPPORTING FROM DEEP
 
This session is about forward runs and the ability of players to work at pace, supporting from deep or getting beyond a front striker to attack danger areas. For teams looking to build attacking play, intelligent forward running is essential, as is the timing and execution of those breaks. Players must also work to vary passes into feet with balls into space.
