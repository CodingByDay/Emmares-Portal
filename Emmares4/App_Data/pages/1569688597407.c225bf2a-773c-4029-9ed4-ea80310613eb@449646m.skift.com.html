Editor's Note

The collapse of UK-based Thomas Cook Group last Monday marks the end of an era, with ripple effects widening throughout the travel industry. In the end, the storied 178-year-old tour operator could not adjust to changing times and, mired in debt, could not be saved.

Here at Skift, Europe Editor Patrick Whyte led the charge in covering this important moment. As Asia Editor Raini Hamdi reports, however, the Thomas Cook brand lives on in the form of Thomas Cook China and Thomas Cook India, separate companies with diversifed revenue streams. See the special section below for our coverage, and in the coming week, watch out for more insightful analysis on this continuing story.

Meanwhile, the iconic Cox & Kings, more than two centuries old, also faltered this week. Will it be next? Stay tuned.

— Faye Chiu, Assistant Managing Editor, fc@skift.com, @2fayechiu
