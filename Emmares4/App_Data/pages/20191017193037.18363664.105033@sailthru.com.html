We knew quite a bit about Google's new Pixel before the phone was even announced. Because the company itself leaked the device design months before its official debut Tuesday, we knew about the Pixel 4's revamped camera and its lack of a fingerprint sensor.

But that foreknowledge didn't make this week's unveiling any less exciting. That's because what Google is really selling when it shows off a new Pixel isn't hardware, but the software features packed within—like gesture controls, face unlocking, and an AI-powered camera, just to name a few. To read about everything Google has loaded into its new $799 smartphone, check out Lauren Goode's report.

Also, the Pixel wasn't the only thing announced this week. Read the full roundup of Google's new hardware, and be sure to check out our advice on where to preorder your own Pixel.

In other news, macOS Catalina is available for download. Our reviewer Scott Gilbertson says it's worth upgrading your Mac. And there are some features not in this release that you may miss.
