...Welcome To The Week In Movies!!!
Australia’s Premier Online Movie Magazine

It's all slightly old school this week, with The Shining sequel (or "continuation" or "next chapter", if you want to be a little more refined) Doctor Sleep, Martin Scorsese's crime epic The Irishman and Pedro Almodovar's Pain And Glory all hurtling into cinemas and reminding us of great films past and filmmakers with a real sense of history. Also out this week are three local titles: an Aussie charmer (Emu Runner), an Aussie shocker (The Furies) and an Aussie doco (At The Coliseum Deluxe). And don't forget that The Japanese Film Festival and The Russian Resurrection Film Festival are making their way around the nation right now! See you at the movies!

THE LATEST
Pedro Almodovar's Life Of Pain And Glory
The venerable writer and director behind such indelible classics as Volver and Women On The Verge Of A Nervous Breakdown still broils with a passion for filmmaking, and delivers his most personal work yet with Pain And Glory. 

On The Road: Moon Rock For Monday

We visit the set of Moon Rock For Monday, the tale of a terminally ill young girl who hits the road looking for a cure with a fugitive on the run. 
Jen, Reese & Steve On Morning Wars
Jennifer Aniston, Reese Witherspoon and Steve Carell headline Morning Wars, Apple TV+'s major player in its battle for streaming supremacy. 

Kane Senes, Hannah Barlow: For Now

Co-directors, co-writers, and co-stars Hannah Barlow and Kane Senes talk us through multi-tasking and their low budget wonder For Now.
Isshin Inudo: Samurai Shifters
With the supremely goofy Samurai Shifters, director Isshin Inudo has crafted a samurai flick way more Monty Python than Yojimbo or Sanjuro. 

Bradley Liew: Motel Acacia

With funding from six nations across Asia and Europe, and a multinational cast (with two Aussies), Bradley Liew's Motel Acacia goes beyond borders.
The Films Of Errol Flynn
In this first part of an ongoing series, we look at the early works of one of Australia's first - and most divisive - international movie stars.

Sweet River Starts To Flow

Marking the directorial debut of Justin McMillan, this darkly unconventional psychological thriller starring Lisa Kay has just gone into production.
Great White Is On The Hunt
Production has commenced on the killer shark thriller Great White, directed by Martin Wilson and starring Katrina Bowden and Aaron Jakubenko.
