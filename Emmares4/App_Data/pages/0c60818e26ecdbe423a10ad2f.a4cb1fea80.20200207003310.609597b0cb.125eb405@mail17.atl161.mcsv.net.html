Not just for trolls

Hola, 
 

Deepfakes have mostly been talked about as a technology used for trolling or deception. But what about commercial possibilities? 

 

Our 2020 AI Trends report reveals how deepfakes could “resurrect celebrities, shake up retail, and revolutionize influencer marketing.” 

 

In the 40-page report, we also look at: 

  • Quantum machine learning taking baby steps

  • Google’s plans for AI-powered smart cities

  • Next-gen cybersecurity threats harnessing AI

Deepfakes sit at the super-visible intersection of entertainment and social networks.

At Samsung, researchers used deepfake tech to create a realistic Marilyn Monroe “talking head.” Similar tactics were used to rejuvenate British actor Bill Nighy in Pokémon Detective Pikachu.  
Source: Getty Images
Deepfakery’s influence is only beginning to be felt. This week, Twitter announced a plan to kill deepfakes spreading in tweets, but “only if they’re harmful.”

Hmmm: Good fakes vs. bad fakes?

Good luck with that.

We look at 23andMe’s approach to consumer genetics, including its business model, competitors, and challenges, in this  client-exclusive report. 
     

  • 15 years: Google Maps turns 15 today. Google has changed a lot since then, making moves to  become an AI powerhouse,  push into healthcare, spend billions on acquisitions, and much more. 

  • 2,200 years: Scientists have grown dates from seeds that are estimated to be up to 2,200 years old. The ancient seeds were recovered from archaeological sites and carbon dated to assess their age. The researchers are using the reanimated fruit to gain insights into DNA preservation as well as farming practices from the time.
