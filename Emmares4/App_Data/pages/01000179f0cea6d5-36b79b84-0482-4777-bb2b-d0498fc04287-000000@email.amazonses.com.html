Will Apple end the newsletter boom?

The problem with killing email pixels is that most responsible senders use them to stop sending emails to people that don’t engage with their emails – and email pixels are one of the primary ways that we know that people aren’t opening the newsletter.

The end result is that you’ll end up getting a lot more email because senders have very little way to know who to send it to.

And there’s very little tracking benefit to using email pixels in the first place. Gmail and others already proxy them so we can’t see your IP address, and the only piece of information is that somebody opened an email.
