As the coronavirus continues its spread worldwide, the news is moving faster than ever and it's hard to keep up.

The good news: QuickTake is launching a weekly show to get you up to speed. In this week's Covid-19 wrap: we dive into the shocking unemployment numbers, meet a nurse working on the frontlines, go inside a hospital in Wuhan and check out the video game that could help fight the virus. Watch here:  https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=T6jIf6KBztw

And as promised, here are 10 uplifting stories to help end your week on a higher note.

1. Human testing of Johnson & Johnson's coronavirus vaccine will begin by September. It could be available for emergency use in early 2021.

2. Engineers at the University of Florida have developed a way to make DIY ventilators. Nearly anyone can make the life-saving machine using items found in a hardware store.

3. Big tobacco becomes an unlikely partner in the race to find a coronavirus vaccine. A partially owned unit of Phillip Morris expects human trials for a potential vaccine this summer.

4. Leonardo DiCaprio and others will help launch America’s Food Fund. The fund has already raised $12 million to help those affected by the coronavirus.

5. This shoeshiner gave his land to help people struggling amid the coronavirus outbreak. Byung-rok Kim donated to his local government in Paju, South Korea to help the community stay afloat in the virus crisis.

6. Restaurants in San Francisco are feeding employees in the city's six biggest hospitals. 42 restaurants have so far raised over $350,000 and have delivered about 5,000 meals, with funding for thousands more.

7. Orchestras worldwide give us powerful at-home concerts. For example, the Antwerp Philharmonic are reuniting remotely to weave together strings, woodwinds and brass.

8. The coronavirus made Anthony Fauci America's top doc. Since the outbreak began, he's become a TV star and drawn fans worldwide.

9. You can now book a virtual trip to see the the Northern Lights.
These live webcams tours could be a surprise boom for Alaska tourism.

10. A tribe of Kashmiri mountain goats are taking over a Welsh town. In Llandudno, the goats are roaming the streets, climbing walls and chewing through plants, as humans retreat indoors.
