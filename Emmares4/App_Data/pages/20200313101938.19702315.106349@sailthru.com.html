03.13.20

As the number of coronavirus cases in the US continues to climb, communities are rightly encouraging, if not enforcing, ways of social distancing: Officials are postponing events, companies are asking employees to work from home, and, with the help of video conferencing platforms and collaborative software, academic institutions are moving to "distance learning."

This week, as universities around the country announced that classes would become virtual (and, in some cases, that campuses would be shutting down altogether), WIRED’s Lauren Goode spoke to students and professors about how this transition will be significantly harder on some than others. Also, in the interest of staying healthy during the outbreak, we put together a handy guide on how to make your own hand sanitizer, in the likely event your local store is all out. And for those of us who are leaning more heavily on laptops as work and school goes remote, here's a helpful tip on how to keep your computer humming along.

And in non-coronavirus news, this week we also spoke with British entrepreneur James Dyson about how his "vacuums and fans" company made the unexpected move into personal care. Sure enough, Dyson's latest product is a pricey hair straightener called the Corrale (which, it seems fitting to note, was demoed over video chat following the cancelation of the launch event).
