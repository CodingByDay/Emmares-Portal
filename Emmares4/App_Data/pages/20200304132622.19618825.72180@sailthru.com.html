Panasonic Eneloop (8-pack) rechargeable AA batteries for $14.70 at Amazon (normally $18)

If you have a handful of devices that run on traditional batteries, this is a good chance to save on Panasonic’s reliable and well-performing Eneloop series of rechargeable AAs. This set of 8 isn’t massively discounted from its typical $18-19 going rate, but it is the lowest price we’ve seen thus far.

Sonos Play:1 (refurb) for $99 at Sonos (normally $149)

Like many Sonos speakers, the Play:1 is easy to manage, works with most of the major music streaming services, and has a well-balanced sound that should prove pleasant to most ears. While Sonos has formally discontinued the compact home speaker and replaced it with the $179 Sonos One SL, it is selling certified refurbished Play:1s for $99 as part of a wider refurbished sale. Sonos says it comes with a one-year warranty. While we haven’t reviewed the Play:1 on Ars, in 2017 we wrote a deep dive on the Sonos One, which performs very similarly in terms of sound. Just remember that there’s no Alexa or Google Assistant support on the Play:1, and that you’ll need to pair it with a newer Sonos speaker to have it work with Apple’s AirPlay protocol. It’s also worth noting that there is a risk of Sonos cutting off software support later down the road, though we wouldn’t anticipate that happening in the near future here, since the Play:1 launched at least four years later than all the speakers Sonos recently dropped from its update plans.

BUY NOW

Amazon Kindle Paperwhite (8GB, ads) + Kindle Unlimited 3-month membership for $85 at Amazon (normally $130)

Here’s a great discount on Amazon’s Kindle Paperwhite, which we generally consider the best e-reader for most people. We gave this latest iteration a very favorable review when it launched in late 2018, praising it for its comfy design, crisp display, weeks-long battery life, IPX8-rated waterproofing, and Bluetooth headphone support. This entry-level model saddles you with ads on the lock screen and a relatively meager 8GB of storage, but the core reading experience is still good and Amazon’s e-book library is still massive. Today’s deal matches the lowest price we’ve ever seen, so if your Kindle is getting long in the tooth, this seems like a good opportunity to upgrade. Just remember to turn off auto-renewal for the included Kindle Unlimited trial if you’re not interested in that.

BUY NOW

Razer Viper for $60 at Amazon (normally $80)

A recommended mouse from our 2019 holiday gift guide series, the Razer Viper is excellent for both PC gaming and general use. At just 69 grams, it’s supremely light and comfortable to handle. It tracks smoothly and quickly, with five customizable DPI presets that max out at a 16,000 DPI resolution. (That maximum is overkill, per usual, but the option is nice.) The Viper’s RGB lighting is relatively restrained and easy to hide in an office, it includes eight customizable buttons, and its ambidextrous design lets it work equally well for righties and lefties. Though it’s not wireless, its unusually flexible cable doesn’t drag or get stuck on objects the way other mice could. This $20 discount is tied for the largest we’ve seen. It’s worth noting that Razer did launch a $40 Viper Mini mouse on Wednesday—we haven’t tested it, but it’s smaller than the standard Viper, has a lesser sensor, and lacks the side grips and ambidextrous button layout.
