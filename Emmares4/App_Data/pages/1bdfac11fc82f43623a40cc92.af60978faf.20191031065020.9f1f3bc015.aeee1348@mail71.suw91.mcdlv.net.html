Trick or treat! It's Halloween, and that means horror, principally in the form of the local zombie blood-guts-and-laughs extravaganza Little Monsters, which is in cinemas now. And also out this week is another entry in one of the most disappointing franchises of all time...yep, here comes Terminator: Dark Fate! Will it right past sequel wrongs, or merely stand as another epic fail? Check out our verdict below. Also out this week: the charming comedy Brittany Runs A Marathon; and the topical docos Happy Sad Man and Yuli. And don't forget that The Japanese Film Festival and The Russian Resurrection Film Festival are making their way around the nation right now! See you at the movies!

THE LATEST
Abe Forsythe Mixes Kids And Zombies With Little Monsters
The writer and director behind the daring and audacious Aussie comedies Down Under and Ned turns his cheeky eye to the undead with his riotously inappropriate horror comedy bloodbath Little Monsters. 

Jillian Bell: Brittany Runs A Marathon

The seasoned scene stealer (22 Jump Street, Rough Night) and co-creator of TV's Idiotsitter takes the lead in the comedy Brittany Runs A Marathon. 
Mike Green: Outback
Debut feature writer/director Mike Green surmounted more than a few obstacles to get his bracing rural chiller Outback on the festival circuit. 

Alicia Vikander: Earthquake Bird

Starring Alicia Vikander, the Japan-set Netflix film Earthquake Bird, directed by Wash Westmoreland, found a welcome home at the Tokyo Film Festival. 
Spooky Halloween Treats!!!
From the much loved Monster Squad and Jaws through to curios like Terrorvision and Carnival Of Souls, here are some classic Halloween treats.

Alana De Freitas: Before The Dawn

This young Australian performer has decamped to Los Angeles, and already has a writing/starring credit with Prime Video's Before The Dawn.
Nicholas Pesce: The Grudge
The young director of Eyes Of My Mother and Piercing has been handed the reins on the upcoming reboot of the horror fave The Grudge.
