Will impeachment reach the Senate?

Shortly after the House impeached President Trump Wednesday, House Speaker Nancy Pelosi stalled on passing the approved articles of impeachment to the Senate until trial plans are solidified. What gives? 

  • Pelosi's playing hardball. At the same news conference, she said she'd hold off naming House impeachment managers until the Senate decides the trial process, a move seen by some as leverage to call witnesses at the Senate trial, and by others as the "prosecutors getting cold feet in front of the entire country."
  • Acquittal may be worse than no trial. Trump wants to be vindicated. And some Democrats say a likely Senate acquittal could make him even more unleashed and further coalesce his base. The alternative? Leave an open impeachment hanging over Trump as 2020 primary voting begins.
  • Time is running out. The Senate has more on its plate besides impeachment and only hours left in session before it goes on recess until Jan. 7. Sending the impeachment articles now would force lawmakers to drop all other business to focus on the trial. 
  • But the Speaker can't withhold them forever. Or else she'll be blocking the results of impeachment, thereby defying the Constitution. However, with such little precedent on the impeachment process, the path forward is murky at best.

This is not normal

Weighed down. Half of Americans will be obese by 2030 with 25% tipping the scales at more than 100 lbs overweight unless eating habits improve, said a new study, which put women and black adults at the highest risk.

The future is now

Deep roots. The world’s oldest fossilized trees, dating back 386 million years, have been discovered in upstate New York. Scientists say they’re remnants of the oldest-known forest and could help fight climate change.

What's good

Life-changing. Amputees can finally take a dip thanks to a first-of-its-kind 3D-printed prosthetic designed to ease transition from dry ground to water. Doctors hope it will help adults with limb amputations to remain active.
