Greetings, QuickTake readers! In this edition: Kobe touching memorial, Assange’s extradition fight and helping self-driving cars “see” better.

Weinstein convicted

Harvey Weinstein was found guilty of rape and a criminal sexual act in New York on Monday, more than two years after the allegations against him sparked the #MeToo movement. A jury convicted Weinstein, 67, of rape in the third degree and a criminal sexual act in the first degree stemming from incidents involving two women that allegedly occurred in 2006 and 2013. He was found not guilty of the two most serious counts of predatory sexual assault and first-degree rape that could have resulted in a life sentence. He faces five to 25 years in prison when he's due to be sentenced March 11. Moments after the verdict, Weinstein's lawyers, who vowed to appeal the conviction, said the disgraced mogul asked: "How can this happen in America?"

$ignificant figures

$3 billion. The U.S. will sign military deals worth that much with India, Trump said on stage with PM Narendra Modi during his state visit, telling a cheering crowd that America makes "the greatest weapons ever made."

1,031.54. How many points the Dow plummeted on Monday, its worst day in two years, as authorities struggled to keep the coronavirus from spreading more widely outside China.

101. The age of Katherine Johnson, a pioneering black mathematician who solved equations by hand during NASA's early years and was portrayed in the film "Hidden Figures," who died on Monday.

Highly quotable

"MVP of girl dads." In an emotional tribute, Kobe Bryant's widow, Vanessa, praised his devotion to their family and coaching of their 13-year-old daughter, Gianna, saying: "Babe, you take care of our Gigi."

"Potential pandemic." WHO officials said new coronavirus cases in regions from Italy to Iran are "deeply concerning" but stopped short of calling it a full-blown pandemic, saying, "now is the time to prepare."

"A symbol of all that Trump condemned." Julian Assange's lawyers argued the Wikileaks founder was victimized by the president's "war on leakers and journalists" at the opening of his extradition trial in London.

This is not normal

Tainted grapes. Some Australian vintners are scrapping vintages after the 2019-2020 bushfire season created "the toughest year in living memory" for the industry by tarnishing their taste with burnt and ashy notes.

The future is now

Autonomous boost. To help self-driving cars "see" through snow and fog, a team of engineers developed a ground-penetrating radar that maps the road's surface and tracks location better than standard censors.

What's good

Hope for future diversity. In honor of Black History Month, the NHL rolled out a 525-square foot traveling museum to spotlight "200 years of black achievement in hockey" and look ahead to the next generation of stars.
