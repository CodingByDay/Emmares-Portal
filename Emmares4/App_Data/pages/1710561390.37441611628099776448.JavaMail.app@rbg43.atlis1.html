Dear Reader,

New ideas are needed to treat the millions of people who live with Alzheimer's. Attention has been focused for decades on the aberrant proteins beta-amyloid and tau. But treatments that remove these proteins have not succeeded in dramatically altering the course of the disease. Now researchers are taking a new approach by tweaking immune cells called microglia.
