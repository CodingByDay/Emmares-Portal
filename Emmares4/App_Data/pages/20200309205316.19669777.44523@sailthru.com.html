Virus fears slam global markets

Major Wall Street indices fell by more than 7% Monday, following a day of "utter carnage" across global markets sparked by an oil price war and economic fears over coronavirus. At the end of a day-long rout, the Dow Jones Industrial Average tumbled 7.8%, a loss of more than 2,000 points, making it the worst session since the December 2008 financial crisis. The S&P 500 plunged 7.6% and the Nasdaq fell by 7.29%.

Stocks tumbled so quickly after markets opened Monday that trading on the New York Stock Exchange was halted for 15 minutes for the first time since October 1997. The nosedive was, in part, a reaction to Saudi Arabia slashing oil export prices by almost 10% over the weekend, which sank crude by 25%, the most since the Gulf War in 1991. Prices stabilized in early Asian trading.

Highly quotable

"Manipulated media." Twitter put that tag, which only shows up in the timeline view, on a doctored video of Joe Biden that was shared by White House officials and retweeted by Trump as it works to target deepfakes.

"Don't be scared of two explosions." What Ashraf Ghani told a crowd of supporters in Kabul after blasts were heard during his swearing-in ceremony for his second term as Afghanistan's president.

"NYS Clean." The name of New York's own hand sanitizer, unveiled by Gov. Andrew Cuomo to safeguard against price gouging and reportedly has a higher alcohol content than Purell and smells like a "floral bouquet."

This is not normal

Critical period. The Great Barrier Reef is likely amid its "most extensive" and potentially devastating coral bleaching event yet as warming ocean temps trigger unprecedented heat stress over the next month, NOAA says.

The future is now

Masters of disguise. To thwart facial recognition systems, artists are painting their faces with seemingly random geometric shapes, which turn images of faces into mathematical formulas that can foil algorithms.

What's good

Green answer. As unrecyclable wind turbines pile up in landfills, a Danish startup found a way to crush the fiberglass blades to make noise pollution barriers that work as well as aluminum and wood but require less upkeep.
