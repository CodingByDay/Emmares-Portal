Dear Reader,

It's easy to forget that big cats still live in the U.S. Populations are quite small and threatened, but recent motion-sensitive camera images show that jaguars remain in Arizona and New Mexico. With help from humans, some 90–150 jaguars could sustainably live in the central mountain ranges of these states, according to modeling research by Eric W. Sanderson of the Wildlife Conservation Society and colleagues. Read his essay below to learn more about "justice for the jaguar."
