Dear Reader,

In the digital era, humans produce a staggering amount of data, and finding the space to store it is an ongoing problem. That's why scientists are increasingly interested in stashing information in compact DNA. As Latchesar Ionkov and Bradley Settlemyer write in this week's lead story, "The 74 million million bytes of information in the Library of Congress could be crammed into a DNA archive the size of a poppy seed—6,000 times over."

Sophie Bushwick, Associate Editor, Technology
