The poet William Blake wasn't referencing initial public offerings when he imagined "the doors of perception," yet the metaphor is germane to two listings. Behind one door is Saudi Aramco, seeking a $1.71 trillion valuation in the world's biggest IPO. Perceptions may be influenced by Crown Prince Mohammed bin Salman's initial target of a $2 trillion valuation. Behind the other is Alibaba Group, which raised $11 billion in a Hong Kong stock sale. Perceptions there may be swayed by Alibaba's ability to hold the listing as the city struggles with civil unrest. In case you've been busy cleansing your doors as per Blake's exhortation, here are some stories you might have missed. — Michael Tighe

 

Predicting a Prodigy?

Is there a genetic crystal ball that will reveal your baby's "potential talents?" Dozens of companies in China and Hong Kong say yes, and they're promising new parents a peek into their child's future through the prism of a saliva sample.

 

Balancing Act

The People's Bank of China has a $35 trillion problem, and governor Yi Gang's "flood" prevention strategy often puts him at odds with other government officials. Yet Yi maintains the support of senior leaders because his plans carry another key benefit.

 

Moving Day

New York, London and Hong Kong are losing their luster as havens for wealthy international home buyers because of political turmoil and populist backlash. So if you're looking for somewhere to park your cash, try these cities instead.

 

Trading Places

Women are leaving the trading floors of the $6.6 trillion-a-day currency market because they perceive an industry bias and don't see a lot of other women around them. Banks say they have programs in place to hire and promote women, but their upper-management rosters tell a different story.

 

Juicing GM in China

General Motors is going all-in on electric cars, but it's struggling to make a dent in China. As the nation's auto sales slump and EV competition builds, GM is rolling out a plan to resuscitate its business in the world's biggest market.

 

School Politics

The waiting lists at Hong Kong's most prestigious local schools may be getting shorter. Many finance professionals are opting to enroll their children in international schools or overseas boarding schools to shield them from the city's divisive—and destructive—political climate.

 

Playing Defense

A different type of courtside seat is available to watch the New York Knicks battle the Los Angeles Clippers. That's because the NBA teams' billionaire owners—James Dolan and Steve Ballmer—are in litigation over a piece of suburban L.A. that Ballmer has big plans for.

 

Choice of Freedom

In a part of the ocean far, far away, a group of islands are deciding whether to become the world's newest nation. If the people of Bougainville choose independence from Papua New Guinea, they'll take a massive trove of mineral resources with them.
