...Welcome To The Week In Movies!!!
Australia’s Premier Online Movie Magazine

The curve has flattened even more in the past week, so hopefully restrictions - including closed cinemas - will start to ease a little some time soon. In the meantime, here's some movie related content to keep you busy for a while! We've got reviews of the latest titles to hit digital platforms (including Vivarium, The Way Back, Pain And Glory, Sorry We Missed You and A Beautiful Day In The Neighborhood), plus we take an in-depth look at the making and legacy of the Aussie classic Animal Kingdom; we profile the literary leanings of Reese Witherspoon; we check out AFTRS' online courses; and pay tribute to the late, great Brian Dennehy. Stay safe, and (figuratively if not literally) see you at the movies!

THE LATEST
Family Business: The Making & Legacy Of Animal Kingdom
It put debut writer/director David Michod on the map, made Ben Mendelsohn and Sullivan Stapleton international stars, and scored Jacki Weaver an Oscar nomination...the bloody and brilliant Animal Kingdom is without question one of the most important and influential Australian films of the last decade. 

Reese's Literary Leanings

We look into the recent career moves of actress/producer Reese Witherspoon, who has struck gold with a series of literary adaptations. 
AFTRS: Business (Almost) As Usual
As the world changes with shocking rapidity due to the rampaging of COVID-19, AFTRS keeps its education on track with some great online courses.

Opinion: Open The Cinemas (Soon)

The beaches in Sydney are open for swimming again, and rugby league is about to kick off soon (!), so it's time to open cinemas too...soon-ish.
Australian Production In Jeopardy?
On April 15, the Federal Government suspended the quota for Oz content on free-to-air TV and Foxtel, spelling big time trouble for local production. 

Short Film: Blood Orange

Writer/director M.P. Wills offers up a playfully perverse journey of self-discovery with his short film, Blood Orange, starring Rahel Romahn.
A Tribute To Brian Dennehy
Best known for drawing first blood against Sylvester Stallone's Rambo, the recently passed Brian Dennehy was a truly masterful character actor.
