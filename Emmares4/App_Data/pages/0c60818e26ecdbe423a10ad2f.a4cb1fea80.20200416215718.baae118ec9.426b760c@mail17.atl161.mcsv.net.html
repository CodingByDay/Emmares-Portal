That's gotta hurt

Hola,

Ouch. Deals to VC-backed companies in the US are down 16% YoY in the first quarter of this year, according to data from the PwC/CB Insights Q1'20 MoneyTree report.

While venture capital firms and other investors who bet on startups might argue they’re “open for business,” the data suggests that as the pandemic crested, new term sheets became a rare commodity.

 

Deals did happen, but they tended to be larger and later-stage VC-backed deals. And clearly some investors sat on the sidelines (especially large institutional investors and asset managers who are more recent players in startup investing).

CORE INTELLIGENCE
Search Of The Week

At a time when medical staff are in extreme demand and standard in-person appointments aren’t feasible, healthcare chatbots are relieving some of the pressure on health systems. The AI-enabled tech can help answer patients’ questions, monitor biometrics, and even help diagnose symptoms.


This week, we’re highlighting a platform search that reveals  450+ companies working at the intersection of healthcare and chatbots.

450+ companies developing chatbot tech (aka virtual assistants/conversational AI) in healthcare. We’ve excluded other common industry applications, such as financial services. Source: CB Insights

Back in 2016, the much-hyped chatbot trend largely fizzled out when users realized the tech wasn’t particularly effective in general applications. Since then, it’s made a comeback in specific industries where its focus is more narrow. 

 

Now, in the wake of Covid-19, chatbots are resurgent. Just last week, Google Cloud launched an open-source chatbot resource for companies to add up-to-date coronavirus-related content to their own virtual assistants. 

 

In addition to healthcare, we covered several other industries benefiting from the tech in our Hot or Not column earlier this month.
