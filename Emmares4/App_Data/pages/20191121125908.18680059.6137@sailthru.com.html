During Wednesday’s Democratic presidential debate, the candidates were asked about their views on paid parental leave—a policy most Americans support. As the entrepreneur Andrew Yang pointed out, the U.S. is one of few countries that doesn’t have a federal paid family leave law. And only five states mandate some sort of paid time off for new parents. 

That reality has left it up to companies to fill the gaps, a patchy endeavor at best. The 20 biggest employers in the U.S. give their workers paid time off when they have children while some Wall Street firms, like Goldman Sachs, have bumped up offerings to lure in the best talent. 

That still leaves most Americans without financial support as they care for their newest family members. In 2018, only 17% of workers had access to paid family leave, according to the Bureau of Labor Statistics. Most of the Democratic presidential contenders, and some Republicans, say they want to change that. —Rebecca Greenfield

The ‘dirty doctor’

A group of more than 120 people alleged that Gordon Bates, a British doctor who worked for Barclays, sexually assaulted them during pre-employment medical exams in the 1970s and 1980s, 

Bloomberg Businessweek reports. Half a dozen employees said they told the bank that Bates had crossed the line. Barclays says it’s not liable because the doctor worked as a contractor, and those harmed weren’t yet employees.

The case, now before the U.K. Supreme Court, won’t just decide if the bank is liable for alleged sexual assaults that happened decades ago: it may potentially set a precedent for the modern gig economy.
