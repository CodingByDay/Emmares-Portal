If you're looking for something to watch this week while hibernating, we've got reviews of over ten diverse releases hitting digital, as well as some great movie reading: Hugo Weaving and writer/director Ben Lawrence talk about their new drama Hearts And Minds; Natasha Gregson Wagner discusses the fascinating doco about her late mother, Natalie Wood: What Remains Behind; we look at the new TV series Upload and the web series No Pink Cowboys; trawl through the bleak world of dystopian cinema; and investigate ten films that nearly got made but didn't quite get there. Stay safe, stay sane, and (figuratively if not literally) see you at the movies!  
THE LATEST
Hugo Weaving And Ben Lawrence Stir It Up With Hearts And Bones
Co-writer and director Ben Lawrence and leading man Hugo Weaving dig deep with the new Australian drama Hearts And Bones, the gritty story of a traumatised war photographer and a Sudanese refugee in Sydney.  

Natalie Wood: What Remains Behind

Actress Natasha Gregson Wagner ruminates on the new documentary about her famous and tragic mother, Natalie Wood: What Remains Behind.
Preview: Upload
Greg Daniels - the man that brought TV's The Office to the USA - turns his idiosyncratic attention to the afterlife with his new TV series Upload. 

Ten Films That Never Were

From Superman Lives with Nicolas Cage to Robert Altman's fascinating-sounding final project, here's a host of intriguing films that never were. 
A Guide To On Screen Dystopia
It's feeling a little bit like the end of the world at the moment, but here's a collection of films that really take things to their undeniable end point. 

Vale Irrfan Khan

India lost one of its greatest acting icons this week with the sad passing of Irrfan Khan, the star of The Lunchbox, The Namesake and Talvar.
Web Series: No Pink Cowboys
Fabiana Weiner co-created, produced and stars in the award winning No Pink Cowboys, a sparkling web series inspired by her dating life.
