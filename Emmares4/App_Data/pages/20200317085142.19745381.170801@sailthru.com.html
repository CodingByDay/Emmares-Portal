THE BIG STORY

Social distancing goes national


The White House released new national guidelines for containing the coronavirus outbreak on Monday afternoon. Here are the major recommendations, which will remain in place for 15 days before being reviewed:

  • Avoid gatherings of more than 10 people
  • Don't dine in bars, restaurants and food courts
  • Avoid non-essential travel

 

Most big cities have already put these kinds of guidelines in place but this is the first time they were made nationally — although unlike the state and city orders to close down crowded places and limit restaurants to take-out, these are just recommendations.

 

As of this morning there have been 4,664 confirmed coronavirus cases in the US, although the true number is likely far higher due to the limited number of people being tested. The hardest hit states are Washington, New York and California; 85 people have died.

STAYING ON TOP OF THIS

 

Ohio’s Democratic primary was postponed at the last minute


Ohio was due to hold its Democratic presidential primary today, but late last night, after hours of confusion, Gov. Mike DeWine said that an emergency health declaration meant polling places would be closed. “To conduct an election tomorrow would force poll workers and voters to place themselves at a unacceptable health risk,” DeWine said.


Hours earlier, a judge ruled against a lawsuit seeking to postpone the primary. DeWine had supported that lawsuit, and said previously that as governor he could not unilaterally postpone a primary election. Polls were due to open this morning at 6:30 a.m; it’s still not exactly clear how all of this is going to play out. If you want to follow the news from Ohio today, our reporter Henry Gomez is on the ground there and will have all the latest. 


The Democratic primaries in Florida, Arizona and Illinois are all scheduled to go ahead today as planned. 

SNAPSHOTS

 

Some people are still holding their weddings despite advice to avoid large gatherings. Two couples spoke to David Mack and about why they didn't cancel their big days. 


There’s been a surge in Amazon orders during the outbreak, and the company is hiring 100,000 temporary workers to deal with it. But current workers are worried the company isn’t doing enough to keep them safe.

 

The UK declared a major shift in its response to the coronavirus outbreak after realizing that its "mitigation" strategy would not work. Experts advising the government said its previous strategy would "likely result in hundreds of thousands of deaths."

 

Los Angeles is arresting fewer people and releasing some inmates early to lessen crowding in its jails. “We're protecting that population from potential exposure,” LA Sheriff Alex Villanueva said yesterday. 


We have the answer to the question everybody is asking right now: How is Rudy Giuliani feeling about the coronavirus outbreak? He spoke with Miriam Elder, and did not disappoint. 


“As a lifelong introvert, I want to stress that, for people who have the means to do so, social distancing isn’t so bad,” writes Michael Blackmon. “First of all, how freeing is it to not have to deal with FOMO?!”

DO YOU WANT TO HELP SOMEONE?

 

One question we keep hearing from readers is how you can help the many people who are already being hit by the outbreak — especially the people living paycheck to paycheck who are going to be hit extra hard by stores, restaurants, and other businesses closing. 


So we put together a list of a bunch of groups who are making sure that people get the food, money, and medical care they need. Some are local, some are national, and some are global. All of them need donations right now.
