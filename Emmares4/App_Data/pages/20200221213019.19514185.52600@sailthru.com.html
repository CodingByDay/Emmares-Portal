TGIF, QuickTake readers! Start your weekend in high spirits with these 10 uplifting stories from the past week.

1. Carnival celebrates inclusivity. Everyone is getting into the spirit for Brazil's street party, including mental health patients and plus-size women.

2. Fast-tracking a vaccine. Researchers created the first 3D map of the coronavirus, potentially opening the door to developing antiviral drugs.

3. An outpouring of support. Over $200,000 has been raised after celebs rally behind a 9-year-old bullied at school for his dwarfism.

4. The world's first air quality satellite. The South Korean satellite can track 20 types of pollutants like nitrogen dioxide and aerosols.

5. A song in surgery. Orchestra violinist Dagmar Turner was brought out of anesthesia to play the violin while doctors removed her brain tumor. 

6. Another AI-first. Researchers used a new algorithm to discover an antibiotic that can kill antibiotic-resistant strains of bacteria like E. coli.

7. Shooting down the glass ceiling. Women now have a permanent commission in the Indian Army, thanks to a ruling by the nation's top court.

8. A president's praise for Parasite. Director Bong Joon-ho and his cast celebrated the film's Oscar win by meeting South Korean President Moon Jae-in. 

9. Private space tourism.  SpaceX will fly up to four paying customers aboard its Crew Dragon for a private orbital flight. 

10. Dressing a diverse Barbie. Celebrity stylist Shiona Turini teamed up with Mattel for a new campaign celebrating Black History Month. 

Have a great long weekend! Watch your inbox for our next newsletter on Tuesday. Follow QuickTake on Twitter, YouTube, Instagram, and Facebook.

Thanks for reading!
-Masiel Torres
