They're the flashbacks nobody wants, but the viral outbreak in Wuhan, China, triggers total recall of the bird flu contagion in 2013 and the SARS epidemic in 2002 that each killed hundreds of people. All three can be traced to markets that sell live animals.

The Chinese government is trying to isolate the coronavirus by essentially quarantining a city of 11 million people. Yet more infections, more deaths and more travel restrictions are being announced just as hundreds of millions of people start crisscrossing the nation for Lunar New Year celebrations. The outbreak is causing panic buying of face masks, and it's gone global.

If you're busy keeping apace with the developments, here are some stories you may have missed.

Why Leave?

How long is too long? The leaders of some big Wall Street banks — like JPMorgan's Jamie Dimon — are staying on the job longer than the typical American CEO. And that's raising questions about whether the next generation of executives is being held back.  

A Climate Radical

British hedge-fund billionaire Chris Hohn is using his green to fight climate change. Hohn's TCI Fund Management—the world's best-performing large hedge fund—is telling companies: Shrink your carbon footprint, or we'll oust your board or dump your shares.

Martian Chronicle

China is planning a 39 million-mile trip this summer that it hopes will end with a successful touchdown on Mars. The red planet's orbit is closest to Earth this year, and that's triggering a flurry of launches by NASA, other space agencies and Elon Musk's SpaceX.

Victims of the Fury

Hong Kong's economy is taking a beating. Big spenders from mainland China are staying away after the yuan's depreciation and months of violent protests, causing city shops and restaurants to lay off workers and shut their doors.

The Green 30

There certainly are more than 30, but that's the number we picked. Bloomberg Green identifies some of the people, organizations and trends that are offering solutions to the climate crisis and that bear watching in the year ahead.

Billionaire Baller

Joe Tsai may feel a kinship with his Brooklyn Nets players when bodies start banging on the basketball court. The owner's image got roughed up when he likened Hong Kong's protests to a separatist movement, and now he's trying to get NBA games back on CCTV.

Please Take My Money

They're known as the Patriotic Millionaires, but they're not a rock band. They're a group of about 200 ultra-affluent people pushing to  rework the U.S. tax code so they have to pay more. Its members include Abigail Disney and Oscar Mayer heir Chuck Collins.

When Only a Special Rat Will Do

Need a ratty gift for that special someone? We've got a Lunar New Year gift guide to help you find the right present to celebrate the start of the Year of the Rat. Hint: This year's element is metal.
