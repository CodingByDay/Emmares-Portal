As COVID-19 restrictions lift, we can practically smell the popcorn! In the meantime, we've got reviews of this week's digital releases (Sorry For Your Loss, 100% Wolf, About An Age, Proximo, High Resolution and The Extraordinary), plus some tasty screen reading: we review some Screenability titles from The Sydney Film Festival; chat to St. Kilda Film Festival's Richard Sowada; track the greatest years in Aussie film; run the rule over the hypothetical relationship statuses of the best romantic comedy couples; and tip our hat to Akira Kurosawa and Sunday Too Far Away's legendary shearer Foley. Stay safe, stay sane, and see you at the movies...in the increasingly closer future!  
THE LATEST
Screenability At The Sydney Film Festival
One of the highlights of the upcoming online Sydney Film Festival is certain to be the Screenability sidebar, which features short films about those living with disability. 

Great Years In Australian Cinema

Great Australian films tend to come in waves, and in this special feature, we break it down to pick some of the best years in our cinema's history.
Romantic Comedy Couples
We gathered together a group of screenwriters to make the call on whether the couples in the best romantic comedies would have gone the distance.

Sunday Too Far Away's Foley

With a special online screening of a restored print on the way, we salute Sunday Too Far Away's iconic gun shearer Foley, played by Jack Thompson. 
Dark Noise Commences Shoot
Writer/director Clara Chong and DOP Ben Allan have bravely gone into production in Sydney on their film Dark Noise, with COVID-19 still in play.

Richard Sowada: St. Kilda Film Festival

In his first year, St Kilda Film Festival director Richard Sowada takes us through the changes and developments in store for the event in 2020.
The Cannes Film Festival 2020 
The Cannes Film Festival won’t be going ahead this year, but the organisers have selected a host of films for The Cannes Label instead.

Akira Kurosawa: Screen Titan

With a filmography including masterpieces like Seven Samurai, Yojimbo, Ran and The Hidden Fortress, Akira Kurosawa is indeed a master.
Blockbuster: Art Versus Profit
Ever since Steven Spielberg's Jaws put the bite on the box office, we've been in the thrall of blockbusters, like those of Marvel and LucasFilm.
