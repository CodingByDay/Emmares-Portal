Trump's risky business

President Trump is expected to sign a bill passed by Congress supporting Hong Kong protesters, despite threats from China that it would rattle U.S. relations. The bill, derided by China as a “gross” interference in Hong Kong affairs, swiftly passed the House and Senate in less than 24 hours, teeing up a weighty decision for Trump at a crucial moment in his presidency: Sign the bill into law and risk torpedoing trade talks, or veto the measure and risk Congress easily overriding him for the first time.

More: 

  • A trade deal—and the resulting economic bump—is key for Trump's re-election campaign, but going against Congress could be a dicey move as he faces impeachment in the House.

  • Nancy Pelosi said the bill shows the U.S. "stands in solidarity with the freedom-loving people of Hong Kong," adding, "this has been a very unifying issue for us." 

  • The bill, S. 1838, would require annual reviews of Hong Kong’s special status under U.S. law and sanction officials responsible for human rights abuses and undermining the city’s autonomy.

$ignificant figures

6 days. Hong Kong schools reopened after shuttering for nearly a week, even as protesters remained holed up on a university campus, surrounded by police. 

3%. The percentage by which the global economy is expected to shrink by 2050 as a result of climate change. 

150,000 people. California utility PG&E shut off electricity to thousands in Northern and Central California to prevent power lines from sparking wildfires. 

This is not normal

Not just Venice. Extreme weather is wreaking havoc all across Italy after near-daily thunderstorms flooded roads in Rome, triggered landslides in the North, and pushed Tuscany's Arno river to its highest level in 20 years.

The future is now

Farm-to-aisle. Supermarket giant Kroger is cutting out the middle man to sell the freshest food possible (and up its environmental cred) by putting farms in its stores so shoppers can pick their own veggies and herbs.

What's good

"Love Flies Up to the Sky." If you can't get into Yoyoi Kusama’s trendy infinity mirrors room in NYC, set your sights on her balloon for the Macy's Thanksgiving Day parade, the event's first-ever designed by a woman.
