New Skift Research — Introducing the Skift Recovery Index

Skift Research is excited to announce the launch of the Skift Recovery Index. From today, every week we will provide a detailed insight into the recovery of the travel industry in 15 countries.

The Skift Recovery Index is a real-time measure of where the travel industry at large — and the core verticals within it — stands in recovering from the COVID-19 pandemic. It provides the travel industry with a powerful tool for strategic planning, of utmost importance in this uncertain business climate.  

What sets us apart from the other indexing sources and dashboards is that we aggregate and decipher data measuring different aspects of travel performance, which are all crucial for understanding and estimating the short-term as well as long-term recovery courses. We are currently working with 10 data partners covering different aspects of travel and will continue to add more in the next few weeks.

In this first report we will take an overview of the broader trends for the past five months, looking back to how the crisis unfolded, and where we stand today. We will update the index every week, and subsequent reports will provide deeper analysis into specific verticals and countries as we continue to track the recovery.

The global Skift Recovery Index stands at 39 points for the week of May 24, relative to a baseline reading of 100 for the same week in 2019, indicating that our industry’s output has fallen by just over 60% due to the crisis.

We are making this first report free to the public. Click here to access the report.

 All future reports will be available to our Skift Research subscribers. All data — for deeper investigation — will also be available to our subscribers. You can find out more about becoming a subscriber here.
