Booming GDP Growth Triggers Another S&P Record
 

Stocks didn't take the most conventional path to gains Thursday, but they climbed the ladder nonetheless as the U.S. economy continued to flex its recovering muscles. The Commerce Department revealed that U.S. GDP grew by a seasonally adjusted annual rate of 6.4% during the first quarter of 2021, exceeding Kiplinger's forecast for 5.5% though coming in a little shy of consensus estimates for 6.7%. "Personal consumption spending remained the main driver of growth, as we had anticipated," says Barclays economist Pooja Sriram, who adds that the report "points to ongoing recovery and keeps the U.S. economy on track to recover to its pre-pandemic level by Q2 '21." Adding fuel to the fire was a modest decline in weekly unemployment filings, to 553,000 from 570,000 the week prior. Stocks spiked at the open Thursday, declined through midday, then recovered throughout the rest of the afternoon. Facebook (FB, +7.3%) helped power the S&P 500 (+0.7% to 4,211) to a fresh all-time high after announcing a 48% jump in Q1 revenues and a wide earnings beat. Apple (AAPL, -0.1%) reported a monster quarter, too, with sales up 54% and profits well ahead of analyst projections, but its advance was more muted thanks to a warning that the global chip shortage could dent current-quarter results. The Dow (+0.7% to 34,060) finished with a solid gain, while the Nasdaq (+0.2% to 14,082) closed modestly higher.
