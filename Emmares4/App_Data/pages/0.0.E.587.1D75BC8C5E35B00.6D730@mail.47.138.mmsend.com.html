Is another blowout earnings season on deck? Analysts had long predicted that 2021’s first quarter was going to be a monster one for corporate earnings, as companies bounced back from the weak results caused by the early stages of COVID-19 in Q1 2020. And it has been: With only a handful of firms still to report, S&P 500 companies have reported a whopping 52.4% year-over-year surge in profits for Q1. Well, here’s some more good news: Q2 is expected to be a whopper quarter, too. John Butters, FactSet Senior Earnings Analyst, says that between March 31 and May 31, estimates for Q2 earnings per share have jumped by 5.8%. That’s the highest such increase since FactSet began tracking the metric in 2002, and it compares very favorably to an average 3.7% decline in expectations during a quarter’s first two months over the past 15 years. The second quarter’s estimated growth rate currently sits at 61.0% -- if that comes to pass, it would mark the S&P 500’s greatest earnings improvement since it reported a 108.9% jump in profits in Q4 2009. Just remember: Much like back then, that leap would be coming off a low recessionary base.

Today, the Food and Drug Administration gave conditional approval to the first new Alzheimer’s drug in two decades. Aduhelm, made by Biogen (BIIB), is designed to slow the cognitive decline caused by Alzheimer’s (also called dementia), by eliminating amyloid plaques that have been associated with the disease. The scientific community has hotly debated the effectiveness of the drug, and the FDA’s decision goes against the recommendation of a panel of independent experts it convened back in November. The FDA’s conditional approval requires Biogen to conduct a large clinical study proving that removing the plaque has cognitive benefits. The drug is expected to cost between $10,000 and $25,000 and be prescribed by primary care physicians, increasing its marketing power with about $5 billion to $6 billion in potential sales.
