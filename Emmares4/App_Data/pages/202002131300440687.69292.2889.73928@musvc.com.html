Dummy Hubs: Handy Helpers


By Jim Langley “Dummy” hubs are used as a place to park the chain when the rear wheel has been removed. Sometimes they’re called “sleeping hubs” or “chain holders.”

They’re usually comprised of a short axle that’s bolted in place in the right rear dropout of the frame with a built in pulley that sits inboard of the dropout to rest the chain on. With the chain on the pulley it can’t get tangled and it’s more likely to remain on the chainring, too.

Hiplok Z Lok Review

 

By Brandon Bilyeu The Z LOK design was inspired by a product that most people are familiar with, the humble zip tie. Zip ties are a serrated strap (typically nylon) with a ratchet head on one end. When the strap is inserted into the head, forming a loop, the ratchet allows the zip tie to be tightened around objects. The ratchet only works in one direction, so the zip tie does not loosen and must be cut to be removed.
