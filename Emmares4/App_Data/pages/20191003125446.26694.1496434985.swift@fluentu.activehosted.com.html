Learning a language is hard enough as it is.

Let's not make it any more difficult than it has to be.

To save you a lot of trouble, here's a quick story.

When I was learning Chinese and Japanese, I was so eager to learn.

I would get vocabulary word lists any chance I got.

I found books of idioms.

I started memorizing dictionaries.

And of course, I loved my textbooks.

And then it started happening....

I'd try out a new word or phrase with my friends and they'd burst out laughing.

Other times I'd say something and they'd look at me with a confused expression.

What was going on?

I was becoming an expert at using obscure and awkward phrases.

Talking with me was like talking with a robot or someone speaking another dialect.

What's the solution to this problem?

It's to be careful about the sources that we use to learn a language.

Whenever you encounter a new word, quickly check it by using it with one of your native speaker friends.

Another option is to try to use native language content. If you're learning from TV shows and commercials, then you can be more sure that it's "safe" content.
