Even in an era when just about every company says it won’t tolerate harassment or inequality, there’s one sector still silencing aggrieved employees: Wall Street. Forced arbitration, captive human resources departments, high-priced lawyers and a culture of fear are used to keep financial workers running scared. While this grim strategy isn’t unique, the industry’s mastery of it has prevented the #MeToo movement from making much of a dent among masters of the universe. —Josh Petri

Boeing Faces Huge Test With Starliner Launch

NASA’s effort to resume flying American astronauts on American spacecraft—something that hasn’t happened since the Space Shuttle program ended in 2011—faces a major test. Boeing on Friday hopes to fly its new Starliner spacecraft on an initial voyage to the ISS—a mission that could set the stage for human flight in 2020.
