Just how expensive is Biogen’s (BIIB) new Alzheimer’s drug aducanumab, also known as Aduhelm? By the mid-2020s, it could account for 1% of national health spending and 8% of prescription drug spending, thanks to its $56,000 per patient annual price tag, according to one estimate. Note the potentially substantial impact on spending by Medicare Part B, the portion of the federal health insurance program for senior citizens that covers drugs administered in doctors’ offices and hospitals. In a scenario where 1 million patients annually use aducanumab at the current list price, Medicare Part B spending on prescription drugs could more than double from its current annual level of $37 billion.

But this also makes aducanumab a ripe target for Washington politicians and patient advocates angry about rising prescription drug prices. The Institute for Clinical and Economic Review, a nonprofit, figures a justifiable price for aducanumab of only $2500 to $8300. Both the Centers for Medicare and Medicaid Services and insurers could push back against Biogen's price, especially given the lack of clear drug effectiveness. And by setting such a high price for a drug of uncertain benefit to a vulnerable patient population, Biogen will, at the very least, need to deal with some thorny public relations issues.

Microsoft (MSFT) cleared the $2 trillion market-capitalization mark on Tuesday, but Wedbush analyst Daniel Ives believes the tech mega-cap’s ceiling is even higher. In a note released this morning, Ives reiterated his Outperform rating (equivalent of Buy) and raised his 12-month price target on Microsoft shares from $310 per share to $325, implying 22% upside over the next year. The spark? Renewed optimism over what has been Microsoft’s primary growth story for years: The cloud. In his latest June-quarter checks, Ives said his team is “seeing deal sizes continue to increase markedly” amid an acceleration of enterprise-wide shifts to cloud-based architecture, as companies accept that their workforces will retain a heavy remote focus post-pandemic. Says Ives: “While many tech stocks overall are all being lumped together as part of the WFH trade, we believe the growth story at MSFT is not slowing down as more enterprises/governments head down this cloud path over the coming years.”
