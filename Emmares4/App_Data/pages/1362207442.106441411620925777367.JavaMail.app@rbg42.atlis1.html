May 13, 2021

Dear Reader,
 

This week, we’re looking at the rise of interplanetary aviation and the fall of a giant rocket from space. Our lead story reports on the latest happenings from NASA’s Ingenuity Mars Helicopter, which successfully completed a series of five test flights before entering a new, more exploratory phase of its mission. From there, we have coverage of the uncontrolled atmospheric reentry of the large booster from China’s Long March 5b rocket, which briefly posed a hazard to populated areas before safely splashing down earlier this week in the ocean. Elsewhere, we have additional stories about quantum entanglement via drum duets, math games from the late John Conway, physicists’ progress in solving the three-body problem, and more. Enjoy!

Lee Billings, Senior Editor, Space & Physics
@LeeBillings
