Right now, if you want to step into the augmented reality ultraworld, what do you do? There are full-coverage headsets you can put on. Cool, but they're bulky and awkward. Or you can hold up your phone and peer at your surroundings through the camera lens. Less cool, as it narrows your field of view and, after a while, sends lactic acid racing to your shoulder. Equally as imperfect are all the iterations of smart glasses, regular optical frames that float a tiny display in front of one or both eyeballs.

This week, WIRED's Julian Chokkattu got a peek at the future of augmented reality wearable tech: a smart contact lens. The new prototype from Mojo Vision puts a tiny, dense display right in the middle of a contact lens. It's worn like a regular corrective lens, directly on the cornea. When you look around the world, it adds layers of text, icons, and helpful information to every place you direct your gaze. Translations, directions, sets of instructions, contact info, surface temperatures—it's all visible to you, and you don't look like a fool wearing it. Mojo Vision's tech is still a few years away from being complete, but Julian says it shows tremendous promise.
