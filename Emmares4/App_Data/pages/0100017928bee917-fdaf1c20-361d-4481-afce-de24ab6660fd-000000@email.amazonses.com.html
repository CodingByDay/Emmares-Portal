Did You Know?

The idle (nominal) voltage on a telephone line is -48 to -52 volts DC, but 105 to 150 volts of 20 Hz AC current is superimposed over the DC voltage already present on the idle line in order to trigger the phone ringer and alert the user or party line users to an incoming call.
