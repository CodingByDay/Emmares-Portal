Relationships are tricky to navigate. There are highs and lows, and just when you think you know someone, they can act like someone you don't know at all. Take Donald Trump and Xi Jinping.

With the first phase of a U.S.-China trade deal in "the final throes," Trump signed legislation supporting Hong Kong protesters, who want greater autonomy — from China. That incensed Xi's government, which summoned the U.S. ambassador and threatened retaliation, telling reporters to "stay tuned." While you were busy tracking these ups and downs, here are some stories you might have missed.

Toughest Job in Finance

China's banking regulator oversees a $40 trillion system racked by inefficiencies, and observers say his strategies for shoring it up are, on their face, contradictory. Yet Guo Shuqing is winning favor for walking that tightrope between eliminating risks and seeding growth.

 

Who Am I?

DNA makes you "you," yet different DNA tests can give different answers about who "you" are. Our reporter K. Oanh Ha tried out tests in both the U.S. and China so she could compare the results, and she found them concerning.

 

A is for Acing

The toughest test in finance takes hundreds of hours of prep. But a flood of CFA applicants from China keeps beating it in spite of the language barrier. The reason why may be found in high school.

 

No Escape From Reality

"Is this the real life? Is this just fantasy?" Freddie Mercury's questions may be running through the minds of some analysts as they scrutinize SoftBank's bookkeeping after the WeWork fiasco. When is profit a real profit and not just one on paper?

 

Is the Aporkalypse Now?

There aren't enough pigs on the planet to satisfy China's demand for pork. African swine fever is ravaging herds globally, and China is slaughtering millions of animals to contain the outbreak. That's triggering economic and political crises in the world's biggest consumer of pork products.

 

Mariah's Subsidized Housing

It's easy to get "swipe wrist" when scrolling through Airbnb to find a vacation getaway. Not so for celebrities like Mariah Carey and Beyonce. The swiping is done for them — the company is using influencers to gain a luxury sheen before next year's potential IPO.

 

Planning a Bloc Party?

The Soviet Union's collapse sent many of its republics into an economic tailspin, but not Belarus. During his 25-year rule, President Alexander Lukashenko made the nation a better place to live than most of its former comrades. That may change now that Russia is calling.

Speed E-Racer

If your electric car packs more punch than you imagined, you can thank its cousins on the racetrack. Technology breakthroughs by competitors in Formula E are being transferred into passenger cars as manufacturers look for any edge to attract buyers.
