Start your engines! It's all-out action as the car racing drama Ford V Ferrari and the rebooted Charlie's Angels speed into cinemas this week. There are slightly slower paced films out this week too, with the taut political drama The Report and the natural wonder of Ailo's Journey. We also take an early look at next week's cracking rock doco release Suzi Q. And don't forget that The Japanese Film Festival and The Russian Resurrection Film Festival are continuing their journeys around the nation right now, and that The Capricorn Film Festival kicks off next week on Wednesday! THE LATEST
Liam Firmager Rocks Out With Suzi Q
After documenting the life of Peter Brock in King Of The Mountain, Aussie director Liam Firmager sets his sights on an even bigger icon with rock superstar and feminist pioneer Suzi Quatro in his wonderfully entertaining documentary Suzi Q.

Daniel Jones: The Report

The real life US Senate staffer who inspired this week's political drama The Report talks about having his life transferred to the big screen. 
Em Baker: I Am No Bird
The subject of weddings might seem an unlikely topic for a feminist documentary, but director Em Baker has done just that with I Am No Bird. 

Jeff Greenberg: Screen Forever

We chatted with the high powered talent agent at the recent Screen Forever conference about Australia's role in new content creation.
After The Wedding: Complex Emotions
Anna Kendrick, Bill Hader and Shirley MacLaine unwrap the Xmas movie Noelle, one of the high profile offerings set to premiere on Disney+.

Macoto Tezuka Conjures Stardust

Prolific Japanese director Macoto Tezuka reminisces with FilmInk about his much loved 1980s cult film, The Legend Of The Stardust Brothers.
The Films Of Errol Flynn: Part 2
In this second part of an ongoing series, we look at the early works of one of Australia's first - and most divisive - international movie stars.
