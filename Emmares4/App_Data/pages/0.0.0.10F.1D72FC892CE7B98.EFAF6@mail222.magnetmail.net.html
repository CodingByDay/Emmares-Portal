Financial stocks have enjoyed a rip-roaring 2021, rising 19.3% year-to-date to make it the market’s second-best-performing sector behind energy (27.9%). However, a few analysts are becoming a bit more timid on financials given their rapid run-up and the potential for bond yields to plateau. In that group is Raymond James, whose analysts previewed the sector ahead of the Q1 earnings season, which begins this week. The financial firm believes “it will increasingly be a stock picker’s market for the banks given the move in bank stocks the past few quarters, (but we generally see greater upside potential in smaller banks vs. their larger brethren given stronger growth potential, improving credit outlook, less regulatory risk, and rising M&A activity).” Raymond James outlines a number of potential winners based on several potential cases -- bull, base and bear -- but a few stocks make the cut in every scenario. That includes Strong Buy-rated small cap Banner (BANR), mid-cap Axos Financial (AX) and large cap Signature Bank (SBNY).

Expect the Census Bureau to release a really good retail sales report for March this Thursday -- perhaps showing better than 10% growth. Credit card data points to strong growth driven by government stimulus checks, tax refunds and better weather. Every category of spending was up strongly, except for groceries. Airline tickets picked up the most.

Meanwhile, there’s good news about an antibody treatment developed by Regeneron (REGN): It was 81% effective in preventing COVID-19 in those who had been exposed to it when given as a single shot. This mirrors previously released results from Eli Lilly (LLY) for antibody treatments given intravenously and is a big deal because the intravenous requirement had been a barrier to the expanded use of the treatments. Demonstrating that results are just as good when given as a simple shot has the potential to broaden their use.
