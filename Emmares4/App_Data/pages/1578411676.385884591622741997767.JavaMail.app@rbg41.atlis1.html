June 03, 2021

Dear Reader,

This week, we're celebrating the news of NASA's impending return to Venus, an exploration destination the space agency has overlooked for most of the past three decades. Yesterday, NASA's administrator Bill Nelson announced that two ambitious missions, VERITAS and DAVINCI+, will each journey to our sister world before the decade is out. Could this be the beginning of a renaissance in Venus-centric planetary science? Read our coverage to find out, then turn to our other stories about the delayed launch of the James Webb Space Telescope, new wrinkles in the ongoing mystery of dark matter, the greatest unsolved problems in mathematics, and more.
