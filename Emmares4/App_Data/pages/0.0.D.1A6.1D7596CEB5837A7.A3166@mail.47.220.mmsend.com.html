The economy gained a sizable 559,000 jobs in May, restoring the trend of strong gains as the economy opens up and more people are vaccinated. Restaurants accounted for a third of the total, as restrictions on dining fade away. They will continue to contribute the bulk of the gains this year, as they are still 1.5 million below their prepandemic level. Recreation, hotels, education and health care also showed nice gains. Wages appear to be rising briskly for production workers.

Normality is slowing returning. The number of teleworking employees declined to 16.6% in May from 18.3% in April. The number prevented from looking for work because of the pandemic dropped to 2.5 million from 2.8 million. However, total employment is still 7.6 million below its prepandemic level. At this rate of job gain, it will still take 14 months to recover fully. There is concern that some of these 7.6 million have permanently retired or may not come back to look for work again, contributing to labor shortages.

JBS has made a quick recovery from the recent cyberattack that forced the world’s largest meatpacking company to temporarily halt operations at several North American plants. But the incident is only the latest evidence of America’s vulnerability to the sort of ransomware attacks—in which a hacker effectively holds a target’s computer network hostage in exchange for a sum of money—that also took down the Colonial Pipeline just a few weeks ago. And it’s forcing Uncle Sam to get serious about cybersecurity. The Dept. of Justice, for example, is reportedly planning to elevate the priority of ransomware cases to the same level as terrorism ones. Businesses are also feeling pressure to beef up their defenses. The question is whether they can move fast enough to stave off this growing threat.
