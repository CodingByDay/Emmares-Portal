Westerdam liner docks in Cambodia

The outcast Westerdam luxury cruise liner has been taken in by Cambodia. The ship had been left stranded at sea for nearly two weeks after Thailand, Taiwan, Japan, the Philippines and Guam turned it away over unfounded fears of suspected coronavirus cases on board. Cambodian authorities greenlit the ship’s arrival to Sihanoukville after representatives for Carnival’s Holland American Line stressed that all 2,257 passengers and crew were healthy. It will remain in port for several days and passengers will be allowed to go ashore before traveling home.

More:

  • Holland America will arrange and pay for customers’ flights home and refund the cost of the cruise, it said.
  • The Westerdam’s next scheduled voyage, from Yokohama on Feb. 15, was canceled, as the cruise line assesses the "impact of current port restrictions in Asia on cruises departing Feb. 29 or later.”
  • China's Hubei province reported 14,840 new coronavirus cases after it revised the method for counting infections, bringing the death toll in mainland China to at least 1,355.
