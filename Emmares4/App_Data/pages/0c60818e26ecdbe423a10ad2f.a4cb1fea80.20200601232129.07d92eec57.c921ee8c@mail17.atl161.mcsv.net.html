A grim outlook

Hi there,


Here's what we're watching in business and tech news this week:

  • Friday’s May jobs report is expected to contain significantly worse numbers than April’s — which saw US unemployment skyrocket to a record 14.7%. Covid-19 wiped out 21M US jobs in April, the equivalent of a decade of job gains.
     

  • This is a relatively quiet week for earnings. Look for Zoom Video Communications, DocuSign, and Slack to post strong results this week, propelled by the move to remote work amid Covid-19. Tomorrow, look for updates from luxury jeweler Tiffany & Co. regarding the LVMH merger.
     

  • Tencent is reportedly buying a $200M stake in Warner Music Group ahead of the record company’s trading debut this week. The Chinese tech giant — which owns Tencent Music, one of China’s largest music streaming businesses — has previously acquired minority stakes in Spotify and Universal Music Group. Take a look at more of Tencent’s largest bets in this infographic.
     

  • Eyes are on drugmaker Eli Lilly, which began human trials of its experimental Covid-19 antibody treatment today. The drug aims to treat patients diagnosed with Covid-19, but the company eventually wants to also test for disease prevention in those who are at risk.
