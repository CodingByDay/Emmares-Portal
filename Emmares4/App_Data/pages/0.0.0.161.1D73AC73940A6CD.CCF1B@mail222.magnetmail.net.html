Where to look for the next great market rotation: We’ve talked for months now about the U.S. economic recovery and the resultant rotation trade into more value-oriented and cyclical stocks. That’s hardly an American-only phenomenon, of course – most of the rest of the world’s economies are rebounding, too, albeit at different speeds. BCA Research, for one, thinks the next great reopening opportunity is going to materialize across the Atlantic. “A rotation from pandemic leaders to laggards will dominate global growth dynamics going forward. GDP releases next week will show the U.S. economy growing and the Euro Area one contracting in Q1. However, U.S. economic growth is currently in the process of peaking and will be overtaken by the Eurozone over the coming 12 months,” says BCA Research. “This process will buoy European equities which are also more attractive on valuation grounds and are more exposed to the global economy. Thus, we recommend an overweight allocation to Eurozone stocks and favor EUR/USD on a cyclical investment horizon.” Investors looking for a starting place for their research might consider the European Dividend Aristocrats -- a group of serial dividend raisers that collectively tend to yield more than their American counterparts.

As for the U.S. economy, its prospects keep looking better and better. After a banner report on consumer spending in March and amid continuing progress in getting Americans vaccinated against COVID-19, the staff of The Kiplinger Letter are now predicting that U.S. GDP will grow by 6.6% this year. That would mark the fastest annual growth since 1984: A very welcome rebound after the beating the economy took from the pandemic and its ripple effects in 2020.
