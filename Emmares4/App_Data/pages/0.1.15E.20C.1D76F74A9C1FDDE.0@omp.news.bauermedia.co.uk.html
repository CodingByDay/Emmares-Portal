Plagiarising the past vs Thinking Different
Hordes of Gen-X (and older) car enthusiasts are inevitably excited by restomods and the ability of 400Vs to spark life into the forgotten designs of the past. Renault's new concepts evoke the ulititarian 4 and super-chic 5, but where's the originality - and do younger buyers even care? Even Volvo are ransacking the back catalogue.

Presenting variations on a CMF-theme such as the MeganE is very different to the reinvention of the wheel that gave those old cars such distinct, new characters. Nissan's plans are linked to Renault, as well - scroll down to see what's in store.

There's some old-school cool in the best way this week - the Polo GTI gets more power, and the Cayenne Coupe gets much more power; just as Taycan owners face a dealer trip to avoid the sudden loss of it. Plus we take a look at the wild directions '90s innovation could take with the Mazda MX-3 in our retro drive. Best of all, though, Citroen shows how to think different - we compare the Ami EV with the mould-breaking original 2CV to see how clean-sheet design has changed.

Have a great weekend, and enjoy!

Citroen Ami: a friendly 2CV for individuals?
One company could plunder its back catalogue for electric inspiration; Citroen. For decades it introduced technology and concepts no other firm dared to try - but keeps on moving forward, with few callbacks in design or concepts, little attempt to cash in on nostalgia.

Yet there are striking similarities between the original affordable clever car, and Citroen's vision of minimalist electric transport for the 21st century. Tim Pollard takes the highly original Ami to meet the original back-to-basics Citroen - the 2CV.
