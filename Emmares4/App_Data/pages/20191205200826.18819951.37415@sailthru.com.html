France strikes against Macron plan

Hundreds of thousands of people in France walked off their jobs Thursday as more than 30 unions launched an open-ended, nationwide strike to oppose President Emmanuel Macron’s pension reform plans. Protesters marched in Paris, Lyon, Marseilles and dozens of other cities, forcing the closure of metros and trains, hospitals, schools, and even the Eiffel Tower. Macron’s plan is to replace the country’s pension system with a universal points-based one to make it financially sustainable, which critics say would shrink benefits and force millions of people to work longer.

More: 

  • Police deployed tear gas in Bordeaux and arrested more than 80 in Paris after masked activists smashed store windows, set fires and hurled flares into crowds of otherwise peaceful protesters.
  • The strike is being compared to France’s crippling 1995 protests, also against retirement reform, and could become one of Macron’s biggest challenges since the Yellow Vest protests started a year ago.
