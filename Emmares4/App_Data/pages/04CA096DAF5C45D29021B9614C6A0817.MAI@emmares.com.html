Reaction saves & Wing it
 
This week's session focuses on the reaction saves of your goalkeeper. This session looks to help develop the speed of your keepers when it comes to key moments in the game. Due to their vital importance to the outcome of any given game, the reactions of your keepers are of paramount importance when you rely on them most. This session should be run regularly to constantly push for faster reactions and grow the confidence  of the keeper in net.

Meanwhile, this week's advanced session focuses on the importance of delivering the ball into the box no matter what. This session will help your players to understand that the style of delivery when delivering the ball into the box isn't always important, as long as the ball makes it there. This will not only help the decision-making skills of your players, but help to develop their crossing play and awareness of the players in the box.
Today's Sessions
 
 
Reaction saves
 
Increasing a goalkeeper’s reaction time by even a fraction of a second will improve their chances of making vital saves.

This session encourages your goalkeeper to be more mobile, turn sharply and return to their feet quickly to block a rebound. How will they react?
 
 
Wing it
 
Things don’t always go to plan in the attacking third. Encourage your players to “wing it” by showing them that how they deliver the ball into the box doesn’t matter – as long as it gets there.

The winger must be unpredictable and “mix-up” his game in order to make himself hard to mark.
