Microsoft admits to signing rootkit malware in supply-chain fiasco

Normally you should be able to trust that a driver provided and signed by Microsoft is going to be legitimate—it was one of the major security features during the switch to 64-bit Windows.

In this case, somebody managed to get a rootkit malware driver through the signing process using one of the third-party companies that have access to sign with Microsoft’s keys. The rootkit then started sending data to China.
