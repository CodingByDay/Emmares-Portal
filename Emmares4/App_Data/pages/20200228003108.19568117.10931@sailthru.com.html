Borrowing a page from the gang on "Sesame Street," we're declaring the Letter of the Week to be P.

For starters, there's investor panic about how rapidly the novel coronavirus is crisscrossing the globe. The MSCI All-Country World Index fell to the lowest since October, and the S&P 500 fell from a record into a correction in six days.

Then there's the P-word that must not be named: Pandemic. The World Health Organization won't stick that label on the outbreak while research continues. Still, cities in the U.S. are preparing for infections by setting up war rooms and stockpiling supplies.

Trying to ease the jitters on Wall Street and Main Street, Donald Trump chose his own P — Vice President Mike Pence — to coordinate U.S. government efforts.

And half a world away, Chinese President Xi Jinping is urging cadres to ramp up "publicity efforts" — shorthand for propaganda — to tamp down bubbling anger over the government's initial response.

So, as you wait for the Letter of the Week to be V — as in "vaccine" — here are some stories you may have missed. — Michael Tighe

I Reddit on r/WSB

Next time you're online swapping GIFs in your FortNite: Battle Royale subreddit, you might want to check out what the do-it-yourself traders are saying in r/wallstreetbets. The Reddit message board is reshaping the options market and sparking wild rallies. 

A Brighter Smiley

Walmart's trademark character Smiley may soon flash some whiter teeth. The retail giant is opening health centers to provide basic medical and dental care to compete with CVS and Amazon. And while you're in the dentist's chair, you can still get your tires rotated.

Hot Stuff

If Kevin Costner ever makes a sequel to "Waterworld," Singapore wants to be the stunt double for Dry Land. The island nation is spending $72 billion to safeguard itself against rising sea levels as the prime minister calls climate change a matter of "life and death."

Game Over

It's all fun and games until somebody gets hurt. Or, in the case of HQ Trivia, until managers start feuding and allegations of a toxic work environment emerge. Take a look inside the the downfall of a formerly top-ranked game in Apple's App Store.

Calpers Questions

Is California's $400 billion state pension system run by a Chinese agent? A Republican congressman says Ben Meng, who grew up in China, is a tool for the Communist Party. Calpers is counter-punching with help from some heavy hitters on Wall Street.

What Happens at Canyon Ranch...

Want to know how the super-rich pamper themselves? We spent a week undercover at Canyon Ranch to learn some of their secrets, and at the same time found out about some of their... peculiarities.

Will Cum-Ex Do What Nazis Couldn't?

Germany's Wehrmacht couldn't destroy the Warburg family bank during World War II, but some controversial dividend trades could. Executives are accused of using the transactions, called Cum-Ex, to help swindle the government of almost $11 billion in revenue.   

A Really Highball

Tired of trying to shoehorn your way to the front of a cramped bar on long-haul flights? Some airlines feel your pain, and they're replacing sky bars with swanky, spacious lounges where you can socialize — and even attempt to do some work.
