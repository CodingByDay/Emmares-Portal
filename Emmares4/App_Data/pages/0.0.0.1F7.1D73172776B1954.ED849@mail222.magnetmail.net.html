Strong Start to Earnings Season Sends Dow Higher
 

The Dow was the clear winner among the major indexes today, as Wall Street got its initial taste of 2021's Q1 earnings season. The industrial average gained a modest 0.2% to finish at 33,730 thanks to a record-setting quarter for investment bank Goldman Sachs (GS, +2.3%). "GS is executing on all cylinders with industry-leading performance, and we believe the capital markets will remain very active in a low-rate, risk-on environment," says Kenneth Leon, director of equity research at CFRA. Quarterly results from Wells Fargo (WFC, +5.6%) were also well-received, but JPMorgan Chase (JPM, -1.9%) slumped on disappointing loan revenues. Energy (+2.8%) was a big winner today after data from the Energy Information Administration showed domestic crude oil supplies fell more than expected last week. It wasn't enough to keep the S&P 500 above water, with the broad-market index falling 0.4% to close at 4,124. The Nasdaq declined 1.0% to 13,857.

Also Wednesday, investors enjoyed the latest in a string of high-profile stock-market debuts. Cryptocurrency exchange Coinbase (COIN) kicked off its highly anticipated direct listing by opening at $381.00 – well above the $250 reference price set by the Nasdaq on Tuesday – before closing up 31.3% to $328.28. Coinbase joined a laundry list of outstanding offerings in 2021 that include the likes of online gaming platform Roblox (RBLX), female-led dating-app operator Bumble (BMBL) and popular online education provider Coursera (COUR).
