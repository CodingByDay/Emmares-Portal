Hi there, 

 

No quarantine boo? How about a virtual one? 

 

Therapy chatbot startup Replika says that roughly 40% of its 500,000 monthly users see its app as a romantic partner, according to the Wall Street Journal.

We’ve talked about how chatbots can transform the mental health industry — specifically, in supplementing cognitive behavioral therapy. These bots largely fell short of expectations during their initial hype thanks to their stilted responses, but as companies strive for efficiency via automation and deploy more advanced neural network algorithms, chatbots are now able to generate more empathetic responses than ever. 

 

As a result, more users are seeing them in a more human-like — and even romantic — light. The lack of human contact amid self isolation also helps.


Chatbots have applications beyond mental health — take a look at other industries where they could see surprising success here.
