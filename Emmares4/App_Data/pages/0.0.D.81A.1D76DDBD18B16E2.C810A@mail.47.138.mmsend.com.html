2021 Midyear Market Outlook (Part 2): Inflation is Wall Street’s topic du jour as we head into 2021’s second half, and for good reason. A surge in inflation -- Kiplinger’s forecasts overall inflation will end the year at 5.0% -- doesn’t just reflect a red-hot economy and spikes in demand for many goods and services. It also has ramifications for the market as a whole, given growing pressure on the Federal Reserve to hike short-term interest rates to keep the U.S. economy from overheating. However, Lindsey Bell, chief investment strategist at Ally Invest, suggests inflation is worth monitoring, but not worrying over. “Historically, inflation has dampened stock returns, but the market has still performed moderately well over time as long as the economy was still growing,” she says. Her suggestion for investing in this environment? “It may be wise to look for growth at a reasonable price (GARP) to find investment opportunities,” she says, pointing to materials companies with direct commodity exposure, financial stocks, and small- and mid-cap firms. Granted, record levels for the major indexes mean few stocks are trading on the cheap at present, but we have sniffed out at least a few GARP stocks for investors seeking discount-priced growth.

Perhaps the most remarkable aspect of the economy right now is that consumers continue to spend heavily on goods, even as they also start to splurge on services that they missed out on last year due to the pandemic. More money spent on things like vacations and restaurant meals would normally mean less spending on physical items like refrigerators or new cars. But it turns out that the American consumer wants both goods and services, and has the cash savings to buy both. In fact, spending on durable goods like appliances and automobiles has logged five years’ worth of normal growth over the past year or so. This sort of increase in spending on durable goods hasn’t been seen in the U.S. since 1946.
