Just as we all expected, the first player traded during draft week is ... Rob Gronkowski?! Gronkowski is headed to the Tampa Bay Bucs where he will catch passes from Tom Brady, reuniting a QB/TE combo that has connected for 78 touchdowns. The last time we saw Gronk was 2018, when he ranked TE9 on a per game basis, averaging just 52.5 yards per game. It will be tough to draft Gronk as anything more than a low end TE1 in 2020, but he will likely be drafted above the Noah Fants and Jonnu Smiths of the world, with a chance that his price tag rises in casual leagues. Beyond Gronk's value, this trade has an immense impact on the Bucs offensive weapons. Tom Brady was already a consensus top 10 QB, but his ceiling is now a top 5 QB. The move raises questions about Chris Godwin and Mike Evans, who accounted for nearly 47% of the Bucs targets in 2019. Their stock in down, but how low are the FFT guys on the Bucs WRs?
