TGIF, QuickTake readers! Start your weekend in high spirits with these 10 uplifting stories from the past week.

1. This farm in England is using alpacas to protect its turkeys. Alpacas are said to have a guard-dog like quality and were enlisted by farmers to fend of foxes

2. This experimental "acoustic cluster therapy" can help improve chemotherapy. Doctors are using ultrasound waves to pump more drugs into tumors without attacking nearby healthy cells.

3. Disposable, sterile hijabs were introduced in a U.K. hospital first. Muslim doctor Farah Roslan says it provides a solution to infection control concerns.

4. A biochemist was crowned Miss America. Camille Schrier won the title after she performed a science experiment showing the catalytic decomposition of hydrogen peroxide. 5. A 3D-printed prosthetic that’ll keep amputees active. The Fin is designed to ease transition from dry ground to water so those who wear it can enjoy a swim.

6.  The world’s oldest fossilized trees have been discovered in upstate New York. Scientists say the 386 million-year-old remnants could help fight climate change.7.  A neurotech startup unveiled the world's first brain-sensing wearable. It allows users to control their devices with just their thoughts.

8.  A new food labeling system may reduce calorie intake. A new study found people ate 200 fewer calories when told how much physical activity it would take to burn off.
9. Chewing gum reveals DNA from the Stone Age. Scientists have recovered the entire genome of a Danish girl from her teeth marks on a 5,700-year-old lump of tar.

10. A Christmas tree that stresses aviation security. Knives, scissors, lighters, blades and other confiscated items from travelers were used to create the contraband tree.
