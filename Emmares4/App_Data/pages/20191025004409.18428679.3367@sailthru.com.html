Pork is a constant companion of politics. In the U.S. and elsewhere, "pork" — preferential government spending — usually comes in the form of an expensive bridge or highway. In China, political pork is the kind that's served for dinner. An outbreak of African swine fever has decimated the nation's herds, pummeling farmers and jolting prices. So Beijing has gone on an overseas buying spree and told local officials to make it easier for farmers to restock. If you've been stuck in the long line at the meat counter, here are a few stories you may have missed. — Michael Tighe

The Names to Know

Bloomberg Intelligence analysts track about 2,000 companies so you don't have to, and they've come up with a sortable list of the 50 you should watch next year. Spoiler alert: It's not about the biggest names; it's about the fundamentals.

 

The Great Sag

Synchronized swimming can be fun. A synchronized slowdown across 90% of the global economy? Not so much. The IMF and World Bank met in Washington to discuss potential remedies, and here are the major takeaways.

 

Off Brand

It's the nightmare scenario for American companies: A billion-plus Chinese motivated by national pride decide to "buy local" and ditch brands like Apple, GM and Nike as the trade war drags on. The first inklings of that switch are becoming evident.

 

Playing Hardball

Billionaire money manager Ken Fisher is paying almost a $2 billion price for his vulgar comments at an industry conference. Now some current and former employees describe what it's like to work for him at Fisher Investments.

 

Face It

Hong Kong protesters say they wear face masks and sabotage CCTV cameras because they fear the police can use facial-recognition software to identify them during rallies. Turns out they might be right.

 

Check Please

Got $300 billion and want to save the Earth? Then use it to stop global warming. U.N. scientists say that's the price tag for halting the rise in greenhouse gases and giving humanity a 20-year window to fix up the place. Will that be cash or charge?

 

Shy and Retiring

If you want to keep a low profile, Instagramming selfies with Rihanna probably isn't the best strategy. But 24-year-old Eric Tse hopes to stay out of the limelight after his parents, who run Sino Biopharmaceutical, gifted him shares worth $3.8 billion. Like his superstar pal sings: "I drink to that."

 

6,600% Returns

As Mars Blackmon (aka Spike Lee) once told Michael Jordan: "It's gotta be the shoes!" Millions of Chinese sneakerheads are buying and selling pairs like commodities through online exchanges, with these limited-edition Air Jordans skyrocketing in value.
