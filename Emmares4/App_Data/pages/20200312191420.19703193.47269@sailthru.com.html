Greetings, QuickTake readers! In this edition: The Fed’s injection intervention, one pro-sports cancellation begets another and a trip to the planet where it rains liquid iron.

Trump quarantines Europe

After earlier downplaying the coronavirus threat in the U.S., President Trump on Wednesday evening called for a ban on most travel from Europe for 30 days, starting Friday night. Included in his 10-minute prime-time address were scattered executive actions to protect American workers and businesses rocked by the "foreign virus," he said, casting blame on the EU for failing to restrict travel from China and other hotspots.

What Trump got wrong:

  • "We will be suspending all travel from Europe." Americans or U.K. residents coming from Europe aren't restricted as long as they go through extra screenings. It also only applies to 26 countries, not the entire continent. He didn't explain why the U.K. was exempted.
  • "Insurers agreed to waive co-pays for coronavirus treatments." They agreed to cover coronavirus testing with no co-pays or deductibles but not treatment or tests ordered by a patient's doctor.
  • "Prohibitions will apply to the tremendous amount of trade." The White House quickly clarified, and Trump tweeted, the official proclamation states it "only applies to humans, not goods and cargo.”

On the other side of the Atlantic:

  • European leaders denounced the ban. The European Commission said the pandemic "is a global crisis, not limited to any continent and requires cooperation," adding the EU "disapproves that the decision to impose a travel ban was taken unilaterally, without consultation."

$ignificant figures 

$1.5 trillion. How much the Fed vowed to inject into the financial system to calm panicky markets amid the coronavirus meltdown, announced just before U.S. stocks plunged 10% in the Dow’s worst day since 1987.

37. The number of days the coronavirus pathogen can stay in a patient's respiratory tract, a study found, which suggests people could remain infectious for weeks and reinforces the difficulty of containment.

308,745,538. The population of the U.S. at the time of the 2010 census. The 2020 census, the first to be conducted primarily online, kicked off Thursday amid fears it'd need a deadline extension due to coronavirus.

Highly quotable 

"Not much more than a one-day-at-a-time approach, no?" Tom Hanks said he and wife Rita Wilson tested positive for coronavirus while in Australia and will stay "isolated for as long as public health requires."

"Impracticality of hosting such events." The NCAA called off March Madness over the "evolving Covid-19 public health threat," following moves by the NBA, MLB, NHL and MLS to suspend or postpone play.

"It is a failing, let’s admit it.” Anthony Fauci said the U.S.'s distribution of Covid-19 tests "is not really geared to what we need right now," and said the public health system is "not set up" for people to get easily tested.

This is not normal

Tipping point. The Amazon could become an open savannah in a half-century and Caribbean coral reefs could die off in 15 years, a study of 40 separate ecosystems says, warning of irreversible changes within years.

The future is now

Alien weather. Some 390 light-years away in another solar system exists WASP-76b, an ultra-hot Jupiter-like exoplanet, where scientists detected a near-constant rain of extremely dense, scaldingly hot iron droplets.

What's good

Aww. Scientists discovered the world’s smallest dinosaur in Myanmar, trapped in 99-million-year-old amber, which preserved a bird-like skull that would've been the size of a bee hummingbird, the tiniest living bird.
