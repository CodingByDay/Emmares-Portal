TAMMY'S ALWAYS DYING...AVAILABLE NOW!!!
“Tammy’s Always Dying is a richly observed comedy-drama. Amy Jo Johnson’s direction is intelligent and restrained.” Michael Ordona, The Los Angeles Times

Boasting a bravura turn from Oscar nominee Felicity Huffman (Transamerica) and a rich bounty of cinder-black humour, Tammy’s Always Dying is a bruising tale of a mother-daughter relationship pushed to the brink. Every month, when the welfare money runs out, 35-year-old Catherine (the gifted Anastasia Phillips) has to talk her self-destructive mother Tammy (Felicity Huffman) off the ledge of the same bridge...literally. This routine has gone on for so long that it’s the only thing Cathy feels she’s good at. And it's Tammy’s selfish way of keeping hold of her daughter. When Tammy is diagnosed with terminal cancer and the possibility of life without her becomes very real, so does Cathy’s repressed desire to leave her small town. Does she stay and trust the woman who has always let her down, or does she find the courage to live her own life?

Tammy’s Always Dying is available to stream until June 3, 2020 exclusively through Classic, Lido, Cameo and Ritz Cinemas’ On Demand Streaming Service for $14.99.
TALKING ABOUT TREES...AVAILABLE THURSDAY MAY 28, WITH PRE-SALES AND BOOKINGS OPEN NOW!!!
“With unfailing artistry, director Suhaib Gasmelbari brings much-deserved attention to four Sudanese filmmakers whose unquenchable passion for cinema withstands political repression.” Jay Weissberg, Variety

An official selection of The 2019 Berlin International Film Festival, Talking About Trees – the stunning debut feature from talent-on-the-rise Suhaib Gasmelbari – is a moving, heartfelt documentary that will strike a chord with movie lovers everywhere. Sudanese nationals and veteran filmmakers Ibrahim Shadad, Suleiman Mohamed Ibrahim, Manar Al Hilo and Altayeb Mahdi have been friends for over 45 years. They left their motherland in the 1960s and 1970s to study film abroad and founded The Sudanese Film Group in 1989. After years of distance and exile, they are reunited, hoping to finally make their old dream come true: to bring cinema to Sudan. They are determined to leave something behind and ignite a love for cinema in their homeland. Like a cinema version of the Buena Vista Social Club, the protagonists of Talking About Trees will sweep you away with their charm and passion for their art.   

Talking About Trees is available from May 28 – June 10, 2020, with pre-sales and bookings available now, exclusively through Classic, Lido, Cameo and Ritz Cinemas’ On Demand Streaming Service for $14.99.
