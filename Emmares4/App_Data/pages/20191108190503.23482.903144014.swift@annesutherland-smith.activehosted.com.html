Japan Travel Planning - Planning Calendars
Avoid FOMO!

Reader,

When you are already paying a large amount of money for your dream trip to Japan, you want to ensure that you make the most of your trip.  That includes being aware of major events so you can fine tune your itinerary to ensure that you can attend, or alternatively select the lowest crowd days for popular activities.

We personally hate going on a big trip and after we arrive realise that something was on and we have missed out because we found out about it too late.  FOMO much?

This information is readily available but in multiple locations and only when you know to look for it :)  so it is very easy to miss out on an event you were interested in (for example a sumo wrestling tournament - a very common issue with our readers!), or accidentally find that you planned to visit during a peak time.

As a result we have spent many hours creating detailed planning calendars for Japan which include details of all of the public holidays, Japan school holidays and major events to help you plan your trip. The downloadable PDF documents include clickable links for each event to help you easily find out more information.

What do the Japan Travel Planning Calendars Include?

Each Japan Planning Calendar includes an overview of key events which occur during the period of each calendar, following by monthly tables which provide further detailed information about specific events. Each event has a clickable link so that you can find more information about that particular event.

At the end are blank monthly calendars which have been populated with public holidays, school holidays and other major events of interest to tourists. You can print out the relevant months and use these monthly calendars to plan your trip.

Example of What Each Calendar Contains:

As an example, in the Winter 2019-2020 calendar you can expect to see the following events.  The calendars for the other seasons contain a similar amount of information.

  • Major events including public holidays and Japan school holidays, with New Year's Eve as a major event as well as Chinese New Year at the end of January.
  • Winter weather - temperatures as well as what to expect.
  • Skiing and Snowboarding season information, including peak season details
  • The major festivals including snow, illumination, cultural, fireworks and fire festivals for the most popular tourist destinations. Think Sapporo Snow Festival, Nozawa Fire Festival, Setsuban Bean Throwing Festival, Shirakawago Winter Light Up and many more!
  • The start of the cherry blossom season in Okinawa and the early plum blossom season and early cherry blossom season in mainland Japan.
  • Details for the sumo wrestling tournament being held over Winter 2019-2020.
  • Automobile and Motorcycle events - some of these events are only held every two years! Events over Winter 2019-2020 include the Mooneyes Hot Rod and Custom Car Show, the Osaka Motor Show, Nagoya Motor Show, Fukuoka Motor Show, Tokyo Auto Salon, Auto Messe Show and Nagoya Auto Trend Show.
  • Anime, Manga and Otaku events, including ComicCon, Hokkaido Comita, Jump Festa, Comiket, World Hobby Fair and Wonder Festival.
