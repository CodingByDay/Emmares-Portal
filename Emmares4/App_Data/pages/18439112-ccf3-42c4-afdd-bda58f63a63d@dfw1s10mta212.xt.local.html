This Week

      A note from our Executive Editor, Ann Gadzikowski

I’ve grown so accustomed to spending time at home that going out to a shop or a restaurant feels like a really big deal. For children who have been learning at home, going to school for the first time in over a year (or perhaps the first time ever) must seem like an even bigger deal.

A little separation anxiety is normal for children in ordinary times. Now, because of the stress and isolation caused by the pandemic, mental health experts say that children are much more likely to experience some anxiety about going school. They need support from caring adults who will help them express their feelings, develop coping strategies, and boost their confidence.

Britannica for Parents has resources to help support your family. We’ve created a free downloadable back-to-school activities in both English and Spanish that will help you and your child prepare for a successful transition back to school in the fall.

Visit our feature story to learn more!

Ann
