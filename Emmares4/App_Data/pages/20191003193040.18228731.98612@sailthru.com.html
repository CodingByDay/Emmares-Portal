Microsoft surprised just about everyone this week by showing off not just one but two new dual-screen mobile devices. The pocketable Surface Duo and the tablet-like Surface Neo won't go on sale for at least a year, but Microsoft trotted them out early to signal how it's positioning its future in the mobile landscape.

The smaller Duo is interesting, mostly because it runs Microsoft apps on top of Android, but also because Microsoft is being very careful not to call it a phone. Yes, it makes phone calls and it can take a photo, but the device is less a "Microsoft phone" and more a "Microsoft mobile productivity solution." At least, that's what the company says. Everyone else will just call it a phone, and they will not be incorrect.

There was other Microsoft hardware this week, including the new Surface device with a mobile chip inside, and some wireless Surface-branded earbuds. You can read up on all the Microsoft stuff in two separate stories—one about the Neo and Duo, and one about everything else.

Also this week, a review of the new GoPro, a look at Instagram's messaging app, and a guide to all the best live TV-streaming services.
