To gauge the degree to which the coronavirus is changing life across Asia, look no further than some of the memes being swapped around China and Hong Kong. There are jokes about Valentine's Day bouquets of face masks and gifts of toilet paper following a recent run on tissue and other sanitation products.

If no one has gifted you a heart-shaped box filled with ethyl alcohol, you can cuddle up with these stories instead.  —Tracy Alloway

I'm a Survivor

Tiger Ye at home in Wuhan on Feb. 13 after being discharged from quarantine.

Photographer: Tiger Ye/Tiger Ye

Tiger Ye is a 21-year-old student in Wuhan who contracted the novel coronavirus — and nearly died from COVID-19 — last month. (That's not his real name; he didn't want to be identified for fear of being ostracized.) Here, he describes his harrowing experience dealing with the disease in the epicenter of the outbreak. Across China, fears of the virus mean many factories and businesses remain have shut, even as people were supposed to get back to work after an extended Lunar New Year holiday. And, of course, no one wants to be stuck on the next virus-hit cruise ship.

Handle With Care

 A Chinese man in a shuttered, nearly empty commercial area on Feb. 12 in Beijing, China. 

Photographer: Kevin Frayer/Getty Images AsiaPac

The outbreak of the coronavirus puts a huge question mark over China's growth story, its financial system, and those who have tied their fortunes to it. This week's issue of Bloomberg Businessweek is all about "fragile China," and explores what the epidemic means for its authoritarian government and its debt-fueled financial system, which has so far defied forecasts of a massive collapse.
 

Bad Pizza

A robot places a pizza into an oven at Zume Pizza in Mountain View, Calif.

Photographer: Marcio Jose Sanchez/AP Photo

A robot-made pizza chain called Zume is the most recent business to be added to a growing list of disappointments in SoftBank’s portfolio. As of this year, Zume no longer makes or ­delivers pizzas. Read on for why CEO Masayoshi Son is struggling to make some dough on this particular investment.
 

The 1%

A 1958 Bayerische Motoren Werke at the 2018 Concorso D'Eleganza at Villa d'Este in Cernobbio, Italy.

Photographer: Alberto Bernasconi/Bloomberg

What does it take to be considered part of the 1% around the world? In India, an annual income of $77,000 will get you into the top tier of earners. In the oil-rich United Arab Emirates, however, you'll need to be making almost $1 million. We'll walk you through the wide variations in wealth around the globe.

Melting Ambitions

In this photo released on Oct. 16, 2019, North Korean leader Kim Jong Un rides a white horse to climb Mount Paektu, North Korea.

Photographer: Korean Central News Agency/Korea News Service via AP Photo

At the summit of Taehwa Peak in North Korea is Kim Jong Un's flagship ski resort, Masikryong. North Korea's authoritarian leader has been hoping to build up the country's tourism industry, but his ski-destination ambitions now face two challenges: climate change means the snow on the lower slopes is melting, and the spreading coronavirus has cut off the largest group of tourists to the hermit kingdom.

More Stupider

Colt and Larissa in Season 6 of 90 Day Fiancé. 2018.

Source: TLC

At a time when the Trump administration has been cracking down on immigration into the United States, a reality TV show following the lives of non-U.S. citizens applying for K-1 visas to stay in America with their future spouses has become a cultural phenomenon. Brimming with oddball characters, cultural misunderstandings and off-the-cuff quotes ("Things are about to get a little bit more stupider"), 90 Day Fiancé has become a ratings juggernaut for cable channel TLC.
