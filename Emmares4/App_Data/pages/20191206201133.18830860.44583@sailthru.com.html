TGIF, QuickTake readers! Start your weekend in high spirits with these 10 uplifting stories from the past week. 

1. Pantone wants you to zen out in 2020. The color institute chose Classic Blue as its Color of the Year for its calming, reassuring vibes. 

2. Nobel laureates beamed a live video chat to space. Astronauts on the ISS told future scientists to "stay curious" in the Earth-to-Space call.

3. A once-a-month birth control pill cleared a key hurdle. The unfolding capsule slowly releases hormones in the stomach—and works in animals.

4. Uganda hosted a beauty pageant for HIV+ youth. The Mister and Miss Y+ event happens every year to help end the stigma of HIV/AIDS.

5. Japan's makeup giants are banking on skin wisdom. 99-year-old Kikue Fukuhara has been selling cosmetics to older women for decades.

6. China's pets are living in the lap of luxury. Owners are on track to spend $29 billion this year on lavish meals and toys for their furry friends.

7. Cruise ships are getting pulled apart to make them bigger. Ships get sliced in half to add a new section and then get stitched back together.

8. HIV awareness is a daily ritual for Kenya's "King of Condoms." Stanley Ngara uses humor and empathy to teach safe sex practices. 

9. Ford is making cars out of coffee. The automaker will turn coffee bean waste from McDonald’s into bioplastics and save 25% more energy.

10. Electric eel Miguel Wattson is lighting up Christmas. An in-tank system at the Tennessee Aquarium transmits his shocks to a nearby tree.

Have a great weekend! Watch your inbox for our next newsletter on Monday. Follow QuickTake on Twitter, YouTube, Instagram and Facebook.

Thanks for reading!
-Masiel Torres
