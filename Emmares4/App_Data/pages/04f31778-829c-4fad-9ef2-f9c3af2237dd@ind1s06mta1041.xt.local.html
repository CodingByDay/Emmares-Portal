An all-embracing theory of physics that unifies quantum mechanics and general relativity would describe everything in the universe from subatomic particles to the big bang. This theory of everything is the greatest quest in all of physics.

In this talk, Michio Kaku explains the dramatic progress made when theorists succeed in uniting the seemingly incompatible. He explores some of the questions that the pursuit for a theory of everything has provoked. Could there be many more dimensions of space-time than meets the eye? Is our universe just one in a multiverse of possibilities? And what are recent cracks in the standard model really telling us?
