This Week

      A note from our Executive Editor, Ann Gadzikowski

I’m pretty cautious about spending money on electronics, so I was surprised to discover how much I enjoy having a smart speaker in my home. As part of my morning routine I always ask Siri for the weather report as I’m putting on my shoes to walk my dog. And I really appreciate the convenience of using voice commands to adjust the volume when I’m listening to music.

But not every electronics purchase is a guaranteed win, especially when shopping for gadgets that will be used by children. That’s why Britannica for Parents provides resources to help families make tech-savvy decisions about which devices, apps, and games they bring into their home. 

Our featured article this week, “Choosing Tech Gadgets for Your Family,” will help you make smart purchasing decisions up front. Our library of resources also includes guidance on how and when to introduce your child to different kinds of tech tools.

Do you have any recommendations for gadgets for children? Send your ideas to parents@eb.com. Your responses might be shared here next week.

Last week we asked you for baby-centric tips for new parents. Rosalinda in New Zealand replied, “My best tip for parents of infants is to relax and put all else aside. There's a natural harmony that's meant to exist between parents and babies. Peace and joy must be sought after, guarded, taking precedence over all else from moment to moment.” 

Thank you, Rosalinda!

Ann
