There's a decidedly Aussie flavour to this week's cinema releases, with the blood-soaked horror comedy Two Heads Creek, the coming of age comedy drama Bilched, the highly original Judy & Punch, and the Aussie funded and directed doco Suzi Q. THE LATEST
Jesse O'Brien Goes Way, Way Out With Two Heads Creek
With nearly as much blood as the elevator in The Shining and more meat than the local butcher has on stock, director Jesse O'Brien (Arrowhead) gets the gore and guts going for his utterly loopy take on the modern horror genre with Two Heads Creek.

Adewale Akinnuoye-Agbaje: Farming 

Busy actor and law grad Adewale Akinnuoye-Agbaje (TV's Oz) mines his own difficult childhood for his confronting directorial debut Farming. 
Making Welcome To Woop Woop
Compromised in post-production and maligned upon release, Stephan Elliott's Welcome To Woop Woop is now a bizarre curio with a passionate cult.

Australia's Best Street Racer

Veteran exec producer Nathan Earl takes us through the unusual process that led to the eight-part web series Australia's Best Street Racer.
Helen Barlow: Doing Jury Duty
Veteran Australian film journalist Helen Barlow recounts her experiences serving on the juries at the Zurich and Stockholm Film Festivals.

Mariana Di Girolamo: Ema

Chilean actress Mariana Di Girolamo headlines the fascinating Ema, the latest cinematic eye opener from director Pablo Larrain (No, Jackie).
The Films Of Errol Flynn: Part 3
In this third part of an ongoing series, we look at the key works of one of Australia's first - and most divisive - international movie stars.

Mirrah Foulkes: Judy & Punch

Australian actress Mirrah Foulkes turns director with Judy & Punch, a highly idiosyncratic take on the iconic batter-and-bash puppet show.
Scott Z. Burns: The Report
Regular Hollywood screenwriter Scott Z. Burns delivers his second directorial effort with the powerful political drama The Report.

Abe Forsythe Joins Robocop Returns

The talented Aussie up and comer behind Little Monsters, Ned and Down Under will be suiting up for the big budget sequel, Robocop Returns.
Nathan Dalton On Aussie Film
The Quanta writer/director calls for local filmmakers to recognise their rightful place in the international community and value their  contribution to cinema.
