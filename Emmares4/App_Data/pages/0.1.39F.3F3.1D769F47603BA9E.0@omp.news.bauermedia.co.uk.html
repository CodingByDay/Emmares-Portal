GTB and GTS: Ferrari's hybrid, Porsche's blend
Ferrari has released the long-awaited mid-engined 296 GTB Hybrid so often rumoured as a modern Dino. Though the reality draws upon many classic cues, it's not a lithe lightweight - it makes over 800bhp. Porsche, however, has added more flexibility to the 992 with the new 911 GTS - Turbo suspension, Carrera power, and a few extra tweaks - is this the best 911 yet?

Dacia's been polishing too, giving the Duster a new look and new tech for 2021; it's as aspirational as the new Honda Civic is anonymous. Scroll down for more, including a first drive of the all-new Mercedes-Benz C-Class and the jacked-up Yaris Cross, a glimpse at the next Peugeot 308 SW, and an old BMW 1-series that's offering remarkably cheap thrills. Would you rather have that than the next Audi RS3?

Porsche 911 GTS: balancing act
It's remarkable how Porsche's truly iconic 911 has become a single entity, yet for decades has had diversity and focus sufficient to create whole ranges. It seems, within reason, there is a 911 for every taste and budget, and for the 992 series arguably the most focused 911, the GTS, has joined the range.
