Here's a reminder for people who are self-employed or don't have taxes withheld from other sources of taxable income (e.g., interest, dividends, or capital gains): Second quarter estimated tax payments are due tomorrow. Estimated taxes are typically paid in four equal installments — generally, one installment for each quarter of the year. The first estimated tax payment for the 2021 tax year was due on April 15. The second payment for 2021, which is for income earned from April 1 to May 31, is due on June 15. Your state may have an estimated tax due date tomorrow, too.

There are several ways to pay estimated taxes, including by check, cash, money order, credit card and debit card. File IRS Form 1040-ES if you're making an estimated tax payment by check or money order (write your Social Security number and "2021 Form 1040-ES" on it). There are many online payment options as well, such as by using the Electronic Federal Tax Payment System at eftps.gov. The various payment methods are described in the instructions for Form 1040-ES. You might be hit with a penalty if you don't pay enough estimated tax during the year. However, the penalty doesn't apply if you owe less than $1,000 in tax. You can also avoid the penalty if your 2021 estimated tax payments (plus any withheld federal income tax) equal at least 90% of your 2021 tax liability, or 100% of the tax shown on your 2020 return (110% if your 2020 adjusted gross income was more than $150,000).

Novavax’s COVID-19 vaccine proved to be 90% effective in its final Phase 3 trial. That puts its two-dose vaccine almost on par with those developed by Pfizer and Moderna. While too late for U.S. vaccination, it is expected to be important for world vaccination programs, especially since Novavax’s doses only require regular refrigeration. Novavax’s manufacturing capacity will take a couple months to come online. But if given FDA approval, it expects its production rate to be at 100 million doses a month by September, and 150 million doses each month by the end of the year.
