May 20, 2021

Dear Reader,

This week, we're celebrating another arrival at the Red Planet. Although China's Tianwen-1 mission has been orbiting Mars since February, its Zhurong rover did not land until late last week, on May 14, touching down in the sandy wastes of the Utopia Planitia region in the world's northern hemisphere. Any interplanetary landfall is remarkable, but this one is all the more so because it is China's first—making it after the U.S. only the second nation ever to successfully land and operate a spacecraft on Mars. Elsewhere, we have stories about the possibility of traversable wormholes, new frontiers in dark-matter detection experiments, and the astrobiological implications of humanity's "dataome." Read on, and enjoy!
