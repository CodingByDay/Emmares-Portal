Greetings, QuickTake readers! In this edition: The EU’s €1-trillion fight, Bernie’s big reason to woo California, and the first AI-made antibiotic.

A new line of attack

Scientists have produced the first 3D map of the coronavirus in a "critical breakthrough" toward developing a vaccine and antiviral drugs. The rendering, created by a team of researchers at the University of Texas at Austin and the National Institutes of Health, specifically assembles the molecular structure of the so-called "spike protein" that clings to human cells and causes infection. It's a key step that gives researchers around the world a new entry point in the race to add to our available weapons to fight the virus. Globally, coronavirus has infected at least 76,185 people and killed at least 2,244 others.

$ignificant figures

€1 trillion. The size of the EU’s budget plan under deliberation in Brussels at the body’s first high-level meeting since Brexit with member states at odds over how to fill the €75-billion budget hole left by Britain.

45%. The success rate of this year's flu vaccine, according to the CDC, which said it prevented many of the most severe infections in children during the longer-than-normal 2019-2020 flu season.

24%. The share of likely voters in California’s March 3 primary who back Bernie Sanders, setting him up to walk away with up to half of the state's delegates on Super Tuesday. Joe Biden followed at 17%.

Highly quotable

"Racism is poison." Angela Merkel condemned the shootings in the German city of Hanau that left 11 people dead, including the suspected gunman, and said: "Everything will be done to investigate these murders."

"Good chance of exoneration." Trump said he won't pardon Roger Stone, who got 40 months in jail for lying to Congress and witness tampering, but indicated he'd intervene if the conviction isn't overturned.

"Attempt to undermine sovereignty." U.S. and U.K. officials blamed Russia’s military intelligence agency for a cyberattack in the country of Georgia last year that disrupted thousands of websites and TV broadcasts.

This is not normal

Dire prediction. Deforestation of the Amazon rainforest will accelerate at a "catastrophic" pace this year, a Brazilian paper company CEO said, after the annual rate of destruction jumped the most in over a decade this year.

The future is now

Another AI-first. Using a new algorithm, MIT researchers discovered a powerful antibiotic called halicin that wiped out a wide range of the world's most problematic antibiotic-resistant strains of bacteria, including E. coli.

What's good

VIP praise. "Parasite" director Bong Joon-ho and his cast met South Korean President Moon Jae-in to celebrate their Oscar win, which is now a “great pride of our people,” amid rising coronavirus fears, Moon said.
