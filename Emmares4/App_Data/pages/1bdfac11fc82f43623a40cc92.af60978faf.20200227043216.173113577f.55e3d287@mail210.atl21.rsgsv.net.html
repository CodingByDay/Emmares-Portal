We'd like to interrupt the celebrations around the conviction of serial grub Harvey Weinstein (throw away the key...) to fill you in on this week's cinema releases. In the tradition of Police Rescue: The Movie, an Aussie TV favourite heads to the big screen in Miss Fisher And The Crypt Of Tears; Saw co-creator and all-round good guy Leigh Whannell crafts a superb slice of contemporary horror with The Invisible Man; Shia LaBeouf mines his own life story for Honey Boy; Daniel Radcliffe shoots it up with Guns Akimbo; the scares are plentiful in Aiyai: Wrathful Soul; and Edward Norton plays an idiosyncratic gumshoe in Motherless Brooklyn. See you at the movies!

THE LATEST
Edward Norton Adopts Motherless Brooklyn
After being stuck in development hell for nearly twenty years, director/star Edward Norton has finally brought the unconventional 1950's detective yarn Motherless Brooklyn to the screen, marking his belated follow-up to his 2000 directorial debut, Keeping The Faith.

High Ground Hits At Berlin Film Fest

The indigenous themed Aussie drama High Ground, starring Simon Baker and Witiyana Marika, has made an auspicious debut at The Berlin Film Fest.
Roger Ward Looks Back On The Set
The legendary Aussie actor - best known for Mad Max and Turkey Shoot - looks back on the production of his screenwriting debut, The Set.

No Friend But The Mountains On Film

The acclaimed book No Friend But The Mountains - written by Behrouz Boochani while detained on Manus Island - has been optioned for film.
Kirk Douglas Aussie Connections
The much loved smash hit Aussie classic The Man From Snowy River wasn't the only connection that the late, great Kirk Douglas had to Australia.

Gallic Hero Guillaume Canet

A true multi-tasker, writer, actor, producer and director Guillaume Canet has three films playing at the upcoming Alliance Francaise French Film Fest.
Peter Renzullo: Skylight 
Despite being legally blind, Peter Renzullo is making his mark on the film scene, just releasing the trailer for his second feature, Skylight - Caroline's Tale.
