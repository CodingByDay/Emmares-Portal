What you’ll want to read tonight

What Will Hong Kong Look Like in 2047?

What happens on July 1, 2047, when the "one country, two systems" agreement expires in Hong Kong? That question has fueled the dramatic protests that have raged for more than six months, turning one of Asia’s premier financial hubs into a battleground featuring tear gas volleys, petrol bombs, vandalism, road blocks and subway shutdowns. All of the unrest is really about Hong Kong’s future: Can the city retain its freedom of the press and assembly, its English common law legal system, its low tax rate and open markets? Up to now, this is what’s kept Hong Kong distinct from China. Whether it can stay that way is a bellwether for China’s rise—and how it will co-exist with the West.
