The big story

I’m not in the habit or practice of writing book reviews, especially for this newsletter, so consider this an abbreviated elementary school book report. This week, I wrapped up the last pages of “Uncanny Valley,” a memoir of author Anna Wiener’s experience moving to San Francisco as a non-technical startup employee. It debuted a couple of weeks ago, telling the very much unremarkable story of a 20-something tech worker weaving through startup life to varying degrees of success. 

If you can get past the ceaseless Brooklyn hipster versus Mission hipster verbiage of the book’s first quarter, you’re rewarded with a very focused portrait of an industry and city upheld by advertising and obsessed with reverse-engineering truths from its wealth.

If you’re a regular reader of TechCrunch, chances are decent that you like direct-to-consumer startups and productivity apps and that you might even ascribe hold the advice of wealthy investors on Twitter in high regard. You also likely have very distinct ideas about topics like 80-hour work weeks, diversity & inclusion, and meritocracy depending on which corners of the web you spend your time.

This book won’t drench you in history or perspective on any topic, but it will lure you into acknowledging how little you think about Silicon Valley’s greater context while also permitting you to hear the workplace chatter of tech workers you will have varying levels of empathy for.

The book finds deeper meaning in its last couple dozen pages. A new president is elected and some in the industry take a longer glance–for the first time–at the scales weighing what’s been accomplished and the things we’ve deemed expendable.

Trends of the week

Here are a few big news items from big companies, with green links to all the sweet, sweet added context:

  • Bezos and Saudi Arabia
    This saga appears to be far from fully-reported out, but the facts are that this week the United Nations called for an investigation into allegations that Saudi officials were behind the phone-hacking of Amazon founder Jeff Bezos.
  • Wikipedia reaches new milestone
    This week, Wikipedia announced that its six millionth English language article was published onto the web.
  • Netflix says “no” to ads
    The crowded presence of so many major platforms in the video streaming space seems poised to rewrite the space’s rules, but for now, investor prodding hasn’t seemed to shift Netflix’s position that it should be an ad-free platform.
