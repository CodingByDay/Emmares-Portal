What do Lil Nas X, Greta Thunberg and Jacinda Ardern have in common? They’re all brand new icons under the age of 40. The “Bloomberg 50” roundup of the most influential people of 2019 shows that it was a year of unexpected explosions of fame, and not just for the rapper, the climate activist and the Kiwi prime minister.

The contradictions of this moment in history go far beyond a chart-defying rap-country mashup. Even as Beyond Meat’s faux burgers and sausage won fans at Burger King and Dunkin’, Popeyes was rolling out a (real) chicken sandwich so hyped for so long that a customer lost his life trying to get one. And while Thunberg’s campaign held the attention of the United Nations General Assembly, millions of young protesters in Hong Kong still haven’t managed to shake Beijing’s grip on the embattled city.

If these 12 months have taught us anything, it’s to look beyond the usual suspects. And next year probably won’t be any different: Bloomberg Businessweek highlights the 17 names to watch in 2020. —Philip Gray

Death by distracted driver 

More than 3,000 people die in America annually because drivers are distracted. Glancing at a smartphone for just seconds can be fatal. Moreover, the danger is stubbornly resistant to legislative efforts. New data from tracking technology indicates drivers are still distracted despite increased awareness and long-standing laws. Advocates say it’s predictable and preventable—but overlooked because it's so common.
