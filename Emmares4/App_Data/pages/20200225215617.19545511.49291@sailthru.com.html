Greetings, QuickTake readers! In this edition: Accusers elated over Weinstein verdict, states gang up on Juul, and the super-sized solar farms taking over the world.

Prepare for virus to spread: CDC

Americans should prepare for the spread of coronavirus in communities across the country, top U.S. public health officials said Tuesday, warning a potential outbreak could cause "severe disruption to everyday life." "It's not so much a question of if this will happen anymore, but rather more a question of exactly when this will happen," said Dr. Nancy Messonnier, the head of the CDC's National Center for Immunization and Respiratory Diseases. At least 57 cases have been reported in the U.S. with more than 80,000 cases globally. Stocks slumped again on Wall Street Tuesday amid fears the outbreak will put the brakes on the global economy.

Earlier, Trump minimized the virus's threat on the last day of his India state visit, and HHS Secretary Alex Azar said it posed a "low immediate risk" to Americans but admitted in Senate testimony the U.S. was short on protective masks, ventilators and other supplies.

Elsewhere, doctors at the University of Nebraska Medical Center in Omaha announced the U.S.'s first clinical drug trial to treat COVID-19 that will test roughly 400 patients at 50 sites globally.

Around the world:

  • In Japan, government officials called on citizens to work remotely to prevent the spread of the virus and urged people with mild illnesses to see family doctors rather than hospitals with virus-control facilities.
  • In Iran, the head of the government's coronavirus task force, who had earlier played down virus fears, tested positive for the virus.
  • In Geneva, WHO officials praised China's "drastic measures," including aggressive lockdowns and the building of new hospitals, that "changed the course of the outbreak faster than expected."
  • In Italy, officials reported a 45% one-day increase in new infections and Venice tourism took a hit as authorities canceled the last two days of its iconic Carnival. Paramount also halted the production of "Mission: Impossible 7" that had been scheduled to shoot in the city.
  • In the Canary Islands, 1,000 tourists were quarantined at a hotel after an Italian doctor staying there tested positive.

$ignificant figures

39. The number of states investigating the sales and marketing practices of Juul as well as the safety and effectiveness of its products as smoking cessation devices. 

112. The age of Chitetsu Watanabe of Japan, the world's oldest man, who died Sunday, almost two weeks after he was certified by Guinness World Records.

Highly quotable

"Seismic shift in our culture." Weinstein accuser and actress Rosanna Arquette said she "burst into tears" when the disgraced mogul was convicted, which "shows people that you can't do this anymore," she said.

"Russia is backing him." Without offering evidence, Trump accused Democrats who don't want Bernie Sanders to win the party's nomination of leaking information on Russian efforts to help his campaign.

"The only friend I’d skip school for." What Malala Yousafzai said after meeting activist Greta Thunberg, who visited Oxford University where the Nobel laureate studies politics, philosophy and economics.

This is not normal

Toxic air. The biggest share of the world's dirtiest air is in India, says a new report, which found the country is home to 14 of the top 20 most polluted cities, due to crowded urban areas, fossil fuels and emissions.

The future is now

Surging solar. The Benban solar park, made of up 600,000,000 panels covering 15 square miles of the Egyptian desert, is one of the world's largest renewable energy farms capable of powering over 1 million homes.

What's good

Breakthrough. In a "groundbreaking procedure," two cheetah cubs were born via IVF with a surrogate cheetah mom for the first time in history, giving zoologists new hope for other endangered species.
