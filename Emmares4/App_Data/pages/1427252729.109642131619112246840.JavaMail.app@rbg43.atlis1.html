April 22, 2021

Dear Reader,

After years of preparation and weeks of delay, on Monday NASA’s Ingenuity helicopter took to the skies on Mars. Its flight—the first-ever of its kind on another planet—was a mere 30-second hover a few meters off the ground, but portends a not-too-distant future in which otherworldly aircraft become an integral part of humankind’s interplanetary exploration. Ingenuity’s second flight occurred just this morning, and up to three more are planned. Elsewhere this week, we have stories on potential quantum-based breakthroughs in telescope designs, a new module for China’s ambitious space station, and the successful production of breathable air from Mars’s thin atmosphere. 

Lee Billings, Senior Editor, Space & Physics
