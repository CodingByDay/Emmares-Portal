Dow Edges Higher as Jobless Claims Keep Sliding
 

Fresh data out Thursday continued to paint a rosy picture of the American economy's resurgence, but it prompted little response on Wall Street, where a modest morning rally became even more meager by the close. Weekly jobless claims declined yet again, with filings for the week ended May 22 lower by 38,000 claims to 406,000, easily coming in under estimates for 425,000. Meanwhile, the Bureau of Economic Analysis' second estimate of first-quarter U.S. GDP growth came in unchanged at a robust 6.4%. But while the small-cap Russell 2000 enjoyed another boisterous day of gains, advancing 1.1% to 2,273, the blue-chip indexes barely budged. The Dow climbed 0.4% to 34,464, the S&P 500 eked out a 0.1% improvement to 4,200, and the Nasdaq recorded a marginal loss to 13,736. "Higher GDP numbers typically move stock markets and bond yields higher ... but today's GDP estimate was largely expected," says Chris Zaccarelli, chief investment officer for registered investment advisor Independent Advisor Alliance.
