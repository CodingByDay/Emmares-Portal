Senate battles over trial rules

Donald Trump’s impeachment trial began in earnest Tuesday, making him the third president in U.S. history to face possible removal from office. A partisan fight promptly broke out in the Senate chambers, with the House impeachment managers and Trump's legal team debating the trial rules.

Here's what you need to know:

  • McConnell tweaked rules on trial length. In last-minute changes to the trial resolution, he gave each side 24 hours spread over three days, rather than two, to make their cases, which could still allow the proceedings to wrap up within days.
  • Schiff chided GOP's witness refusal. The House Intelligence Committee chairman insisted a trial that "deprives the Senate of all relevant information" would be "a cover-up" and said "the truth will come out. The question is, will it come out in time?"
  • Trump's legal team argued his case. Both his personal lawyer and White House counsel Pat Cipollone cast doubt on the Democratic charges, adding "the only conclusion will be that the president has done absolutely nothing wrong.”
  • Cipollone was deemed a "material witness." A letter sent from the House said "evidence indicates" he has "detailed knowledge" of Trump's alleged pressuring of Ukraine and that he now faces a possible "disqualification" from defending Trump.
  • Schumer proposed trial amendments. The Senate Minority Leader introduced an amendment to subpoena “all documents, communications and other records” relating to Ukraine, which was voted down 53-47 on a straight party-line vote.

$ignificant figures

291. The number of confirmed cases of China’s mysterious respiratory virus in an outbreak that's killed six people and spread to five countries, including the U.S.  

3,737. How many metric tons of plastic waste Malaysia has sent back to 13 rich countries, including France, the U.K. and U.S., saying it'll no longer be the “rubbish dump” of the world.

473 million. The number of "underutilized" people in the world's labor market, a new report says, because they work fewer paid hours than they'd like or lack adequate access to paid work.

This is not normal

Has spring sprung? Mild winter temps in the southern U.S. are causing tree leaves to appear 22 days sooner than usual. And if the warmth goes north, it could mean an earlier allergy season and smaller harvests.

The future is now

Double the arms. Coming soon to ORs in the U.K. is Versius, the world's smallest surgical robot, with four flexible, rotating arms that give doctors more dexterity, which may reduce pain and recovery time among patients.

What's good

Finally free. After years of abuse in the Guatemalan circus industry, 12 tigers and five lions have been relocated to a sanctuary in South Africa, home to 200 other animals that have been rescued since 2018.
