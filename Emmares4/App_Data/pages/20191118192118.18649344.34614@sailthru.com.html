Hong Kong campus under siege

A fiery standoff at Hong Kong Polytechnic University culminated before dawn Monday with police storming barricaded entrances and protesters setting them ablaze. About 600 people were trapped on the campus, from which protesters, holed up for days, hurled gasoline bombs and arrows at surrounding riot police. Some who escaped were met with tear gas and rubber bullets and arrested by police. By evening, tens of thousands of demonstrators marched toward PolyU, forming human chains to pass materials to aid those stuck inside.

More:

  • Plainclothed soldiers from the People’s Liberation Army cleaned up debris-filled roads, causing alarm over potential military intervention.
  • A Hong Kong court found last month's controversial mask ban unconstitutional and police said they'd temporarily stop enforcing it.
  • Senate Majority Leader Mitch McConnell urged Trump to speak out on behalf of the "brave" protesters.

$ignificant figures

$1.7 trillion. Saudia Arabia put that valuation on oil giant Aramco, the world's most profitable company, well below the $2 trillion target saught by Saudi officials.

1.2 billion. The value of Kylie Jenner's cosmetics line after makeup and fragrance giant Coty agreed to pay $600 million for a majority stake in the business. 

$1 billion. Ex-Microsoft CEO Steve Ballmer is battling fellow billionaire James Dolan over a high-priced arena for the L.A. Clippers down the street from Dolan's concert hall. 

This is not normal

Alarming spike. Deforestation in the Amazon hit the highest level since 2008, Brazil's space agency said, with about 6,200 sq. miles of rainforest cut down in the last year. It concluded this summer's raging fires were not "normal," and increased deforestation likely contributed.

The future is now

Desert oasis. Dubai is pushing for a green revolution with Sustainable City, a $350 million, car-free, solar-powered settlement with 500 villas, a shopping plaza, a mosque, a school, and a farm, to be finished next year.

What's good

Bye-bye, Bei Bei. The Smithsonian National Zoo in D.C. is bidding farewell to the beloved giant panda on temporary loan from the Chinese government—and the four-year-old bear is traveling in style aboard a private Boeing 777F with 66 lbs. of bamboo, snacks and water.
