TGIF, QuickTake readers! Start your weekend in high spirits with these 10 uplifting stories from the past week.

1. Goldman Sachs made a diversity ultimatum. Wall Street's biggest underwriter will refuse IPOs with only straight white men on their boards.

2. China will ban single-use plastics in 2020. Plastic bags and staws will be prohibited in supermarkets and shopping malls across all major cities.

3. Greece elected its first female president. Katerina Sakellaropoulou received an overwhelming majority of votes from its parliament.

4. Babies received the world’s first malaria vaccine. Infants in three African nations received the vaccine that took three decades to develop.

5. Robots are coming to ORs in the U.K. They'll help doctors perform minimally invasive operations by giving them four extra arms.

6. Women are the majority of the U.S. labor force. Over the past year alone, they've also taken on more positions traditionally held by men.

7. Abused circus animals have been rescued. Twelve tigers and five lions have been relocated to a sanctuary after a lifetime of deprivation. 

8. Astronauts made the first space-baked cookies. The treat took 2 hours to bake in an experimental oven after several attempts.

9. The Philippines is making roads and cement with plastic garbage. The country is embarking on a $157 billion infrastructure push.

10. This Star Wars fan received an R2-D2 inspired bionic arm. The 3D-printed prosthetic detects muscle movements and lifts up to 17.64 lbs.

Have a great weekend! Watch your inbox for our next newsletter on Monday. Follow us on Twitter, YouTube, Instagram, and Facebook.

Thanks for reading!
