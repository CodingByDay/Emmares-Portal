Happy Thursday! It’s April 22, which is Earth Day—so here’s some interesting trivia about that. Earth Day was created in 1970 by Senator Gaylord Nelson of Wisconsin, which makes today’s Earth Day the 51st one celebrated.

But we probably wouldn’t be celebrating Earth Day (at least not half a century worth of them) without the 1962 book Silent Spring by Rachel Carson. The book, about the environment in general but particularly about the impact of pesticides, was a runaway hit that got Americans and people around the world talking about our collective impact on the world around us.
