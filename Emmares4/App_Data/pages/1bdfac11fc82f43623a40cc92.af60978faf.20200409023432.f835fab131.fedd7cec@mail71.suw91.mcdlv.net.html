...Welcome To The Week In Movies!!!
Australia’s Premier Online Movie Magazine

As we all knuckle down in partial lockdown for the public holiday weekend (anyone been arrested for getting a cup of coffee yet?), we've got reviews of the latest titles to be express released to digital platforms (including 1917, Miss Fisher And The Crypt Of Tears, Astronaut and The Hunt), as well as some entertaining reading: we highlight shows about show business; we've got a list of movies about our older population; we take a look back at the making of Tracks; celebrate the career of Patricia Arquette; and use Once Upon A Time In Hollywood as a jumping off point for two feature stories. Stay safe, have a happy (housebound) Easter, and (figuratively if not literally) see you at the movies!

THE LATEST
To Stream Or Not To Stream: Showbiz Stories
We're all streaming to the max right now, huh? And with no cinemas and zero new movie developments, we take a look at a bunch of series and films currently streaming that look at the wonderful - and not so wonderful - world of entertainment.

Senior Moments: Old Folks Movies

Our older friends are doing it very, very tough right now, so here's a bunch of flicks to remind us of just how much we love 'em and need 'em.
Adam La Rosa: Tapping The Rage
La Rosa Productions main man Adam La Rosa fills us in on the progress of his production company's ambitious just completed crime thriller, Rage.

Alec Baldwin Set For Aussie Debut

In a fascinating move, an Aussie production company has snagged Alec Baldwin for an online live read of Lyle Lessler's play, Orphans.
King Gizzard Set To Rule Online
Melbourne cult rock band King Gizzard & The Lizard Wizard have shifted the premiere of their new doco to the online world, with a bow set for next Friday.

Patricia Arquette: Screen Icon

The against-the-grain Oscar winner has found a second wind in her career with the amazing TV series The Act and Escape At Dannemora. 
The Making Of Tracks
Based on Robyn Davidson's much loved book, the visually stunning Tracks follows one woman's journey alone through the heart of Australia. 

1960s Film Stars Called George

There's a great joke about movie stars named George in Once Upon A Time In Hollywood, and here's the perfect feature story to match.
What If Rick Dalton Came To Oz?
In this hypothetical, we muse on what Rick Dalton might have done if he'd come to Australia after the events of Once Upon A Time In Hollywood.
