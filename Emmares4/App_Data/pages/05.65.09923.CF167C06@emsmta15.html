Robert asks: I’ve read that even if we were able to travel at the speed of light it would still be too slow to reach even the closest star. Are wormholes the only solution to travel faster than light?

Wormholes! Now we're talking. The idea of wormholes is theoretically possible based on general relativity -- a mathematical theory -- but we can't travel through them. The same theory says nothing can travel faster than the speed of light. But on wormholes: we don't know if they actually exist. If they do, some theories say we could jump into a wormhole but it would take infinitely long to actually travel through them, in which case we'd die before we ever came out the other side. Space and time is confusing. What about if we made a wormhole though? Is that possible? I think the best explanation of this comes from one of my favourite videos on the subject: Kurzgesagst's Wormholes Explained. Check that out and you'll know everything you need to know about wormhole travel. I should have an interview with the team behind Kurzgesagt coming soon, so perhaps I could ask them about wormholes when that happens. Thanks a lot for the question, Robert.
