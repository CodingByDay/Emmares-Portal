TGIF, TicToc readers! Start your weekend in high spirits with these 10 uplifting stories from the past week. 

1. Benefits to the rat race. Not only did scientists teach a group of rats how to drive tiny cars, but they also found it reduced the rodents’ stress levels, which may help advance studies on the human brain. 

2. There’s new hope for patients with Alzheimer’s. Drug company Biogen is pursuing FDA approval for an experimental treatment that’s been shown to slow cognitive decline in patients with early Alzheimer’s disease.   

3. Central Park is getting its 1st statue dedicated to women. It’ll feature 14-foot bronze likenesses of suffragettes Susan B. Anthony, Elizabeth Cady Stanton, and Sojourner Truth.

4. Activists take a “Baby Shark” break. Anti-government protests in Lebanon took a lighter turn when demonstrators spontaneously sang “Baby Shark” to a toddler after his mom told them he was scared.

5. Zooming in on tiny worlds. The top shot of the Small World microphotography contest depicts a fluorescent turtle embryo and is made up of hundreds of images stitched together to show details invisible to the naked eye. 

6. An oasis for refugees? Scotland has taken in more refugees per capita from Syria than anywhere else in the U.K. in the past 4 years. And they reported feeling happier with 81% planning on staying. 

7. Listen to the world's loudest bird. The white bellbird, native to the northeastern Amazon, is the loudest bird on record with mating calls that can reach up to 125 decibels, louder than an industrial drill. 

8. A beloved comic unveiled its first female heroine. Meet Adrenaline, the redheaded teen who made her first appearance in “Asterix and the Chieftain’s Daughter” this week.        

9. Google reaches “quantum supremacy.” The company announced it built a computer that can perform computations in seconds that would otherwise take 10,000 years.

10. Mummy Mania. Egyptian authorities unveiled 30 sealed ancient coffins in one of its most significant findings of the century. The country hopes the publicity will revive its tourism industry.
Have a great weekend! Watch your inbox for our next newsletter Monday, and make sure to follow us on Twitter, YouTube, Instagram and Facebook.

Thanks for reading!
-Masiel Torres
