I have questions for you

Hola, 

 

Although Warren Buffett has been investing in the insurance industry for decades, there’s a growing part of it he’s described as too complex and opaque.

Yet, as our client-only research shows, “cyber risk” insurance policies written to protect businesses against losses from data breaches and cyber attacks  have grown into a $3.6B market.

 

Is Buffett being too cautious about cyber? Is this the 1% of today’s insurance market that will in a few years become much larger and potentially extremely profitable to boot? 

 

Emerging product categories such as cyber risk are coming along more frequently as the effects of software and connectivity ripple into mature industries.

Our research will continue to dig into this.

To counter Buffett's provocation: who does know what they’re doing in cyber? Will the Fortune 500 and beyond adopt cyber-risk insurance? Will insurance giants and new entrants find a competitive edge through cyber-risk product strategies?
