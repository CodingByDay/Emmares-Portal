A NOTE FROM:

The brightest stars in the film & TV music universe assembled for the 2019 Variety Music for Screens Summit in Hollywood this past week. The American Society of Composers, Authors and Publishers (ASCAP) was a premier partner.
 
Robbie Robertson

The Summit covered a tremendous amount of ground throughout the day, delving into topics from sonic branding to music supervision and synch trends to kids’ music.

ASCAP legend Robbie Robertson spoke eloquently about the connections between his music with The Band and his music for film, including Martin Scorsese’s upcoming The Irishman; director and ASCAP member Cameron Crowe explained the process of adapting Almost Famous for the musical stage. Elsewhere, songwriting legend Diane Warren (“Till It Happens to You,” “I’ll Fight”) and Savan Kotecha (“Love Me Like You Do”) explained how they approach writing songs for films.
 
Marni Condro, Diane Warren, Ray Costa, Savan Kotecha, Wendy Goldstein

High Scorers: The Composer Roundtable presented by ASCAP brought six screen music A-listers together for a wide-ranging discussion on the creative joys and business challenges facing composers today. Michael Abels (Get Out, Us) discussed his unique methods for freaking out an audience, two days before Halloween.

The impact of the streaming revolution was a big focus, too. Siddhartha Khosla (This Is Us, Looking for Alaska) and Amie Doherty (Undone, Star Trek: Discovery) cited the extra time they have to perfect their scores as a major advantage for streaming projects. Nicholas Britell (Succession, The King) spoke to the importance of composers protecting and educating themselves about their income in the streaming age.

“The plumbing of the music industry is really complicated,” he said. “Organizations like ASCAP...are so crucial for that.”

 
ASCAP's Amanda Shoffner, composers Amie Doherty & Michael Abels, ASCAP's Shawn LeMone, composers Siddhartha Khosla & Nicholas Britell, ASCAP's Mike Todd, Lauren Iossa & Alex Vilkas
