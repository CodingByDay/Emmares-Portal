Trump OKs U.S.-China trade deal

President Trump signed off on a phase-one trade deal with China Thursday, averting a new wave of tariffs planned for Sunday on about $160 billion of Chinese goods. The deal includes a promise by China to buy more U.S. agricultural goods and rolls back some existing tariffs. News of the deal was met with immediate criticism from some Republicans concerned about giving up “tariff leverage” needed for a broader trade agreement and some Democrats warning “anything short of a meaningful, lasting agreement would be unacceptable for the American people."

$ignificant figures

19 months. The British pound surged to its highest level since May 2018 as exit polls forecast a strong majority for Boris Johnson’s Conservative Party in the U.K. general election.

$6.2 million. The amount more than a dozen alleged victims of Harvey Weinstein's sexual abuse would split under a tentative $25 million settlement he reached with accusers.

$4 million. The DOJ charged 10 ex-NFL players with defrauding the league’s health-care benefit program, alleging they submitted false claims resulting in a $3.4 million payout.

Highly quotable

"Anger management problem." Greta Thunberg changed her Twitter bio to mock Trump's outrage at her winning Time's Person of the Year after he tweeted, "Chill Greta, Chill!" 

"High-speed recovery." New Zealand authorities successfully recovered six bodies during a risky mission to retrieve the victims of the White Island volcano eruption.

"Airplane wreckage." Chilean officials said they found debris from a military plane that vanished en route to Antarctica this week, along with remains of the 38 passengers on board. 

This is not normal

Peak meat. Scientists are calling on countries around the world to phase out livestock production within 10 years to counter the large proportion of emissions from the meat and dairy sector that add to climate change. 

The future is now

Grabbing history. To bring samples of Mars’ surface back to Earth on future space missions, British engineers are building robotic, vision-based tech to enable a rover to autonomously lift Martian rocks off the ground.

What's good

988. The FCC is setting up a 3-digit number that’ll ring directly to a suicide prevention hotline, replacing the 10-digit one that’s used for other emergencies, to make it easier for people in crises to seek help.
