Is Japan the next Wuhan?'

As China again reported a slowdown in the tally of new coronavirus deaths and infections Wednesday, Japan reported an uptick. More than 3,000 kilometers away, the country has emerged as one of the riskiest places for the spread of COVID-10 as infections more than doubled in the past week to 84, tying Singapore as the country outside mainland China with the most cases. Critics of PM Shinzo Abe have called the government's response too lax, but perhaps more troubling is that cases are surging in multiple parts of the country — sometimes with little to link the outbreaks. What's more, quarantined passengers have finally disembarked the Diamond Princess cruise ship despite concern that some could still test positive for the virus, fueling more uncertainty among Japanese health officials about how far the pathogen will spread.

More: 

  • Hubei reported 349 new infections and 108 new deaths over Feb. 19, a drop that rests partly on a new methodology that differentiates suspected cases from those that are confirmed. 
  • While hundreds of cases were confirmed aboard the Diamond Princess cruise ship during the two weeks it docked in Yokohama, they are not counted among Japan’s total.
