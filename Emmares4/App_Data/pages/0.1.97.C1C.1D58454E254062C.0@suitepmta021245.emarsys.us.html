How to Have a Great First Week
We recently asked what helps you shine in the first week on a new job. As always, you dropped some great knowledge. Here are a few answers from Muse readers on how they knock it out the park when they're starting a new gig:

“Ask questions, be on time, and be kind.”
— Myra Wardhana, retail

“The best piece of advice I'd give to anyone (including myself) on a new job is to be patient with yourself—you're not expected to know it all in a day—and ask as many questions as you need to understand processes and procedures. It's better to be prepared before you start than to be prideful and get it wrong.”
—Ashlyn Mason, branded content manager

“[I] ate in the lunchroom so I could meet people and they could get to know me.”
—Sharadon Smith, career advisor

“Get to know the team and stakeholders.”
—Anujit Saha, sales director
