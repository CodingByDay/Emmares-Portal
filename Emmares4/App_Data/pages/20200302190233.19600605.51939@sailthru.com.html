Greetings, QuickTake readers! In this edition: Covid-19 deaths rise in the U.S., Netanyahu nears a majority and why an extra month of summer is bad news for Australia.

Centrists line up behind Biden

Emboldened by his win in South Carolina's primary election over the weekend, Joe Biden on Monday picked up two new reasons to enter Super Tuesday super confident. Rival centrists Amy Klobuchar and Pete Buttigieg both ended their 2020 presidential bids and pledged their support to the former Vice President hours before Democrats in 14 states and one territory were set to go to the polls. Their support, which could block Bernie Sanders from a clear path to the nomination, narrowed the race to one among Biden, Sanders, Elizabeth Warren and Michael Bloomberg on the biggest day of the 2020 election so far.

This is not normal

Extra dog days. Australian summers are now 31 days longer and winters 23 days shorter than in the 1950s, new research says, which means more extreme heat and less time for controlled burns to prevent bushfires.

The future is now

Roads of tomorrow. In the latest gain for driverless rideshares, Lyft tested 19 autonomous cars that drove 43,000 miles in 2019, contributing to a 40% increase in total autonomous miles driving last year, to 2.87 million.

 

What's good

Critical progress. Amid coronavirus preparations, Africa's fight against Ebola reached an encouraging "milestone," the WHO said, after a vaccine was fast-tracked to the DR of Congo, Burundi, Ghana and Zambia.
