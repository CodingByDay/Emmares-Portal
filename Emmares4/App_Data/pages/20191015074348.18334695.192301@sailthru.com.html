THE BIG STORY

Kurdish forces previously allied with the US have turned to Russia-backed Syria for help

 

Quick background: The Kurdish-led Syrian Demcoratic Forces (SDF) was a key US ally, and had been the most effective western-backed force fighting ISIS. It played a huge role in the terror group's eventual territorial defeat. 


But after US President Donald Trump decided to withdraw pockets of American troops deployed in northeastern Syria, the SDF reached a deal for assistance with the government of Syrian President Bashar al-Assad, which is backed by Russia.


Why would they turn to Assad? The Kurds came under assault by Turkey's armed forces immediately following Trump’s troop withdrawal. The deal means Russia-backed Syrian troops will move to help the Kurds.


Trump's move has drawn widespread criticism, including from Republicans, with many accusing the US of betraying the Kurds.


👉 SDF commander in chief Mazloum Abdi wrote in Foreign Policy that “if we have to choose between compromises and the genocide of our people, we will surely choose life for our people.”

STAYING ON TOP OF THIS

 

A Fort Worth cop who shot and killed a black woman inside her own home has been arrested

Police were called to Atatiana Jefferson's home early Saturday morning after a neighbor saw her door open and expressed concern for her safety. Aaron Dean, the officer who responded to the call, drew his handgun and shot through the window of the home after seeing someone inside, killing Jefferson, police said.


Authorities said that Dean, who suddenly resigned from the police department yesterday, was not being cooperative with the investigation. He was arrested and booked on suspicion of murder.

THE SMARTEST DUMB PHONE

 

The dumbest phone in the world may be genius — for parents

If you’re a parent, you’ve probably been thinking a lot about your kid’s relationship with technology. This is especially a challenge for parents of kids who may be too young for a smartphone, but old enough to need to call or text their parents.


A new phone, called Gabb, does exactly that. Here’s Katie Notopoulos’s description of it: “It has no browser (so no Googling “boobs”), no apps (so no social media or video games), no Bluetooth or AirDrop. It doesn’t even offer email. There’s just calling, text messaging, an FM radio, and a calendar — which won’t even sync with a Google Calendar.”


Worried about sexting? Gabb only does SMS texting, not MMS, which means you can send plain text messages only — no video or pictures.


Read Notopoulos’s review of Gabb, and whether it’s the solution parents have been looking for.

THE REVOLUTION WILL BE TIKTOK'D

 

Indigenous teens are making TikTok videos about racism in Australia

TikTok’s basic features — music, text overlay, one person playing multiple roles — are being put to excellent use by Indigenous teens in Australia. They’re using the app to challenge racial stereotypes, share stories of discrimination, and talk about what being Aboriginal means to them.


Some are even using the platform to powerfully reframe historical events from an Indigenous perspective.
