June 17, 2021

Dear Reader,
 

This week we’re thinking about the future of the night sky. Thousands of spacecraft now orbit Earth, but soon tens of thousands more could join them as part of a growing trend to launch “mega constellations” of satellites to provide high-speed Internet and other services all across the planet. Satellite-clogged skies could be bad news for astronomers and other space-focused scientists, not to mention the stargazing general public. Our lead story details a fresh legal challenge to SpaceX’s Starlink, the world’s largest mega constellation. From there, read about Europe’s new mission to Venus, a dusty explanation for a star’s mysterious dimming, multiple flavors of fast radio bursts, and much, much more.

Lee Billings, Senior Editor, Space & Physics
