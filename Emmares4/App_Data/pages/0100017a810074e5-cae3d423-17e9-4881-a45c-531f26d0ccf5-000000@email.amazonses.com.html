Google Launches July 2021 Core Update

Google changed the algorithm.

Whenever Google decides to dramatically change their algorithm, they end up screwing over publications like ours in favor of the big corporate websites. We lost about 20% of the traffic to How-To Geek overnight thanks to the July 2021 update. Thankfully CloudSavvy IT and our other sites didn’t see any drop this time around—diversifying makes a lot of sense. It’s still not as bad as 2017, when we lost 45% of our traffic and had to spend years trying to build it back.

Does that mean that Google suddenly changed their algorithm to feature better content? Nope. In most cases, our well-researched, in-depth articles are now ranking lower than outdated content from other sites. Back in 2017 their algorithm change was so bad that we were being outranked by scraper websites that had stolen our own articles.

And, of course, Google allows for no recourse for businesses that they’ve managed to screw over. Last time we spent months trying to contact people at Google to get them to fix the glaring problems with their rankings, but didn’t get anywhere.

The lesson here is to never allow yourself to depend on a single Big Tech company. Diversify your traffic and income sources. If you build your business on the back of Google or Facebook, don’t be surprised when they stand up, shake you off, and leave you on your ass.
