New Skift Research Report — The Impact of COVID-19 on the Online Travel In

In just a few weeks coronavirus has spread across the globe and upended the travel industry. Online travel has lived through two past recessions – the tech bubble crash and the global financial crisis – but it has never seen anything like this outbreak before.

The virus has been fast moving and so has its impact on the travel industry, leading to an overload of information that can be hard to track and digest. This report aims to straighten out what we currently know about how online travel is being affected. We present clean and comparable estimates for declines in both transaction volume and revenue at the world's largest online booking sites. We explore how this crisis could shift power dynamics between online distributors and travel suppliers and try to tease out how short-term rentals and metasearch are reacting differently from full-service OTAs.

Finally this report analyzes public statements made by the largest three online travel agencies in the world, Booking Holdings, Expedia Group, and Trip.com. We break down and quantify what those management teams have said specifically about COVID-19.

What You'll Learn in This Report

  • The estimated decline in transaction volumes at Booking Holdings, Expedia Group, Trip.com Group and Airbnb.
  • Estimated range of revenue impacts from coronavirus on the seven largest publicly traded online travel agencies.
  • How the coronavirus outbreak could swing the balance of power between travel suppliers and online distributors.
  • How full service online travel agencies stack up against those specialized in short term rentals and metasearch sites.
  • Deep dive analysis of COVID-19 impact on Booking Holdings, Expedia Group, and Trip.com Group.
