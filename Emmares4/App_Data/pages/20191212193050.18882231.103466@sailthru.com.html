Simone Giertz rose to YouTube stardom as the self-titled "Queen of Shitty Robots." She chose the name for herself after gaining internet fame for the wildly popular videos of her barely functioning machines. Take, for example, the teeth-cleaning robot that swished a toothbrush violently across her face, and a breakfast-serving robot that dispensed both cereal and milk but missed the bowl almost entirely, dumping the morning meal all over the place instead.

The title wasn't just for laughs, though. Giertz (pronounced "Yetch") underscored the shittiness of her robots to prevent fear of imperfection from getting in the way of her creativity. The robots were supposed to be intentional failures. Now Giertz is fully embracing her inventiveness. She's hacked together a Tesla pickup truck that's way cooler (and more real) than Elon's Cybertruck, and she's preparing to go into space with a DIY astronaut training program. WIRED's Lauren Goode has written an inspiring and surprising profile of the woman behind the robots.

Also this week, we bring you gift ideas for couch potatoes, our guide to the best Kindle readers, and Lisa Wood Shapiro's story of her quest to clean up the dirty air in her Brooklyn apartment.
