Drumroll, please. PlayStation's newest console finally has a name—and while there's been plenty of suspense, it's short on surprise. The PlayStation 5, available for the 2020 holiday season, might sound like the next iteration in a line of beloved consoles. But its creators have taken years to turn it into something genuinely new. WIRED's Peter Rubin wrote about that effort this spring, and now, we have a deeper look. This week, Rubin has the story of building the PlayStation 5, which will ditch the spinning hard drive for a solid-state drive and add a haptics-packed controller for more immersive gameplay. You can read all about it here.

Also this week: why you can't talk about the Essential Gem without talking about Andy Rubin, USB-C reaches new levels of almost-ubiquity, and a look into the obsessive world of tiny keyboards.
