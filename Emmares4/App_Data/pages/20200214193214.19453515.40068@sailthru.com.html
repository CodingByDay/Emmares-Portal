TGIF, QuickTake readers! Start your weekend in high spirits with these 10 uplifting stories from the past week.

1. Finally under control. For the first time this season, Australia’s harmful bushfires in New South Wales have been contained by heavy rains.

2. Representation via text. Ivorian designer O’Plérou created an emoji a day for a year to show African culture through his eyes.

3. N. Ireland’s symbols of change. Robyn Peoples and Sharni Edwards wed in the region’s first gay wedding after it enacted marriage equality.

4. Dino discovery. A 79.5 million-year-old fossil of the oldest-known North American tyrannosaur, aka the "reaper of death," was found in Canada.

5. A blue-collar robo-dog. Spot, made by Boston Dynamics, landed a gig patroling an oil vessel where it’ll sniff out gas leaks and generate reports.

6. Unique newborns. The rare weedy sea dragon was bred at a San Diego aquarium, making it one of the world's few to accomplish the feat.

7. Fruitful life. 112-year-old Chitetsu Watanabe of Japan was certified as the world's oldest man and said the secret to longevity is smiling.

8. Bottle rocket record. A 2,800-pound firework set a world record for the largest aerial firework when it was launched over a Colorado ski town.

9. Coma relief? Researchers say an implant may “jump-start” a tiny region of the brain, allowing patients to “wake up.” It worked in monkeys, at least.

10. Valentine’s Day upgraded. Swiss chocolatier Barry Callebaut will sell 3D-printed chocolates to offer customers a customized gourmet treat. 

Have a great long weekend! Watch your inbox for our next newsletter on Tuesday. Follow QuickTake on Twitter, YouTube, Instagram, and Facebook.

Thanks for reading!
-Masiel Torres
