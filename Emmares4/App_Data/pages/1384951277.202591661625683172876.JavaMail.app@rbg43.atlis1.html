Dear Reader,

An alliance of more than 200 million farmers and fishers in Africa advocates for an "agroecology" approach to increasing crop productivity to feed the region's growing population, according to a new essay by the organization's general coordinator and program coordinator. The writers describe this approach as small-scale, ecofriendly cultivation methods that combine indigenous knowledge with cutting-edge science. However, this approach puts the Alliance for Food Sovereignty in Africa at odds with organizations, including ones well-funded by the Bill and Melinda Gates Foundation, that advocate for the use of GM seeds, synthetic fertilizers, petroleum-fueled machinery, and other Western science and technology to address the region's agricultural and related goals. To learn more about these tensions and ways that the writers suggest resolving them, check out the essay below by Million Belay and Bridget Mugambe.

Robin Lloyd
