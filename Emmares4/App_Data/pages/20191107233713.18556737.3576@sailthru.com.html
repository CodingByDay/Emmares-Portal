WeWork's botched IPO became a money pit for SoftBank's Masayoshi Son, but other C-suite holders find valuable lessons in the office-space company's crash. Goldman Sachs's David Solomon says investors in growth stocks like WeWork need to see a clear path to profitability. JPMorgan's Jamie Dimon says private companies should shore up governance before going public. If you've been busy moving your desk, chair and red stapler to another "Office Space" this week, here are some stories you might have missed. — Michael Tighe

 

Tariff Taper?

Donald Trump, a staunch "Tariff Man," may be holstering his preferred weapon in the trade war with China. If the two sides reach a "phase-one deal," they've agreed to roll back some duties on each other's goods. But even if the levies break, an agreement may not happen until December.

 

Best in Class

Stanford's business school keeps its bragging rights as No. 1 for another year, according to Bloomberg's ranking of 94 institutions. Once you've sorted through the list, read the elevator pitches from five deans about why an MBA still matters.

 

A Very Good Year

It's always good to be Warren Buffett, but this year it's been especially good. Berkshire Hathaway has earned a record profit, and it's sitting on a provocative cash pile. A primary driver of that success is a decidedly 19th-century enterprise.

Golden Opportunity

Indians love gold so much they've got an estimated $1 trillion worth stashed at home. But sometimes they need extra cash, and making trades at a bank or pawnbroker can be embarrassing. Now there's an app for that, with loan agents maintaining discretion by going to the stash.

 

K-Pop's Dark Side

The wholesome image cultivated by South Korea's K-pop juggernaut is being tarnished by a dissonant chorus of scandals. The performer known as Seungri is among those under criminal investigation amid swirling allegations of prostitution, drug use and violence.

 

Staying Rich Forever

What to do with that big inheritance? That's the dilemma facing scions of Asia's aging tycoons. Millennials often have different priorities, so the private banks that run boot camps for ultra-rich kids are tweaking their itineraries to include a jungle trek and a "Shark Tank."

 

On the Cheap

Hong Kong's leaders are being accused of spending "peanuts" in their efforts to appease the twin elephants of pro-democracy demonstrations and a recession. The fiscal stimulus announced so far amounts to less than 1% of the city's economy.

 

Couch-Potato's Conundrum

So many streams, but only so many hours. That's the couch-potato's conundrum as the streaming wars begin. Disney's service is about to launch with a mammoth catalog, Netflix strikes back with more Japanese anime, and Apple is betting on lower prices as it ramps up programming.
