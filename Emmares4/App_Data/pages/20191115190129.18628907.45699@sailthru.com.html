TGIF, TicToc readers! Start your weekend in high spirits with these 10 uplifting stories from the past week.

1. Motorola wants you to party like its the 2000s.
It took the wraps off its Razr reboot, a $1,499 foldable smartphone that has an optional “Retro Razr” mode if you’re feeling nostalgic.

2. Montgomery, Alabama’s first black mayor assumed power.
In the city once known as “the cradle of the Confederacy,” Steven Reed praised citizens for choosing “future over fear” after he was sworn-in.

3. This real-life Iron Man took human flight to the next level.
British inventor Richard Browning smashed his own world record for the fastest speed in a jet engine suit by soaring through the air at 86 mph.

4. Italy spearheaded a new climate change fight.
Amid historic flooding in Venice, the country became the first to make climate change lessons compulsory for schoolchildren.

5. At 90, Yayoi Kusama is more popular than ever.
New Yorkers are queuing up for hours to spend a single minute in the beloved Japanese artist’s infinity mirror room exhibit.

6. A bum-shaking llama topped Brits’ holiday shopping lists.
Toy retailers predicted Bobbi the Booty Shakin’ Llama and a diverse line of Barbie dolls would be this year’s best-selling stocking stuffers.

7. The future of travel is flashy—and fast.
Virgin Hyperloop One unveiled one of its levitating passenger pods that offers tailor-made infotainment while reaching up to 760 mph.

8. The UN launched the first-ever bid for cheaper insulin.
To coincide with World Diabetes Day, the World Health Organization said it would start prequalifying the medicine to vastly expand treatment access.

9. A lost deer species reappeared 30 years later.
The silver-backed chevrotain, a.k.a. Vietnamese mouse-deer, invigorated conservationists to step up protections so we don’t lose it again.

10. This very special doggo delighted the internet.
Narwahl, full name: Narwhal the Little Magical Furry Unicorn, is a perfectly healthy rescue puppy with an extra tail on its face. (No, it doesn’t wag.)

Now that you’re in a better mood, tell your friends to sign up to receive our newsletter five days a week, and follow us on Twitter, YouTube, Instagram and Facebook.

Thanks for reading!
