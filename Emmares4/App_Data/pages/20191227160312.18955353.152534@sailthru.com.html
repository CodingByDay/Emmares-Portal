Happy holidays! In case you hadn't noticed, it's officially winter (for those of us in the Northern Hemisphere, at least). For many of us, that means cold temperatures and dry air—conditions that are just as bad for our plants as our skin. So, what better New Year's resolution than to give your tired looking plants the upgrade they deserve? A new home. 

 

The good news: With a simple hack, you can turn just about any vessel into the perfect planter. Here's how.

WHAT I DID

STEP 1: Decide on your pot 

Since you'll be drilling your own drainage holes, you can use essentially any vessel. For ones made of clay, concrete, or terra cotta (like the IKEA one I used), you'll use masonry drill bits, but if you have your eye on a glass container, you can purchase glass drill bits—as long as the glass is thick enough to withstand drilling.  

STEP 2: Drill! 
Once you have your pot, place it upside down on a flat surface on top of a dishtowel or rag—this will prevent vibration—and carefully drill one to three holes, depending on the pot size.
