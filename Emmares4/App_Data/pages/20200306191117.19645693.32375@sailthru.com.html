TGIF, QuickTake readers! Start your weekend in high spirits with these 10 uplifting stories from the past week.

1. The fight against Ebola reaches a milestone. The DRC, Burundi, Ghana, and Zambia created a vaccine that's expected to be registered in additional countries in the coming weeks.

2. New South Wales is officially free from bushfires. After more than 240 days, there is currently no active fires in the Australian state. 

3. NASA snaps its highest-res panorama yet. The Curiosity Mars took 1,000+ photos to create a 1.8-billion pixel panorama of the Red Planet.

4. A "new era" of gene editing to treat diseases. In a first, scientists injected a harmless virus into a blind patient's eye that restored their sight. 

5. Meet the first female duo to win Pritzker Architecture Prize. Yvonne Farrell and Shelley McNamara won the award for leading the way in a male-dominated profession.⁠

6. Leather made out of cacti? Adrián López Velarde and Marte Cázarez created Desserto to replace the need for animals in the fashion industry.

 7. The world’s first rollable smartphone is coming. TCL is developing a phone that extends to tablet-sized device without wrinkles or creases.

8.  A potential gamechanger for in-flight emergencies. A new smart device can send data from ill passengers to doctors on the ground.

9. Sri Lanka's Sea Sisters are reclaiming the ocean. The organization is helping local women learn to surf to empower them to work in surf tourism.

10. The world's best cheese. A Swiss gruyere was chosen as top cheese from 26 nations in the World Championship Cheese Contest in Wisconsin. 

Have a great weekend! Watch your inbox for our next newsletter on Monday. Follow QuickTake on Twitter, YouTube, Instagram, and Facebook.

Thanks for reading!
