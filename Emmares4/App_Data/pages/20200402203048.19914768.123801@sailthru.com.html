Have you lost your sense of smell? Are you convinced your sore throat is something more than seasonal allergies? Do you think you or someone you know may have contracted Covid-19, the disease caused by the novel coronavirus? Stay calm. We've got a guide on what to do next.

The top-level advice is pretty simple: Stay put, and treat it with rest and fluids, much like you would a regular cold. The vast majority of Covid-19 patients won't need medical care. That most likely includes you too—though you should still isolate yourself as best you can, and get in touch with a doctor through a videochat app if your symptoms worsen.

Our primer on managing the disease is just one of the guides we've written about how to deal with the changes brought on by the coronavirus pandemic. We also have a list of apps and tools to fend off cabin fever, and a list of the businesses and non-profits supporting those suffering or otherwise affected by the virus.

Stay safe, stay home, and thank you for coming to WIRED for your daily news updates, tips, and analysis.
