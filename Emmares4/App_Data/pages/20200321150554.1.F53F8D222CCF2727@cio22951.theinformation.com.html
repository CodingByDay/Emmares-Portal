Dear Reader,

My what a difference a week makes. Last week, I was writing this column curled up at a desk from home, wondering when things would go back to normal. This week was anything but normal, and we won’t see normal for a long time. Our hearts go out to everyone affected by COVID-19 and the amazing health workers on the front lines.

The week began with energy. My column on female founders, from last week, generated a lot of conversations and so I looked for ways to do more to do on the subject. That led to a Zoom video call with reporter and columnist Kara Swisher to discuss the issue. Unfortunately, the call was ruined by idiots who hacked it with screenshared porn. I am deeply sorry to anyone who joined us on the call, which we aborted. We will reschedule in some form. And here’s how to prevent this—called “Zoombombing” from happening to you.

In between it all, a lot happened here at The Information. We revamped our daily Briefing email to focus on the impact of COVID-19. If you aren’t receiving it and want to, you can sign up here. We broke news about how Facebook and Google are handling the crisis with employees. And we tried to help you all manage these strange times with a workshop on working from home that, it is fair to say, has already dramatically changed how I work. You can watch the video here.

And don’t forget to check out this week’s 411 podcast. Our Hong Kong bureau chief Shai Oster reports from the other side of the crisis in Hong Kong. It’s hopeful.


I had lots of column ideas baking this week, but none of them are ready yet or seem right. Everything just keeps moving so fast. Topics that seemed relevant on Tuesday, seemed trivial Friday. So for today, I’ll leave you to catch up on coverage you may have missed as you were dealing with the new normal at work and planning your homeschooling schedules. And to those of you with fancy Google Docs planning out your kids’ every minute, I’m impressed and resentful at the same time. How do you have the time? But oh, do send me the schedules. I’m not above copying others’ parenting wins!

Looking ahead, one topic we will continue to dig into is the impact of the crisis on private tech companies and investors, following our reporting about the venture business this week. It’s crazy to think that just a few weeks ago we were stuck in that “When will the good times end?” conversation that seemed to play over and over again without the good times actually ending. It was starting to seem like any downturn would be gradual and perhaps not that devastating. Now, we’re talking about companies that have “removed revenue” from the equation, aka, their businesses are falling off a cliff. We’ll be hosting a live Q&A on the topic next week. Stay tuned for details.

Lastly, I want to thank you all for your readership and support during these times. I started this business to inform professionals about the rapid changes happening in the world and the technology companies driving them. This is a key moment for us and the news industry and we are ready to rise to the challenge. We are also in awe of the other journalists doing so.

To all of you, thank you.
