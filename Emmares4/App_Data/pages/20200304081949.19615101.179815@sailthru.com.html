THE BIG STORY

 

A big night for Joe Biden

 

15 states and territories voted for their Democratic presidential nominee yesterday, and most of them chose Joe Biden. He won the races in Texas, Virginia, North Carolina, Massachusetts, Alabama, Tennessee, Arkansas, Minnesota and Oklahoma. 


“They told us when we got to Super Tuesday, it’d be over. Well, it may be over for the other guy!” he told supporters in California. 


The other guy, Bernie Sanders, won in his home state of Vermont, as well as Colorado, Utah and California. And he came second to Biden in most of the other states, meaning he will pick up delegates almost everywhere. The race between the two is still very much alive. (Maine still hasn't been called.)


But for Michael Bloomberg and Elizabeth Warren, things are looking bad. Bloomberg spent over half a billion dollars on advertising to be competitive in these races, and he appears to have won only one: the caucus in American Samoa, where he came first with 175 votes. The future of his campaign is uncertain. Warren is also in a tough spot: she didn’t win anywhere, and came third in her home state, Massachusetts.

STAYING ON TOP OF THIS

 

Spare a thought for the people of Tennessee 

 

The state was hit by a string of tornadoes yesterday, and at least 22 people are dead and many more homeless. 


"It was so bright outside, just like a completely white sky," East Nashville resident Lisa Estrada told BuzzFeed News. She and her family huddled under a staircase to wait the storm out. "When people tell you that it's like the sound of a freight train, it really is and you just kind of sit there and stare at the windows and wonder whether it's going to blow in or not."


Tennessee Gov. Bill Lee said there was "loss of life all across the state," and officials expect the death toll will rise.

LAUGHING AT BOMBS

 

The Syrian girl whose dad taught her to laugh at the sound of bombs has reached safety


Last month a video of three-year-old Salwa and her father laughing as bombs landed near their home went viral. Abdullah Mohammad said he used the memory of children setting off fireworks during Eid al-Fitr to teach his daughter to laugh at the explosions caused by jets bombing the city of Idlib.


“Is that a plane or a bomb?” Mohammad asks Salwa in the video. She responds, “A shell, and when it falls, we will laugh!”


Salwa is safe, for now at least. She and her parents recently managed to escape the city, and made it to Turkey. “Now, my daughter will be able to go to school. I hope that the conflict in Syria can soon end and that I can return,” he dad told Turkey’s state-run Anadolu Agency.

 

A reporter for The Guardian met the family at their new home and saw Salwa dancing around in a pink princess dress. “For the first time ever, she can laugh at normal things,” she wrote.

WHEN YOU PROMISE A LLAMA...

 

Five years ago, a guy told his sister that when she gets married, he’ll bring a llama to the wedding


She wasn’t even dating at the time. She was only 17! But they were on a road trip, and having one of those sprawling road trip conversations. She talked about how she’d like to get married some day, he teased that he wouldn’t come to the wedding, she said he’d have to come, so he shot back that he’d only come if he could bring a llama as a date. She agreed. 


It’s the kind of rando conversation that’s easily forgotten, but our man Mendl Weinstock never forgets. “For the first few years she definitely thought it was bluffing, but as we got closer to the wedding and especially after she got engaged, she realized I was serious," he told BuzzFeed News. 


Readers, he was serious.
