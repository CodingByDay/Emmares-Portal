PRESSING
 
Putting pressure on the opposition in order to win back possession

In order to regain possession and reduce the effectiveness of opposition attacks, your team must be confident and intelligent in their pressing game. Pressing is a key defensive function and calls for your players to be smart about their movement and work as an efficient team unit. This ensures that as a defensive unit, your backline pose a much greater challenge to the opposition on game day.

When pressing, fitness and speed are often key to ensuring players don't gain ground or have time to pass the ball to a teammate. It is also key to ensure that your players understand that even if they don't win the ball back right away, forcing the opposition to pass backwards and side to side can often be considered its own victory in ensuring their progress up the pitch is halted. Players in your team that contribute constantly throughout the game by pressing are often some of the most valuable players in your squad.
