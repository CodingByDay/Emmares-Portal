INVITE FRIENDS
Social failings and lack of AML checks
 

The Story

The U.K.’s Gaming Commission (UKGC) has ordered Caesars Entertainment U.K. to pay £13 million in fines for eight offenses the regulator has found. The casino operator is accused of ignoring social responsibility failings and a lack of anti-money laundering (AML) checks. The social failings all involve customers who spent beyond their means, or had tried to self-exclude in the past. On the AML side, Caesars is accused of not carrying out adequate source of funds checks on £3.5 million, with £1.6 million in losses, from a single customer over a three month span. The fine, which will go to the National Strategy to Reduce Gambling Harms, is an important lesson to the operator, but not much against their bottom line.
