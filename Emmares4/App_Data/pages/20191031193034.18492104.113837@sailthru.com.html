Back in the day (like, five years ago), carriers used to entice customers to buy new smartphones by offering them at a steep discount. You could walk into a store and pick up a brand-new, $800 device for just $200. Of course, you had to sign up for a two-year contract to get that sweet deal. But hey, cheap phones!

Today, phones are no longer cheap. We pay full price for them—either all at once or in installments—and we do so happily, because in exchange, we've been freed from those long-term contracts. So carriers have come up with a new way of getting us to sign up and stick around: bundling in free subscriptions to streaming services.

This week, AT&T announced that its mobile customers will get HBO Max for free, while everyone else has to pay $15 a month. As Brian Barrett explains, this subscription model is proliferating, with carriers bundling streaming services into their plans as a way to gain new customers and then keep them paying as long as possible.
