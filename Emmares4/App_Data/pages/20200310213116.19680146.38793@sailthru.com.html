How coronavirus kills

Regions from Italy to New York amped up containment efforts as the global death toll from coronavirus topped 4,200 Tuesday with more than 117,000 confirmed cases. In the U.S., the response has shifted to damage control amid more travel restrictions, university closings, and the cancellation of hundreds of corporate, sports and entertainment events.

Still, medical experts say optimizing care for the global contagion and avoiding unnecessary panic relies on understanding how the virus kills: 

  • The spread. It happens via contact with virus-laden droplets from an infected person’s cough, sneeze or breath. A troubled-test kit rollout also impeded U.S. officials' early efforts to contain it.
  • The onset. Infection starts in the nose, causing little more than a cough most of the time if the virus stays above the throat.
  • The tipping point. Danger starts if the virus spreads to the lungs, causing shortness of breath from low blood oxygen levels.
  • The lethality. Covid-19 turns critical when patients suffer respiratory failure or experience other vital organ dysfunction or septic shock.
  • The victims. Patients with the highest risk are those aged 60+ and those with hypertension, diabetes and cardiovascular disease.

This is not normal

Crucial predictions. After abnormally high winter temps led to billions in lost revenue for energy producers, meteorologists are under pressure to sharpen their models to consider the economic impact of climate change.

The future is now

Bendy bricks. Scientists in Melbourne developed a concrete prototype that won't shatter when bent that's made from recycled coal waste, which could reduce the carbon footprint of the entire cement-making process.
