Almost all top-shelf Android handsets have one thing in common: They're powered by the industry-leading Snapdragon chip set made by Qualcomm. Earlier this week, we got our first peek at next year's Snapdragon processor, which means we can now make a reasonable guess as to what capabilities we'll see in 2020's premier smartphones.

The new chip is faster and more power-efficient, but the most important element is support for 5G. Qualcomm is pushing the upgrade hard: The company released two models of Snapdragon chip, one for high-end phones and one for midrange phones, and each will have the ability to connect to the next-generation networks. Yes, 5G infrastructure has been slow to roll out. But Qualcomm is betting that with more 5G-ready Android devices, it will spur adoption across the industry. You can read all about the race to 5G connectivity in Lauren Goode's report on the news.
