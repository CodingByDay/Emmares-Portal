With cinemas allowed to reopen in Victoria from June 22, surely the rest of the country will follow suit soon? We certainly hope so! While we wait for those glorious doors to swing open again, we've got reviews of this week's digital releases (Talking About Trees, Same Kind Of Different As Me, Escape From Pretoria, Guns Akimbo, Endings, Beginnings and The Assistant), plus a little isolation-nearly-ending screen reading: we talk to director Gregor Jordan about taking on Tim Winton's Dirt Music; get the inside track on The Mandalorian from Aussie cinematographer Greig Fraser; take a look at the possible end of Event Cinemas George Street in Sydney; find out about shooting for Quibi from Blackballed cinematographer Joshua Weinstein; and log on for a scan through the programme for The Sydney Film Festival's online event. Stay safe, stay sane, and see you at the movies...sometime soon!  
THE LATEST
Gregor Jordan Rolls Camera On Dirt Music
Making his first film in Australia since 2003's Ned Kelly, and his first film since 2010's Unthinkable, director Gregor Jordan heads into serious literary territory with his adaptation of Tim Winton's cherished novel, Dirt Music, starring Garrett Hedlund and Kelly Macdonald.

Greig Fraser: The Mandalorian

After shooting the local likes of The Last Ride and The Boys Are Back, Greig Fraser is now working on The Mandalorian, The Batman and Dune.
Frances O'Connor Set To Direct
The acclaimed Australian actress will make her directorial debut from her own script based on the early life of essential author Emily Bronte.

A To Z Of Non-White Aussie Film

Australia has a checkered and not always pleasant cinematic relationship with people of colour, and this feature tracks the many ups and downs.
End For Event Cinemas George St.?
Is Event Cinemas George Street in Sydney really going to be demolished to make way for another massive skyscraper? Maybe, or maybe not...

Sydney Film Fest Online Programme

Quickly pivoting in the face of COVID-19, The Sydney Film Festival will be a wholly online event this year, but there's still much to savour.
Working In A COVID-19 World
What is it like working on a film set in this current era of COVID-19? Veteran camera assistant Michelle Pizanis fills us in with this special report. 

Preview: The Vast Of Night

After finding great success at the Slamdance and Toronto Film Festivals, debut filmmaker Andrew Patterson's The Vast Of Night is now on Amazon.
Joshua Weinstein: Blackballed
Cinematographer Joshua Weinstein talks about the challenges of shooting the basketball doco Blackballed for the short form platform Quibi.
