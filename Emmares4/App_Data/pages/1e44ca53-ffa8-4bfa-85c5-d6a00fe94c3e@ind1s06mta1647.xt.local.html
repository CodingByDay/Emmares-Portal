YOUR DIABETES DIET
When you have diabetes, what you eat can have a big effect on how you well you manage it. The right foods will help steady your blood sugar and keep you at a healthy weight.
When you cook at home instead of going out, it's easier to keep track of what you eat. You’ll find plenty of resources with tips for meal planning, grocery shopping, and whipping up delicious dishes.
Don't forget to watch what you drink as well. Water is the best choice, but you can also make some tweaks to enjoy diabetes-friendly versions of sweet tea, hot chocolate, and more.
 
 
GLYCEMIC INDEX
The glycemic index, or GI, is a measure of how quickly a food can raise blood sugar. Foods with a high GI, like white rice and pineapple, raise blood glucose more than foods with a low GI, such as lentils and whole wheat bread.
Simple swaps can help balance your meals and manage your diabetes. You could also follow a diet based on the glycemic index, like the Glycemic Index Diet and the Sugar Busters! diet.
The glycemic index can be a useful tool to help keep blood sugar levels steady, but it doesn't consider portion sizes, how a food is cooked, or the fact that many nutritious foods have a higher GI than foods with little nutritional value.
 
 
HEALTHY SNACKING
Choose the right snacks, and they'll help keep your blood sugar levels in check, curb your appetite, and give you a boost of energy. Aim to keep them to 150 calories or less, and try not to mindlessly graze.
Examples of snacks that can satisfy you without spiking your blood sugar:
• String cheese and a half cup of blueberries
• Yogurt and soup mix combined as a dip for veggies or low-salt pretzels
Keep in mind that exercise can make your blood sugar level drop, so check it before you work out. If it’s too low, have a sweet snack such as handful of dried fruit or four ounces of juice.
