Trends of the Week

Trends of the week

Here are a few big news items from big companies, with green links to all the sweet, sweet added context:

  • Conference moratorium
    More happened this week than COVID-19 news, and yet the virus loomed large in tech with major impacts to tech events. Y Combinator shelved its demo day, as did 500 Startups. Now, Google and Facebook have cancelled their developer conferences and SXSW was canceled yesterday. Read more here.
  • Dorsey preps for a fight to stay CEO of Twitter
    Twitter CEO Jack Dorsey has always been one of the odder chief executives but Wall Street might be turning against him if an activist investor has his way. In response, Dorsey and his company are going on the defensive to justify his track record, but it might be an uphill battle for the co-founder whose company has seen slow growth over the past couple years while other tech stocks have surged. Read more about it in our coverage.
  • Waymo raises big money and bankrupts an adversary
    If you move against Google, things might not go well for you. On the heels of a $2.5 billion external fundraise this week that Waymo announced, we also heard that Anthony Levandowski filed bankruptcy after being ordered to pay Google $179 million by a judge. Read more here.
