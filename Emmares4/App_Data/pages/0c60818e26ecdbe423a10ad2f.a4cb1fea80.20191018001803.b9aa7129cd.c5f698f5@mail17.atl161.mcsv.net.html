Whither VC?
Hola,


Aggregate valuations of venture-backed companies are still at an all-time high. 

But our quarterly deep dive MoneyTree report — created in partnership with PwC — shows deals and funding down almost across the board (in the geographical and industry-specific sense).

Looks like the money and dealmaking is pulling back. 

We had a media dinner in Feb 2016, not long after the last time this happened, and the prompt for one discussion was: Is the dip in VC just a blip? At the time we had seen 1742 deals and $27B going to VC-backed companies in the previous quarter.

Since then we are up to $52B invested last quarter. So clearly it was a blip and the startup foie gras'ing went on.

Our take: The most recent drop in deals and funding will inevitably lead to people saying that tech and venture capital have lost steam, but what we're really seeing is a shift in investor focus.

The first 3 quarters of 2019 saw 12% fewer deals compared to 2018, but funding over that period of time is up 6%. The number of unicorn companies in the US is at an all-time high.

Investors are still active, they're just focusing on fewer larger bets instead of lots of little ones.
