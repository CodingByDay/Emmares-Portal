The most frequent trade in the U.S.-China trade war is swapping optimism for pessimism. Or pessimism for optimism, depending on your half of the glass. Donald Trump expects to sign "phase one" of a deal soon and then keep moving forward. But Chinese officials have expressed doubt about agreeing to a long-term deal. All of which suggests our trade-war trading may continue for some time. In the midst of all the back-and-forth, here are some stories you might have missed.  — Michael Tighe

 

What Musk Got Right (and Wrong)

Tesla Model 3 owners are more satisfied with their electric ride now than they were a year ago. That's one of the findings from our survey of 5,000 of Elon Musk's customers — the biggest ever conducted. The results come as Tesla readies to open a factory in Shanghai by year's end.

 

Drivers and Disrupters

Getting rich is getting harder to do. Change is sweeping through the global economy and making it harder for low- and middle-income nations to climb the ladders of success. Bloomberg's New Economy Drivers and Disrupters Report unearths the forces buffeting and boosting 114 economies.

 

It All Adds Up

Restricting the amount of drinking water available to staff is just one example of the stringent cost controls imposed by Indonesia's PT Bank Central Asia. It's now the world's most expensive lender, and it just posted record quarterly profit.

 

China's Baby Shortage

The official portrait of a nuclear family in China is starting to change. A shortage of infants is prompting some government agencies to rethink their treatment of unwed mothers and start giving them the same access to benefits that married moms get.

 

Staff Wanted

Hedge funds looking to expand in Asia and capitalize on its expanding capital markets say there's one catch: the talent pool is shallow. That's leading to poaching, the boosting of pay packages and the grooming of young talent.

 

Leap of Faith

The science that makes stars shine is inspiring a $22 billion facility in southern France that's proving immune to politics. Thirty-five nations are building a nuclear fusion reactor that may unlock the secret to generating unlimited energy.

 

The Finer Things

It's a lifestyle rich enough for several episodes of "Lifestyles of the Rich and Famous." Teddy Obiang, whose father runs Equatorial Guinea, flaunts his wealth on Instagram even after authorities in several countries seized a Bugatti, some mansions and enough luxury watches to cover each arm.

 

Trade Spat Fallout

Ripples from the U.S.-China trade war are evident across the street from LeBron James's place of business. A $1 billion-plus project near L.A.'s Staples Center is in limbo as Beijing-based developer Oceanwide Holdings faces a full-court press in both countries.
