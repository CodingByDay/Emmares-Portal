New Skift Research — U.S. Travel Tracker November 2020: Travel's Dark Winter

The U.S. added 4.2 million new coronavirus cases in November, amounting to 32% of all the coronavirus cases in the country by the end of the month. The raging spread of the virus led to a sharp decrease in travel for the month. Despite the Thanksgiving holiday, only 32% of Americans traveled in November, marking the lowest travel incidence rate since May.

With case counts continuing to hit record highs, we believe this is just the beginning of a dark winter for the U.S. travel industry.

Skift Research's newly released U.S. Travel Tracker November 2020  report highlights new trends in travel incidences, consumer sentiments and future travel intents distilled from our November Travel Tracker survey.

What You’ll Learn From This Report:

  • Travel incidences, Jan – Nov 2020
  • November travel highlights
  • COVID-19 impacted travel, Jul – Nov 2020
  • Changing consumer sentiments on the economic outlook, Jul – Dec 2020
  • Changing consumer intent on future travel, Jul – Dec 2020
