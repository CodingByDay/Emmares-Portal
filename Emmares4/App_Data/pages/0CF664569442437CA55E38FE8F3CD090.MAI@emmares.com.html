Switching play

Switching play from one side of the pitch to another

 

When passing options are limited, you're struggling to break the defence or you have a teammate free on the other side of the pitch, a switch in play can often be the best solution.

 

A common call in football is that of "switch!" and for good reason, switching play stands to alleviate pressure on your team and allows your team to progress further up the pitch. Switching play can often be the answer to quickly getting behind robust and hard to beat defences that you just can't seem to get behind. Efficient and creative wide-play is key to the success of switching, as this allows for the element of surprise and the execution of the switch to be at its best. Most importantly however, the timing of switches is something that should be focused on, as it will allow for wide players to make runs in behind or to drift into space and receive the pass, as well as drawing in the opposition to the ball before switching. These drills will help your team to nail their switching play, quickly shifting play from one side of the pitch to the other. This stands to significantly improve their ability to pull off switches when they're most needed in games.

Switching play quickly

The focus of this session incorporates a number of my key principles: pressing, counter pressing and playing in tight areas whilst avoiding pressure. It’s about keeping possession in tight areas and then playing away from pressure, helping us to switch the play quickly. It’s a session I like to use because it involves every player and it puts them in high pressure situations that need them to use quick interplay to keep the ball. It ultimately leads to the creation of solid opportunities to score.

Switching the point of attack

This is all about encouraging players to switch the point of attack at pace. We would run this if we were playing against a team with narrow support, such as a narrow back three and a diamond midfield. The idea behind the session is to get the ball into wide areas of the pitch, enabling teams to cross into the runs of the attackers.

As we like to get the tempo of the session as high as possible straight from the start, we warm up with an intense possession practice.

Switching play to score

This session is all about coaching players how and when to switch play to create goal-scoring opportunities. The different activities have a lot of technical, tactical, physical, social and physiological elements within them. These keep the players focused, while also maintaining the sense of competition, which we think is vital to the success of the session.

Winning is really important so it has to be a factor in our training, but we also need to know that learning is taking place so that the players can achieve the targets set for them.

Switching play & pressing high

This session is focused on encouraging players to switch the play to launch attacks. It also helps players to press high as a team when out of possession.

It’s very important for us to practice this as we defend and attack with a back three and five in midfield. We feel that one of our big strengths as a team is to switch play as quickly as possible in order to create 1v1s, 2v1s and crossing opportunities.

These practices allow us to create realistic scenarios that the players will have to face on a match day.
