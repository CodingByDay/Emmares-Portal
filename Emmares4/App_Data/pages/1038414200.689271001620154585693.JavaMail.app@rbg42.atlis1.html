Dear Reader,

For years, technologists have dreamed of using machines to perfectly simulate the human brain. But neuroscience is not so simple—and computers are not so powerful—as the hype would suggest. "Yet as technology races ahead and a certain strain of technocratic salesmanship continues to command the collective human ear, the line between fiction and reality will continue to be blurred, leading to cycles of hype and disappointment that threaten long-term public confidence in science," writes documentary-maker Noah Hutton in this week's lead article.

Sophie Bushwick, Associate Editor, Technology
