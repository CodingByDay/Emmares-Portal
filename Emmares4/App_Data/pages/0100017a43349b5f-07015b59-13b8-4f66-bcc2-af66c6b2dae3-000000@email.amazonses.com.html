What the FLoC: Google Delays Banning Third-Party Cookies

This is a significant development in the war against cookies and tracking. The entire world of advertising has been scrambling for the last year to deal with the “cookieless” future where programmatic ads can’t target users as well, and publishers have been bombarded with alternative solutions that mostly involve getting people to log in and agree to be tracked (which is silly).

Google decided to come out with FLoC, a new solution for tracking what people are interested in without passing their data to the cloud to be tracked. Unfortunately, that doesn’t solve the privacy issues (and might make them worse), but it does allow for a world without cookies or cloud-based tracking.

The problem is that this system—combined with Google’s first-hand knowledge of what you search for—would give Google a huge advantage over their competition, especially since they are planning to block the cookies that their competition uses. So while the privacy argument is leading to regulatory and legal pressure to get rid of cookies in favor of a worse solution, if they actually do get rid of cookies, they are being anti-competitive.
