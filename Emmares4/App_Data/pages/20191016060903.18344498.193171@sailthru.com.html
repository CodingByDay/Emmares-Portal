Warren's rivals take aim, the worst possible case in Syria, what politicians can't do on social media

THE BIG STORY

Keeping up with the impeachment saga

 

It’s been three weeks since House Democrats launched an impeachment inquiry into President Donald Trump. The inquiry revolves around a call Trump had with Ukraine’s president, where Trump pushed for information on former vice president Joe Biden’s son.


The scale of this story can feel a bit Shakespearean. Trump is only one central player, and there is a whole supporting cast. So Hayes Brown put together the definitive guide to all the figures involved in the impeachment inquiry. It’s exhausting exhaustive, and I highly recommend it.


The impeachment inquiry is going on as we speak, but it doesn’t always generate headline-grabbing news. In large part, that’s because the Democrats are conducting hearings with key witnesses behind closed doors. 


Given all we know about 2019 — a particularly loud year for American politics — why would you want to do such a big thing quietly? I texted political reporter Addy Baird for some clarity.

STAYING ON TOP OF THIS

 

War hawks, peace activists, scholars, and humanitarians agree: Trump made Syria much, much worse

Just over a week ago, Turkey began an operation against Kurdish-led forces the US has previously backed in northeast Syria. The US had said that while it wouldn't support Turkey's assault on the Kurds, it would move its forces out of harm's way.


Since then, the events have shifted alliances in the Middle East quite dramatically. So we asked experts two questions: What's the worst-case scenario you can see in Syria after Trump's decision? And is there anything we can do to stop that scenario?


Almost unanimously, they agreed: We're looking at the worst of all worlds, and there's not much that can be done to halt what's coming.

SNAPSHOTS

 

The fourth Democratic debate took place last night. Elizabeth Warren’s rivals came at her like she’s the frontrunner. Meanwhile, Joe Biden, who is still a frontrunner, was an afterthought. Cory Booker had an impressive debate, though it might not mean much.


Alexandria Ocasio-Cortez will endorse Bernie Sanders for president. The endorsement is perhaps the most significant for Sanders of the 2020 cycle so far, from a figure who has become one of the most significant voices in progressive politics, with major sway particularly among young voters. 


Police found a Dutch family reportedly “waiting for the end of time” in a farmhouse basement. A 25-year-old man turned up at a bar and told locals he needed help saving his siblings. Police found siblings between the ages of 18 and 25 in the farmhouse, along with a 58-year-old man who is not their father. 


Police charged 3 nursing home workers with running a fight club of patients with dementia. Police said the employees at the North Carolina facility were encouraging the residents to physically fight each other, filming the fights and egging the residents on.


Fortnite is back after millions of people watched its map get blown up. The video game sensation had been offline for two days following a black hole event that ended its 10th season on Sunday. But it’s back, so you can breathe easy.

WHO MAKES THESE RULES?

 

The things you can and cannot say on social media

Two stories you should read about the limits of what you’re allowed to do on social media platforms if you’re a politician.


First: Twitter just explained why it hasn’t banned Trump. After Sen. Kamala Harris called for the president's account to be suspended, Twitter waited a few weeks, and then.... said no. The deal is this: Unless Trump explicitly violates Twitter's rules, leaving no room for interpretation, the company will do nothing.


Second: Facebook said politicians can lie in an ad, and that it’s taking down ads from Warren, Biden, and Trump for other reasons. After the company said it’ll allow politicians to lie in their ads, it took down more than 160 political ads — but that’s for breaking the platform’s rules around profanity and fake buttons.

SAFE AND SOUND AND FLOOFY

 

A very big dog named Floyd had to be rescued during a hike and the pictures are heartwarming

The first thing you should know is that Floyd is OK. He’s got injured paws, but he’s being looked after.


See, Floyd is a 190-pound doggo, and he may not be in the best shape of his life. So when the 3-year-old mastiff was on a hike with his human up the Grandeur Peak trail in Salt Lake County, Utah, he got so tired, he couldn’t possibly make it back to the car.


After five concerned passersby saw Floyd and his owner struggling, they called Search and Rescue. The non-profit, all volunteer crew quickly sprang to action to make sure the pupperino got off the mountain before it got too dark and too cold.

Pause and be grateful for your resilience and the ways it protected you,

Elamin
