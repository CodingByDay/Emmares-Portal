...Welcome To The Week In Movies!!!
Australia’s Premier Online Movie Magazine

Welcome to another week without cinemas! In lieu of actual movies, however, we've got reviews of the latest titles to be express released to digital platforms (including Onward, The Invisible Man and Birds Of Prey), as well as some great reading: we've got lists of feel-good movies, isolation movies and 1980s teen movies; Robbie Studsor talks about his new Aussie thriller Burning Kiss; and we preview Alex Gibney's new docu-series How To Fix A Drug Scandal. Stay safe, stay home, and (figuratively if not literally) see you at the movies!

THE LATEST
Films To Put A Smile On Your Dial: Feel Real Good Movies
Hey, are those hippies social distancing? If there's one thing that we all need right now, it's something - anything! - to make us feel good and at least temporarily forget the horrors of what's going on around us. Cue a bunch of FilmInk-sanctioned feel-good movies, including Dazed And Confused, Flying High, Grease and more!!! 

Robbie Studsor: Burning Kiss

Debut Aussie writer/director Robbie Studsor crafts a highly original and visually eye popping noir thriller with the sun drenched Burning Kiss.
Preview: How To Fix A Drug Scandal
Prolific documentary maker Alex Gibney (Going Clear, Citizen K) has another talking point on his hands with Netflix's How To Fix A Drug Scandal.

Dan Scanlon: Directing Onward

Director Dan Scanlon has delivered another winner for hit house Pixar with Onward, which has just been released for digital purchase. 
Like Father: Video Essay
In his video essay Like Father, Noah Janssen deconstructs - through a series of well chosen clips - the notion of masculinity in horror cinema.

Love Bug...Creativity In Isolation

Emerging filmmaker Imogen McCluskey (Suburban Wildlife) has used the soft lockdown as an inspiration for her new web series Love Bug.
Popcorn Therapy Podcast
Filmmakers James Pillion and Kane Senes introduce a podcast devoted to the positive value of cinema and its ability to act as a societal salve.

Movies About Isolation

Feeling all alone while self-isolating and social distancing? Here are some great films about people trying to live life on their lonesome. 
1980s Soulful Male Teens
In a direct answer to current discussions about toxic masculinity, this collection of 1980s films feature almost impossibly soulful teen male leads.
