Blood Orange, Jewel, Colin Stetson and More Set to Appear At Guild of Music Supervisors ‘State of Music in Media’ Conference


 

Dev Hynes (Blood Orange), Jewel, Colin Stetson, DJ Shadow, Pos (De La Soul), Wendy Melvoin and Lisa Coleman are a few of the artists set to make virtual appearances at the 6th Annual 'State of Music in Media' Conference, taking place on Feb. 19 and 20.

The virtual event is free to all members of the Guild of Music Supervisors.

The conference grants participants the opportunity to network with some of the industry’s top leaders, plus attend a variety of informative panels on subjects covering music in film, television, advertising, games, trailers and more. GMS President Joel C. High and Vice President Madonna Wade Reed will open the conference, which announced its lineup today. Beyond the panels, Lainey Wilson and Ashley Nicole Greene will be performing.

Daryn-Reid Goodall ZOOM Memorial and Scholarship


 


A virtual Zoom memorial for Set Decorator Daryn-Reid Goodall Saturday February 20th at noon.

Goodall who died on Jan. 21 will also have a scholarship set up in his name at his high school alma mate for students entering film studies.

As a long-standing member of the Set Decorators Society of America, Goodall was a driving force.

His talent for involving members in activities and committees, as well his energy, passion and love for the craft, made everyone who worked with him strive to be better. He was a great mentor, and many of those he mentored are working in the industry today.

Goodall's credits include "Castle" and "Girl Meets World." More information about his scholarship can be found here.

Awards: Behind the Scenes of 'Pinocchio'

Courtesy of Roadside Attractions

Makeup artist Mark Coulier made the Oscar shortlist in makeup and hairstyling for his work on Matteo Garrone’s retelling of the classic tale "Pinocchio," starring Roberto Benigni as Geppetto and Federico Ielapi as the title character.

Rather than rely on digital application techniques or special effects, Garrone made it clear from the beginning that this film would have a natural look.

That meant Coulier sculpting and using eight silicon pieces, and applying that to Ielapi every day as part of his transformation. Additional silicon pieces were used for his hands and legs.

However, before arriving at the final look, Coulier says, "The sculpture was probably changed every day for about 30 or 40 days."

When it came to creating Pinocchio’s wooden look, Coulier says Garrone showed him a piece of wood he liked. "Matteo loved this piece of wood that he had in his office. We spent a month doing paint trials and painting wood all different kinds of ways until we found the right one."

Lion Forge Animation Expands Creative And Executive Team (EXCLUSIVE)



 

The animation studio behind the Academy Award-winning "Hair Love," Lion Forge Animation, is expanding its global creative, executive and production teams.

The studio announced key positions, including the hiring of two-time Emmy-winning animation director and creative visionary Saxton Moore. Moore will serve as executive creative director.

Ariel Yamus will bring his passion and 10-plus years of experience working in design, illustration and animation to the company in the role of senior creative director. Yamus will focus on exploring new methods of creativity for all areas, from storytelling to character design to audio production.

Internationally, Patricio Yamus will head up Lion Forge Animation Argentina, where he will serve as studio head and executive producer.

Yamus was the founder and CEO of BLU Animation Studio, where he will lead a diverse, global team to provide animation production services to international clients in advertising, private equity, brands, government, live-action production and animation production worldwide.

Other hires include Jimmy Thomas, Lina Foti, Ray Brown and Deena Boykin. They will be providing creative direction and support to the team.

“I am incredibly excited to welcome our new and talented team members into the fold. Each person brings something new and exciting to the table, and I can’t wait to integrate them into our projects to see how we can continue to tell authentic and unique stories,” said Lion Forge Animation President Carl Reed.

David Steward II added, “Since the launch of Lion Forge Animation, it’s been our goal to put together the best talent working in the industry to tell these unrepresented stories in a meaningful way. This group enhances our ability to do just that -- and we can’t wait to see the amazing work they do.”
