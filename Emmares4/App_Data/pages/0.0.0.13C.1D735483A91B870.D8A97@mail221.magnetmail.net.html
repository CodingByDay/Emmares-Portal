A federal vaccine advisory committee will review the Johnson & Johnson (JNJ) COVID-19 vaccine this Friday, with a good chance that the company will get the OK to resume inoculations. Dr. Anthony Fauci, President Biden’s chief medical officer, said he thinks the committee will approve resuming use of the vaccine, either with certain restrictions or just with warnings about the potential risk of blood clots in some recipients. When clotting concerns first arose, U.S. health authorities had considered just issuing a warning, but decided to ask for a pause in vaccinations in order to make sure physicians know how to treat the clotting condition correctly. Dr. Walensky of the Centers for Disease Control and Prevention said as much at a White House briefing last week. For example, doctors should not give the drug heparin to patients with this reaction, even though heparin is commonly used to treat clots.

Meanwhile, some good news: The seven-day average of new COVID-19 infections is declining again in the U.S., easing fears of an upsurge caused by new viral variants. Cases were either flat or declining in almost all the previous hotspots in the Northeast and Midwest, though they are still very high in Michigan. However, the uptrend has not broken yet in Maine, Oregon and Washington.

What to expect from tomorrow’s Apple event. Apple (AAPL) product-reveal events have become a staple of stock-analyst coverage over the years, and the iPhone maker’s latest lineup of launches is expected to be announced at an April 20 event dubbed “Spring Loaded.” What’s in store? BofA Global Research is honing in on iPads, given difficult year-over-year sales comparisons in the second half of Apple’s fiscal year: “Tuesday's event could feature a new 12.9-inch iPad Pro with mini-LED display, and a new 11-inch iPad Pro. Mini-LED displays offer better contrast, local dimming and wide color gamut. Both iPads should have the faster A14x chip, thunderbolt, and 5G support. Apple could also launch an iPad Mini with an 8.4-inch display, and a new 10.5-inch low cost iPad.” As is the case with most Apple events, we’re likely to see a few other updates or new products, too. BofA speculates that we could see new a new line of AirPods; an Apple TV upgrade; augmented reality features; and Mac-related upgrades, among other Apple news.
