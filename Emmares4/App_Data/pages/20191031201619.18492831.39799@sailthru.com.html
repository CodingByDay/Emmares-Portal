House OKs Trump impeachment terms

The House on Thursday voted almost entirely along party lines (232-196) to formalize and make public Democrats' impeachment inquiry into President Trump. The resolution provides a road map that lets House Intelligence chief Adam Schiff call for public hearings with witnesses and allows Republicans to request subpoenas. It also gives the White House the right to respond to evidence, which could be rescinded if Trump continues to block Democratic subpoenas. He responded to the Halloween vote, tweeting, “The greatest Witch Hunt in American History!”

More: 

  • Two Democrats representing GOP-leaning districts opposed the resolution: Rep. Jeff Van Drew of New Jersey and Rep. Collin Peterson of Minnesota.
  • The resolution sends a clear signal that an impeachment vote and a Senate trial are all but inevitable, meaning Trump could become the first president to seek re-election post-impeachment.

This is not normal

Fire to ice. Northern California’s Kincade fire has become the largest blaze in Sonoma County history after it incinerated nearly 77,000 acres. It’s now 60% contained, but as the winds let up, a new Bay Area threat has emerged: a freeze warning.

The future is now

"Immune amnesia." Measles is more harmful than we realized, scientists say, after research showed for the first time how the virus causes the immune system to "forget what it once knew," raising the risk of other infections. The discovery stresses the need for widespread vaccination, researchers said.
