Unless you live in Kansas City or San Francisco (or perhaps you enjoy copyright trolling overambitious corporate entities), this weekend's big sporting event may feel kind of meh. No one should sneeze at a good excuse to order wings, of course, but the game itself can only generate so excitement.

 

Luckily, as February rolls in there are plenty of other things to look forward to in the Ars world. If you care about things to watch in particular, for instance, shouting "engage!" just came back into vogue with the (good!) return of Jean-Luc Picard. If comedy is more your speed, Apple TV+'s perhaps most high profile offering for early 2020 arrives when the team from It's Always Sunny debuts Mythic Quest, a seemingly irreverent look at game development life. And if neither of those really rises above the level of a football game and some ads, there's always the last season of perhaps the best modern trip to a galaxy far, far away (srsly, have you seen The Clone Wars cartoon?).

 

So for this week's Orbital Transmission, we're tracking the other stuff to get excited for in February 2020. Whether you're focused on games or gear, 2020 seems to start picking up steam now. And luckily, your favorite five-layer dip will accompany the Oscars' ceremony just as well as it did the NFL—prepping for the former simply involves more watching Brad Pitt cruise around Los Angeles.

Cram in some movies, prepare to win your Oscar pools

If sports aren't really your thing, the most reliable bit of annual gambling might still happen each spring—it's just nudged in-between the Super Bowl and March Madness. This year's Oscars ceremony takes place on Sunday, February 9, and there are plenty of films to get excited about (and to get familiar with if you're filling out an Oscars ballot for friendly competition among friends). Most of interest to the Ars office Slack—a few of our favorite films of 2019 are up against each other in the Best Picture race. Features Editor Nathan Mattise called Bong Joon-Ho's Parasite, "genuinely a Valvano movie experience—you'll think, you'll laugh, and you might even cry (the film's coda induced some audible sniffles at the screening)." In the other corner, Culture Editor Sam Machkovech called Sam Mendes' 1917, "an unmissable document of the human condition amidst the realities of war." Two (err, nine?) films enter, but only one can win.

 

Please someone send the new LEGO ISS set for Valentine's Day

February typically marks the New York Toy Fair, which has in recent years brought us brick-heads a number of drool-worthy LEGO sets to chase. But this year, everyone's favorite builders gave fellow nerds everywhere an early present by announcing an official ISS-inspired LEGO set to celebrate the 10th anniversary of the company's Ideas program. This thing can't be pre-ordered fast enough: The set contains 850-plus pieces and when built stands over 7-inches (20cm) high, 12-inches (31cm) long, and 19-inches (49cm) wide. According to the official company press release, loving details include a posable Canadarm2 and two rotating joints that coincide with eight adjustable solar panels. It goes on sale February 1 for $69.99.

 

At last, The Last of Us Pt. II arrives this month

It's a highly anticipated gaming sequel almost four years in the making. At a late 2016 Playstation Experience event, Sony first revealed The Last of Us: Part 2 would exist and that the game would be set five years after the events of The Last of Us. This time it'd star an angry 19-year-old Ellie along Joel, the lead from the first game. Little plot details would leak out or be breadcrumbed from Sony periodically since, but last fall we got confirmation February 21, 2020 was the big day for the big game. If you are also so excited you don't know what to do with yourself in the interim, we'd recommend watching someone sneak StarFox Arwings into Ocarina of Time as a good way to bide your time.

 

Nostalgia is a helluva drug, smartphone edition

It's been kind of a disappointing year thus far for hopes of The Next Great Thing™ in smartphones. The Galaxy Fold? Woof. The Librem 5 and the promise of a genuine Linux phone? If you squint hard enough, maybe. Instead, the most interesting near-future for non-Apple phones may come from the past. HMD, basically the only company selling viable low-end devices in the US, may have another winner on its hands with the Nokia 2.3. It's not a classic brick phone, but it is a respectable seeming device for just $129. And next week, perhaps the granddaddy of all nostalgic phones re-arrives on the scene with the new Moto Razr. It's foldable... and even contains a skin to convert your touch screen into that classic backlit Moto aesthetic. How soon 'til someone thinks a new BlackBerry is a good idea?
