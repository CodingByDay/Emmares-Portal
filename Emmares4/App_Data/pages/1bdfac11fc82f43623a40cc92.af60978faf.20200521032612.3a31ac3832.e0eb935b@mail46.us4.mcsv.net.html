It's okay to squeeze onto public transport and play rugby league, but cinemas are still closed? Okaaaaay...in the meantime, we've got reviews of this week's digital releases (Tammy's Always Dying, Bloodline, Benjamin, Seberg, The Peanut Butter Falcon and The Trip To Greece), plus some good screen reading: we head to the dark side with Seann William Scott for his new horror thriller Bloodline; check out the new TV offerings Little Fires Everywhere and Upload; saddle up for a ride through the world of "roast beef" westerns; ruminate on the polarising Woody Allen; check in with Al Cossar about what's happening with The Melbourne Film Fest; chat with Aussie producer/director David Parker about his amazing career; and salute the tragically departed Lynn Shelton. Stay safe, stay sane, and see you at the movies...sometime soon!  
THE LATEST
Seann William Scott Gets Nasty In Bloodline
Most famous for playing adorable knuckleheads in films like American Pie, Goon and Dude, Where's My Car?, Seann William Scott executes a massive left turn in the stylish chiller Bloodline, playing a ruthless killer with his own deranged moral code. 

Preview: Little Fires Everywhere

Producer/actress Reese Witherspoon (Big Little Lies) headlines another much anticipated literary adaptation with Little Fires Everywhere. 
Kacie Anning: Upload
Aussie Kacie Anning wrote, directed and starred in the impressive web series Fragments Of Friday, and has now helmed eps of the new US series Upload. 

An Ode To "Roast Beef" Westerns

What's a roast beef western? It's a western made in or funded by Britain, that's what, and in this feature we look at some of the most intersting examples.
Opinion: On Woody Allen
Now one of the most polarising and controversial figures in Hollywood, it's increasingly difficult to examine the work of director Woody Allen. 

A Tribute To Lynn Shelton

Sadly lost to the world at the age of just 54, we pay tribute to gifted writer/director Lynn Shelton by tracking back through her life and career. 
The Melbourne Film Fest Online
Al Cossar, the artistic director of The Melbourne International Film Festival, discusses the challenges of putting on a film festival in 2020. 

David Parker: His Brilliant Career

Upon the release of his new, shot-under-COVID-19 short, Isolation Restaurant (co-created with Nadia Tass), we chat to David Parker about his career.
7 Days Of Disney + For Adults
With new-kid-on-the-streaming-block Disney+ now free for seven days, we trawl through the service's shelves to see what's on offer for the grown-ups.
