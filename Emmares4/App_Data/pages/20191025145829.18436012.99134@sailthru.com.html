This week was a big one for Android phones. We reviewed two new flagship devices from two smartphone giants; the new Pixel 4 from Google and the Galaxy Fold from Samsung. The former is one of the most anticipated smartphone updates of the year, and Google's shining example of everything Android can be. The latter is something else entirely.

You might remember that Samsung introduced the Galaxy Fold in April of this year, showing off the innovative device that opens like a book to expose a second, larger screen inside. You might also remember that Samsung sent the device to tech journalists a little early, and many of those writers discovered problems: broken screens, parts separating from one another, and phones that just went dark and refused to restart. Now Samsung has fixed those problems, and we've gotten a chance to spend time with the redone Fold. The bottom line: It's neat for sure, but you would probably tire of the Fold quickly if you were forced to use it as your only device.

Also this week, we check in with Neil Young on his quest for better hi-resolution audio, blind people are fighting for a more accessible internet, and we watch a lot of television to tell you which Roku is best.
