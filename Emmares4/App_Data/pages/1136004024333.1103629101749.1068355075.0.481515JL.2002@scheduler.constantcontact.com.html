Big Apple Film Festival, a proud partner of MovieMaker Magazine, presents a
2-day virtual summit with top agents and managers for writers and filmmakers currently seeking representation. The event takes place APRIL 21-22.
 
The purpose of this informational summit is to provide insight and strategies for acquiring representation. Questions will be taken in advance and pre-selected, and
loglines/synopses of all participant's projects will be requested in
advance and forwarded to our speakers.
    
15% discount on All-Access Passes available until APRIL 20th with code: MovieMakerPartnerEvent2021
    
PARTICIPATING AGENTS AND MANAGERS:
    
ADAM HARRIS (Agent, William Morris Agency)
    
Adam Harris is an agent in WME's TV department based out of Los Angeles, where he has worked since 2013.  He represents talent, production companies and filmmakers, primarily by packaging and selling TV shows and documentaries. Adam specializes in working with strong diverse voices both in front of and behind the camera, and is passionate about telling stories that can lead to change and amplify messages from different
perspectives.
    
ROY ASHTON (Partner, The Gersh Agency)
 
Roy Ashton is the Head of the TV Literary & Packaging Dept. at The Gersh Agency, with offices in Los Angeles and New York. Prior to joining Gersh as Partner, Ashton spent 13 years at Creative Artists Agency. He functions in all areas of television, helping clients sell and package television projects at the major networks, major cable, and premium cable outlets.
    
Clients include showrunners, writers, production companies, directors,
distributors, authors, and actors from all over the globe.
    
RIMA GREER (President, Above the Line Agency)
    
Rima Greer is president of Above the Line Agency. Based in Beverly Hills, Above the Line Agency represents writers, directors and actors. She began her career at the Writers Guild of America, William Morris Agency and Writers & Artists Agency. Rima has worked as a Hollywood agent for over 35 years. Ms. Greer has also been involved in over 70 produced films and episodes, including studio projects like JUMANJI, CHARLIE'S ANGELS, BACKDRAFT and CONSTANTINE.
    
KATHY MURAVIOV (President, The Muraviov Company)
    
Kathy Muraviov is president of The Muraviov Company. Prior to launching her management company, Kathy Muraviov spent over 20 years as Script Services manager at NBC Universal Studios.  In January, 2004, Kathy left Universal to start her own business of managing screenwriters. Her roster of screenwriters currently have a handful of options in place (she had 6 projects optioned in March alone), and maintains an extraordinary roster of clients from all over the U.S., Canada, the UK, and Australia.
    
RJ MILLARD (President, Obscured Pictures)
    
R.J. Millard is a sales agent, and president of Obscured Pictures, as well as the former VP of Publicity at Focus Features, SVP Marketing and Publicity at Samuel Goldwyn Films/Roadside Attractions/IDP Distribution and Press Officer, New Media Development at the Sundance Film Festival. With over 20 years of experience in the industry, his work includes working with filmmakers and producers on securing distribution deals, representing filmmakers at domestic and international film markets, and film festival strategies. RJ's clients have included Bleecker Street Films, Amazon Studios, Focus Features, IMAX, Roadside Attractions, A24, IFC Films, Broad Green Pictures and more. RJ has run awards campaigns for multiple Academy Award nominated films, as well as numerous Golden Globe and Film Independent Spirit Award nominated films.
    
PETER BRODERICK (President, Paradigm Consulting)
    
Peter Broderick is one of the world's leading Distribution Strategists. He spearheads the distribution of exceptional documentary films and has consulted on over 1400 films around the world. As President of Paradigm Consulting, he helps filmmakers and media companies maximize distribution, audiences, and revenues. Peter was President of Next Wave Films, which supplied finishing funds and other support to filmmakers from the US and abroad, helping to launch the careers of outstanding directors including Christopher Nolan and Amir Bar-Lev. Now focused on the revolution in film distribution, Peter gives keynotes and presentations internationally including at Cannes, Sundance, Toronto and IDFA.
    
Peter's producing credits include: "Following", directed by Academy Award nominee Christopher Nolan, "Blood, Guts, Bullets and Octane" directed by Film Independent Spirit Award nominee Joe Carnahan, "Manic" starring Golden Globe nominees Joseph Gordon-Levitt and Zooey Deschanel, "Killing for Love" distributed by Sundance Selects, "Prodigal Sons", released theatrically by First Run Features, and more
    
LILLIAN LASALLE (President, Sweet 180 Management)
    
Lillian LaSalle is president of New York based Sweet 180 Management, representing directors, showrunners, producers, and screenwriters, playwrights and actors. In addition to managing talent, she is also an accomplished producer. Lillian is the recipient of the 2016 Peabody "Futures of Media" Award for Producing. She has produced feature films
including Loggerheads, which premiered in competition at The Sundance Film Festival and Sweet Land, which won the Independent Spirit Award for Best First Feature for writer/director Ali Selim.
    
SARA ALEXANDER (President, Alexander Creatives)
    
Sara Alexander is the owner of Alexander Creatives, a New York based management company representing artists for film, television, commercials, branded entertainment and new media. Alexander Creatives represents Directors, Producers, Writers, as well as DPs, Production Designers, Costume Designers, Editors and 1st ADs.
    
Literary content representation includes screenplays, books to film, and television series.
    
MICHAEL KLEIN (Manager/Producer, Magnolia Entertainment)
 
Michael Klein is a literary manager and producer who recently joined Magnolia Entertainment. Before joining, he operated his own management/production company for over a decade. He manages filmmakers from across the globe who work in both film and television. Clients have written and directed for Netflix, Amazon, Hulu, Disney, Apple, Blumhouse, Sony, Fox, Warner Brothers, Paramount, Universal, Lionsgate, to name a few. He's excited to be working with Magnolia Entertainment, who represent an esteemed roster of talent, including Rosamund Pike, Rachel McAdams, Dev Patel, and Daniel Espinosa.
 
LEE STOBBY (Founder, Stobby Entertainment)
    
Lee Stobby is a literary manager, producer and principal of Lee Stobby Entertainment who has over 10 years of management and producing experience.
    
His clients have written for numerous feature film projects and series, including JOHN WICK: CHAPTER 3 and currently JOHN WICK 4, Netflix's STRANGER THINGS and ARMY OF THE DEAD, the CW's LEGACIES, as well as clients who have sold scripts to Lionsgate, CW and more.
