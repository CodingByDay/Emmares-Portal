Jobs to be done

 

If you have ever uttered the phrases “disruptive innovation” or “jobs to be done,” your thinking was influenced by Professor Clayton Christensen.  

 

He significantly advanced the world’s thinking on management, strategy, competition, and technology. He’s greatly influenced us at CB Insights and many of our clients.

 

One of his works is an essay called “How will you measure your life?” in which he takes many of his management concepts and applies them to evaluating how one lives.  

 

Like most of what he did, it is clear, cogent, and insightful. Here's a passage — the full essay is linked in The Blurb.

'I am not in the entertainment business'

 

I remember growing up with my dad watching Jim Lehrer on PBS NewsHour. Most of the time, I wanted him to change the channel to something more interesting — Wheel of Fortune or Double Dare or sports or whatever.

 

But over time, I got to appreciate Lehrer for just being a pro.

 

Many of you may not be as familiar with him, but his 16 rules of journalism are a great set of reminders about what journalism should be. The list is linked in The Blurb. I particularly liked 5, 10, 13, and 16. I’m curious which ones you like.
Source: Rolling Stone
