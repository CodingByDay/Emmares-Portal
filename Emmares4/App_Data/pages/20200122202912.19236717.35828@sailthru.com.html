Wuhan quarantined for coronavirus

The Chinese city of Wuhan is on lockdown following the outbreak of the new potentially deadly respiratory virus that's swept the country and spread to five others. Government officials on Wednesday halted outbound trains and flights from the city of 11 million and ordered the temporary shutdown of public transit. The virus, which has infected more than 500 people and killed 17 in China, couldn't have broken out at a worse time: This week marks the peak of the world's biggest human migration as hundreds of millions of Chinese travel for the Lunar New Year.

More:

  • No public emergency—yet. The WHO held off declaring the outbreak a global health emergency, saying officials were evenly split and needed more information. They'll reconvene on Thursday. 
  • Snake-to-human transmission. Researchers said the virus most likely came from snakes in a paper in the Journal of Medical Virology. The illness is believed to have originated at a food market in Wuhan.
  • Vaccine in store? Given its similarity to SARS and MERS, NIH officials said researchers may be able to fast-track a potential coronavirus vaccine that could begin human testing by April.
