Dear Reader,

This week, we've drummed up some research from the quantum frontier. Our lead story details work from two independent teams who each managed to bestow quantum entanglement upon pairs of tiny, drum-like aluminum membranes. Creating quantum entanglement even between two atoms is no mean feat, but in these studies each entangled drum contained on the order of a trillion atoms. The achievement is unlikely to lead to better smartphones or other consumer products any time soon, but could ultimately benefit future quantum computers and networks. Elsewhere, we have stories about the legacy of late NASA astronaut Sally Ride, the successful launch and landing of SpaceX's prototype Starship rocket, the origins of relativistic stars, and much more. Enjoy!
